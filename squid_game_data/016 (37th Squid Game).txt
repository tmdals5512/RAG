History[]
Player 016 was presumably in massive debt and decided to join the Squid Games for a chance to win a huge cash prize. He called the number on the recruiting card, and got into a mysterious car. He then woke up in a giant room with 454 other players. He then made his way to the center of the room and watched as many other players debts were revealed in front of him. Later on, he signed a contract and made his way up a flight of stairs to the first game. 
During the first game, he heard Seong Gi-hun (Player 456) screaming about how dangerous the games were. Player 016 and many other players ignored his warnings and continued the game without any worry. However, he was soon terrified as players were shot in front of him during the mass panic. Player 016 luckily was able to control himself and stand still. He eventually completed the first game after listening to Gi-hun's commands. After returning to the dormitory with the other surviving players, Player 016 begged the guards to let him and the other players go. However, The guards instead showed everyone the massive increase in prize money that accumulated after the first game. However, after a massive increase in the prize pool was revealed, Player 016 changed his mind and decided to vote O during the first vote. Him and the other Os then cheered after they won the vote. 
Later, during Six-Legged Pentathlon, Player 016 formed a small team with Player 178 and Player 189. Later on, Park Yong-sik (Player 007) and his mother asked if they could join their team. Player 016 mocked them and told them to look for other players to team up with. Park Yong-sik then walked off with his mother to find a different team. During this, Player 016 equipped Jang Do-yeong and Player 416 onto the team, forming Team 1. When Six-Legged Pentathlon officially started. They were one of the 2 teams up first. They started off strong with Player 016 clearing Ddakji on his first try. However, they later got stuck on Biseokchigi after Jang Do-yeong couldn't hit the stone. Player 016 and his fellow teammates were angered by this and started to yell and scream at Jang Do-yeong.

Fate[]
After Jang Do-yeong finally manages to complete Biseokchigi, Player 016 knew that it was far to late to advance further into the game. Player 016 and his team were then gunned down for failing to complete the 5 minigames within the time limit.

Trivia[]
He seems to be very skilled at Ddakji, since he made it first try in Pentathlon and on the monitor, we can see him beating the recruiter instead of failing.
His counterpart from Squid Game: The Challenge Season 2, Sydney Belle (Player 016) was also part of the first team to be eliminated in the Six-Legged Pentathlon.
Mistakes[]
Although he died in the previous episode, during the Six-Legged Pentathlon montage in One More Game, the editor reused the clip of Jang Do-yeong finally beating Biseokchigi, where Player 016's number was visible despite the number being slightly blurred.
