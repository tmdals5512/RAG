Byeong-gi (병기 Byeong-gi?), also known as Player 111 or The Doctor, was a participant in the 33rd Squid Game. Prior to entering the competition, he was a doctor who ended up in debt after killing one of his patients through medical malpractice.
Byeong-gi was involved in a conspiracy alongside several corrupt guards which involved him trafficking organs of eliminated players, which the corrupt guards would then sell on the black market in exchange for extra food and information about upcoming games. Due to this breaking the game's rules, Byeong-gi and the other guards were executed on Hwang In-ho's orders.

Personality
Byeong-gi was a doctor before the games. Inside of the games, he took advantage of his abilities to the fullest to survive, allying with the masked men in exchange for information about the upcoming games and using that information to ally with Jang Deok-su's gang, the strongest team. His survival methods are extremely questionable and are rooted into his cowardice and desperation, making him do anything to stay alive.
Despite being reserved and composed most of the time, he is shown to be quick to anger when people talk around him while he works. When the masked men refuse to give him the information he needs, he becomes deeply frustrated and violent. He holds a soldier hostage for information and later kills him, giving up of his deal with the masked men. 

History
Byeong-gi was a doctor who engaged in immoral practices and got into trouble after an incident that resulted in the death of at least one of his patients.[1] This caused him to enter the 33rd Squid Game to pay off his debts.

2020
During the games, he was recruited by a group of corrupt guards, including Worker 028, to perform surgeries on the corpses of the deceased contestants and remove their organs, which the Guards then sold to traffickers. In exchange, the guards provided Byeong-gi with extra food and information about the upcoming games which would increase his chances of survival. This information was written to him on pink slips of paper hidden in his food, which he later ate, meaning Byeong-gi was breaking the Squid Game’s biggest rule.      Byeong-gi's body hung up alongside some guards in the stairs who rebelled against the leadership as a warning for players of cheating  
Just prior to the Tug of War game in the third round, the guards warn Byeong-gi that the game organizers plan to incite a fight among the contestants so that some of the players will be killed. They advise him to ally with Jang Deok-su and his gang for protection. Byeong-gi endears himself to Deok-su by warning him that the next game is Tug of War, which allows Deok-su and Byeong-gi to form a physically strong team and survive the round.[2] Byeong-gi also helped stitch Deok-su's injury after the fight.

Fate
Byeong-gi continues performing the surgeries for the corrupt guards, but the stress of the games and a lack of sleep make him increasingly irritable and paranoid. When told they do not know what the next game will be, a furious Byeong-gi lashes out against the guards and kills one before attempting to flee from them. He ends up in the Sugar Honeycombs game hall. He and one of the pursuing guards are then discovered by the Front Man who says that he does not care at all about the corrupt guards, selling the organs of the deceased players on the black market, but does in no way tolerate the breaking of the biggest rule of the game. The Front Man and his men, then shoots Byeong-gi to death.[1] His corpse was then hung over the stairs along with the traitorous guards as a warning to other players to play fairly.

Legacy
He was indirectly mentioned by the Masked Officer, when he had a conversation with Kang No-eul in "Six Legs."
In Keys and Knives, he was indirectly mentioned by Number 005. Byeong-gi indirectly created a stronger alliance between the season two/three harvesters.

Trivia
Apparently, organ traffickers had been working with him before the game started and assigned him the number 111 because it was easy to remember and to make him stand out from the other players.
Byeong-gi is the only supporting character who died prior to Marbles. He died after violating the rules by cheating.
His death created an uneven number of players, which allowed for Han Mi-nyeo to skip the next game.
Byeong-gi's name is never said throughout the series. His name is only revealed in the credits for episodes 3-5, making him one of only six players to have a name revealed this way, as well as the only one from the first season. This might be because he never formally introduced himself to Deok-su's group, given that Deok-su and Mi-nyeo only learned each other's names after their intimacy in the bathroom.
Byeong-gi was the first player to pass Sugar Honeycombs, having known what the game was beforehand and having chosen the easiest shape, triangle.
Byeong-gi was indirectly mentioned in season two where Guard 011 and the Masked Officer were talking.
Byeong-gi is one of three known players to see the Front Man in disguise. The others being Park Jung-bae and Seong Gi-hun. Although Gi-hun is the only known player to discover the Front Man's true identity.
Byeong-gi is the only major player from season 1 who has an erroneous icon.
Despite his name being revealed through the credits, he was not given a family name.
In the episode "A Fair World", when the guards were beginning to distrust Jun-ho, Byeong-gi indirectly saved him, just before he pulled out his gun to start a fight that would likely have ended with Jun-ho dead or the others finding out about the Organ Trafficking Ring. Byeong-gi started shouting, diverting attention to himself and saving Jun-ho.
He is the only character in season one to have his name revealed in the credits.
Before getting shot, Byeong-gi was standing in the exact same spot he sat at while cutting his dalgona.
Mistakes
Another player was shown on Byeong-gi's icon, which can be also seen on Player 259's and Player 307's icons.
Behind the scenes
Byeong-gi was portrayed by actor Yoo Sung-joo. He was voiced by Brian Kim McCormick in the English dub.
