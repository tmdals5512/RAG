Lee Seung-won (이승원 I Seung-won?), also known as Player 336, was a participant in the 37th Squid Game as well as one of the finalists of the competition. He was a member of an alliance that included Kim Yun-tae (Player 353), Player 039 and Player 096.

Personality
Hey! If you wanna leave so bad, why stuff your faces with gimbap, huh? It'll be quicker if you starve!
Seung-won mocking at Team X in "O X"
Seung-won, like most Team O Players, is a greedy and aggressive man who is willing to kill just for financial gain. During Team O and Team X argument, he was rude to Team X Players by telling them to starve. During the Special Game, he attempted to kill Player 359 showcasing his sadistic nature, after the failed Rebellion, he mocks Seong Gi-hun (Player 456) by saying how the Guards killed every person who participated and spared him. During Hide and Seek, he violently stabs Player 394, a fellow O to death, showing a lack of care for his supposed allies. After Jump Rope, he told Kim Yun-tae (Player 353) that he will get out with 5.6 billion, but when the guards announced that there are 9 players, he was enraged at the guards for adding the baby which lower the prize money. After the discussion, he was willing to kill Jun-hee's baby (Player 222) just for the money showing no compassion, later that night he voted O in the final vote and agrees with the others that they will kill the baby the next day in Sky Squid Game. In the Sky Squid Game game he acts as a sort of middle ground between the intelligent, cerebral and more catious Player 353/Kim Yun-tae and the violent, impulsive, sadistic and unhinged Player 203/Kim Gi-min.

History
Lee Seung-won got into debt through unknown means before being in the Squid Games. While unknown, it's implied the reason was because of gambling or other shady methods. His family a wife and two children was waiting for him at home. After Red Light, Green Light, he voted O to keep playing. For Six-Legged Pentathlon, he teamed with Kim Gi-min (Player 203), Player 206, Player 145 and Player 238. They passed the game and he voted O for a second time, doing the same after Mingle.   

After Mingle, Team X and Team O were divided into 2 different factions and the tension among the 2 groups started rising. When the 2 teams started arguing, he yelled at Team X players, telling them that if they wanted to leave so bad, they could just starve.I bet you're right. 'Cause after all, those assholes killed everyone but decided to spare him. There's a reason for that, isn't there?
Seung-won telling his team while mocking Gi-hun for his failed rebellion in "Keys and Knives"
In the Special Game, he's seen beating up Player 359 with a bed frame multiple times, but fortunately Player 359 survived. After the fight, he is seen with hands in the air when Kwon Byeong-su (Player 047) tries to shoot some O Players. After the re-vote, he and his team laughed at Gi-hun. It was unknown that Seung-won saw Gi-hun strangle Seon-nyeo (Player 044) who mocked him for the death of his friends, shocking the rest of Team O.

Yeah, it doesn't make any sense at all. I don't suppose any of them will just stand there and ask us to do it. You guys really expect us to do it with our bare hands?
Seung-won complains that he was not given any weapons in "Keys and Knives"
In Hide and Seek he joined the Red Team. Before the game started, Seung-won, along with Kim Yeong-sam (Player 226) and Player 096 argue that the game wasn't fair for their team, before the daggers were handed out to them. Before the game, he and Gi-min practiced with their Knives. During the game, he brutally killed his former ally, Player 394, thereby passing the game. In the next vote he voted O for the 5th time.  
In Jump Rope, he is one of the few people who were not nervous of entering the game. When Gi-hun managed to carry a baby to the finish line in a short amount of time, He along with Im Jeong-dae (Player 100) and other players cheered for Gi-hun: Seung-won is seen pointing his finger gleefully and congratulatorily. Seung-won enters the Jump Rope after Player 312, Player 276, Jeong-dae, Player 084 and Player 209 enter respectively making him the ninth player to go on the bridge. Once Player 096 pushes Players 312 and 276 off the platform, Seung-won can be seen fearing for his life behind Player 084. Gi-hun then confronts Player 096, allowing Jeong-dae to pass. Afterwards, Seung-won makes it through collapsing onto the ground. Seung-won survives Jump Rope and makes it to the 6th game. 

Yeah, man! We risked our lives for that money, fair and square! And that kid shows up here, and now it's a player too? You want us to share our money with that thing?! Are you batshit crazy?!
Seung-won complains to the Manager about Kim Jun-hee's baby as a Player in "222"
When 8 Players returned to the Dormitory, the Manager announced there were actually 9 Players. The group were confused, insisting there were only eight, until Kim Jun-hee's daughter was counted as Player 222; taking her mother's place as Player 222 and bringing the total to 9. This caused Seung-won to be enraged, as he stated that the 8 of them had to go through deadly games to advance, while a baby could just join in. The loss of 640 million won due to the baby's inclusion led everyone to attempt to kill the girl, though their efforts ultimately failed. 

So uhhh... we just gotta pick who goes? We eliminate three of us, so the rest can win.
Seung-won asking the manager during the Final Dinner/37th Squid Game in "222"
In Sky Squid Game, Seung-won along with the other O Players, all teamed up against Park Min-su (Player 125), with ultimately Seung-won standing by and watching as Lee Myung-gi (Player 333) saves the group by pushing Min-su from the square tower to his death with an metal pole before the timer ran out. On the triangle platform, conflict broke out between Yun-tae and Gi-min as the group argued over whether they should try to separate Gi-hun from the baby; with great difficulty or eliminate them both together effortlessly, but risk having to sacrifice 1 of their own. Myung-gi then proposed a plan that required a player to pull Gi-hun and the baby to the center to separate them, where then Gi-hun could be pushed off and killed. All the other O Finalists would act as a safety measure to keep the front player safe. After a few rounds of Rock Paper Scissors, Seung-won lost the final round to Jeong-dae and was designated with the task to pull Gi-hun. Look, I know how you feel. No one wants that little kid to die in here. I'm a parent, I got two of my own.
Seung-won to Gi-hun in "⭘△☐"
Fate
Please, don't kill me. They... They forced me to do it. They said would kill me first if I don't push you. I'm begging you, sir. Please, I have a wife and kids back home. I was just trying to survive, that's all. Really, I'm sorry. Sir, please. Listen, please don't do it.
Lee Seung-won begging Mr. Seong Gi-hun to spare him  in "⭘△☐"
Seung-won tied his jacket around his waist and the other O finalists tied their jackets to his to make a harness. Seung-won told his allies not to let go of him and they told him they wouldn't. Myung-gi told Seung-won that if he couldn't take the baby, then what he should do was grab her along with Gi-hun and they would separate them in the center of the tower. When they had the baby, Myung-gi would push Gi-hun.
Seung-won attempted to distract Gi-hun with the promise to let him draw lots to eliminate a random 1 of themselves. Afterwards, Seung-won attempted to attack Gi-hun, but he was overpowered by Gi-hun; who used Oh Il-nam's dagger. Gi-hun grabbed the dagger, which he had received from Hwang In-ho (The Front Man) the night before, cut the harness rope and threatened Seung-won with the dagger to make him move away from the baby; making him walk toward the edge of the tower. Seung-won, terrified, begged Mr. Seong for mercy, telling him that the other O Finalists either forced him to do it, that he had a wife and kids on his home and that he was just trying to survive in that infernal place.
Seung-won saw Myung-gi, armed with the metal pole from the tower, approaching Gi-hun from behind and he begged Gi-hun not to kill him. The other O Finalists yelled at Myung-gi to push Gi-hun, Myung-gi shouted "Get out of the way!", ran towards Seung-won and Gi-hun while they both stepped aside... and Myung-gi pushed Seung-won off the tower; making him fall about 30 meters to his death. He placed 8th in the competition. After killing Seung-won, Myung-gi revealed that he was the girl's father and that was why he pushed Seung-won instead of Gi-hun.

Trivia
During Season 2, Seung-won only has one vocal line, being the one during the argument between the Os and Xs.
He was the first player to die on the second platform in Sky Squid Game, putting him in 8th place overall.
Many fans expected him to have a more prominent role in Season 3, considering he was one of the few actors who had a named character in the  credits while having a background role, the other ones being Kim Gi-min and Kim Yun-tae, which turned out to be true, as he became a bigger antagonist, and also became a Finalist.
Him and Kim Yun-tae are the only finalists in the 37th Squid Game to not receive a voting scene prior to the last vote.
During Jump Rope, he jumped the rope the second most amount of times out of any of the players, only behind Im Jeong-dae.
Seung-won and Gi-min are the highest-placing Six-Legged Pentathlon teammates in the series, both making it all the way to Sky Squid Game, placing 8th and 6th respectively.
When Im Jeong-dae's team is mocking Seong Gi-hun at the beginning of the third season, Seung-won is the only one not to applaud, instead he glares at him, showing he has true suspicion for him.
Seung-won's corpse is never seen from above in the tower in Sky Squid Game.
He is accidentally referred to as Player 363 in the English subtitles.
After he falls off the tower thanks to Lee Myung-gi, his arm appears to be broken, as we see the bone sticking out, just like with Park Min-su.
Lee Seung-won indirectly killed Player 359 because he beat him so severely with the bunk bed rail that he left Player 359 almost completely injured and sleep-deprived, Player 359 didn't have enough speed and strength to kill Kang Dae-ho, and that was because of the beatings Seung-won gave him and Some other factors. Due to this-very very indirectly-he may have saved Gi-hun's life since Gi-hun passed the Hide and Seek game due to killing Dae-ho. However Gi-hun probably would've killed Seon-nyeo if she hadn't mentioned Dae-ho.
In almost every BTS picture of him and other players, Park Jin-woo, barley smiles.
Since Seung-won placed 8th in the competition, his 33rd Squid Game counterpart is Jung Min-tae.
He is the first finalist to pass the fourth game without assistance and the first finalist to kill a female player on screen.
The fashion in which he killed Player 394 is very similar to how Gi-min showed him before the game started.
He is the first minor finalist to have a speaking role.
He was counted 36th in the Team O headcount.
Seung-won received the same vote count on the voting board twice in a row, with 6 votes for X and 12 votes for O, both on the revote and the penultimate vote.
Player 400 was probably going to be Seung-won's original victim in Hide and Seek, since player 394 was originally going to be player 276's victim and it was never planned that player 400 would appear in Jump Rope.
According to the list of uncertain origin he played Jegi in the Six-Legged Pentathlon, being one of four Jegi players to make it to the final game [Player 100, Player 333 and Player 456 being the others].
He has a slight resemblance to a younger Xi JinPing, the Premiere of China.
He has some similarities to Player 312: -Both are well-built adult male players in the 300s range who voted 0 in all their votes -Both are the second player to die in their respective final game, Jump Rope and Sky Squid Game -Both die in their game due to mistrusting someone they thought was their ally, Player 096/The Marine and Player 333/Lee Myung-gi/MC Coin/The Amazing Myung-gi, respectively and their supposed allies then push them off a great height to fall to their deaths -They are somewhat similar ages, Seung-won maybe in his early fifties, about five-ten years older then Player 312.
It is uncertain how large his debt was but most likely it was some way over a billion won but not close to the 5.6 billion won he thought he would get when 8 players remained, as he seemed so gleeful it is likely he would have some money left to spare: maybe somewhere between 2 billion and 2 and a half billion won.
