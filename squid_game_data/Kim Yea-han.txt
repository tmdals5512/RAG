History[]
Kim Yea-han was born on October 21, 1977. He graduated from Micheon Middle School in February 1994 and did not attain any higher levels of education. During his adult life, he held a position as a day laborer before attaining another job. He was working as a gas station attendant in Bongcheon-dong prior to his participation in the Squid Games.

Fate[]
While Kim Yea-han's fate is never revealed, it is presumed that he either died in the games or left after a majority voted to terminate them.

Behind the scenes[]
Kim Yea-han was portrayed by Kim Ju-sik.

Trivia[]
In 1989, a player named Mun Jang-ho won the 2nd Squid Games with Kim Yea-han’s number.
According to his player file, Kim Yea-han's resident registration number is 771021-1116982. He lives at 117-10 Bongcheon-dong, Gwanak-gu, Seoul.
Kim Yea-han seems to share the exact same player icon as Player 320 and Player 040, two players in the 33rd Squid Game.
The person in his icon is also one of the people to portray Player 239 in the 33rd Squid Game.
Kim Yea-han is stated to have worked 13 years as a day laborer and then 17 years as a gas station attendant, despite only 21 years having elapsed between the time of his participation in the 28th Squid Game and when he graduated middle school. This is most likely an error on the prop designer's part
