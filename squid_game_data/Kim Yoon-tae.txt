Kim Yun-tae (김윤태 Gim Yun-tae?), also known as Player 353, was a participant in the 37th Squid Game, as well as one of the finalists of the competition. He worked as a teacher outside of the games.

We'll send it... off to a better place. Come on, you don't have to watch when it happens. Just give me the baby. Or else, I'LL HAVE TO PUSH YOU BOTH OFF! http://squid-game.fandom.com/wiki/File:Yuntae.mp3
Kim Yun-tae to Seong Gi-hun during the second round of Sky Squid Game in "⭘△☐"
Kim Yun-tae, like the other members of Team O, seeks only to keep the games continuing at all costs to receive billions of Won in prize money for unknown reasons so he is greedy. Despite that, during the final game, he also proves to be very smart, quick at calculating math, and more organized and reasonable given his past as a math teacher, while still keeping his greedy and occasionally violent nature.
Despite his overwhelming greed, Yun-tae is more rational overall than the majority of 0 voters. He had shown several times to be generally averse towards violence, such as not participating in the Special Game, not being part of the group of Os that were teasing Gi-hun after the revote and the failed revolt, appearing to be affected while returning to the dormitory after killing Player 350 to pass the Hide and Seek challenge and grieving for Jae-cheol, also being one of the last seekers to do so, being horrified by Kim Gi-min killing Player 349 in Jump Rope, thinking about voting X after the fifth game when he thinks there is 8 players left, which means he only votes O because he debt is very large and opposing Gi-min's demand to kill off both Seong Gi-hun and Kim Jun-hee's baby simultaneously in the triangle platform of the Sky Squid Game due to being averse to having to kill one of his own teammates. Thus he is generally  a more rational person than the very cruel and violent Kim Gi-min, who only uses violent means, although the situation with the baby brought out the very darkest side of Yun-tae.
Due to his greed, he was particularly angry at Kim Jun-hee's baby being designated as the new Player 222 as per the Front Man's suggestion, being perfectly fine with killing her to restore his lost prize money. He joined Kim Gi-min when attacked Gi-hun, he himself attacking Lee Myung-gi. While he initially had the upper hand, he was left dangling on the iron pole and at Myung-gi's mercy. Lee Myung-gi refused to show any mercy to Yun-tae and kicked him off the triangle platform to his death. 

History
Makes perfect sense to me. Hey, this is the guy who said he was a teacher on the outside, huh? No wonder he's so smart.
Lee Seung-won complimenting Kim Yun-tae during Sky Squid Game in "⭘△☐"Kim Yun-tae was a participant of the 37th Squid Game. Before joining, he held a job as a math teacher. At some point, he likely got into heavy debt, almost certainly over 5 billion won. He was approached by The Recruiter and was given a card after playing Ddakji. He would then join the 2024 Squid Game and he then managed to survive Red Light, Green Light. In the first round of voting, he voted to continue the games. During the Six-Legged Pentathlon, he teamed up with 4 unknown players. They managed to complete all their minigames and completed the game. 
In the second round of voting, he again votes to continue the games. However, unlike Im Jeong-dae and the others, he is not seen trying to convince anyone to vote O. It is unknown who he teamed up with in Mingle, but he survived and managed to move on. In the third round of voting, he voted to continue the games.
After the third voting, when the players of both "O" and "X" factions started to argue, he is seen with the "O" team.
After the bathroom incident, he was present in the headcount for both teams and learned that Team X has outnumbered them, resulting the eventful Special Game in which he decided not to attack. Yun-tae did not join the revolt and continued onto the Hide and Seek game, he was then chosen as a Seeker, Im Jeong-dae offered to switch with him but he declined and later passed the game  by killing Player 350 with less than 5 minutes remaining.
After Hide and Seek concluded, Yun-tae and the other surviving players make their way back to the dormitory, where they have another vote. He decides to vote O again along with 15 other players. They end up winning the vote again and continuing the games further. 
Later on, him and the remaining players make their way to Jump Rope, Yun-tae and the other remaining players look in fear at the bridge they must cross. Later on, after witnessing the deaths of many players due to Player 096, he decides to stick in the back in fear. He then watches as Kim Gi-min kills Player 349 by kicking her off the ledge. Soon after he was the first person in the second group to cross the bridge, when Seong Gi-hun and Player 096 were fighting in the bridge, he is seen crying and begging for them to move, eventually after Player 096's death he managed to cross the bridge, then he also became one of the 9 finalists in The final game.

Yun-tae and the others found this unfair and, after a brief discussion, attempted to kill the baby to increase their share, but the Manager stopped their actions, saying that any act of violence isn't allowed until they enter the final game, and to celebrate their achievement, prepared a feast for the finalists. During the voting system, Yun-tae was willing to play one more game, and so he presses "O". 
The final day came for the last game, Kim Yun-tae and the other 8 finalists approached the arena and realized they will play Sky Squid Game. As the players entered the elevator and reached to the square, Jeong-dae pressed the button to commence the first round. Yun-tae and the others who voted "O" wanted to get rid of Gi-hun, Min-su and the baby to satisfy the elimination of three players and increase their shares. 

At first, they aimed at the baby, desperate to get rid of it, but Myung-gi stepped in, saying they should pick a lone target, which Yun-tae begins to agree with. Yun-tae and the group felt sorry that they must push Min-su off the square to eliminate one player, but since Min-su was on drugs, he couldn't help being vulnerable, instead he was pushed off the area, leading to his death.   What do I keep telling you? If we decide to kill them both, it means that, one of us is gonna die next round. So stop acting like an idiot, and let's think about this!
Kim Yun-tae to Kim Gi-min during Sky Squid Game in "⭘△☐"
After finishing round one, the group entered round two on the triangle. Yun-tae grabs the pole at the center of the area and aims at Gi-hun, asking him for forgiveness since he has to get rid of him and the baby. Yun-tae was about to push him off, but Gi-hun warns him that he would eliminate two players in the second round, meaning that they would have to knock out one person in their trusted group in the final round. Yun-tae began to have second thoughts about this, wondering if he's correct, which caused Gi-min to get impatient and threatened to kill him instead, but Myung-gi introduced another idea. 
Everyone knotted each of the jackets together as a rope, and the group were planning to take the baby away using a safety measure while Myung-gi points the pole towards Gi-hun, as well as convincing Gi-hun to draw lots. After some persuasion and a long-time argument, Gi-hun corners Seung-won, and Myung-gi yells for Gi-hun to move, pushing Seung-won to his death, surprising Yun-tae and the others for Myung-gi's ultimate betrayal.  
Later on, Yun-tae participated in beating up Player 039 to turn him into a "lunchbox", holding one of his legs up so that Kim Gi-min could stomp on it and fracture his thighs, immobilizing him.

Trivia
Kim Yun-tae shares a name with the actor of Player 069 in Season One, and also Player 320 who won the 2003 Squid Games.
Yun-tae is one of the three players that made it to Jump Rope whose player number is a prime number, the other two being Player 349 and Player 163.
Subsequently, he is the highest placing contestant in the 37th Squid Game to wear a prime number, as the other two died in Jump Rope.
If Yun-tae had grabbed Lee Myung-gi's hands, he would have fallen with him or perhaps somehow survived. If that had happened, Gi-hun, baby, Jeong-dae, Player 039, and likely Yun-tae and Myung-gi himself would have survived the second round.
Previously, the director wanted to put him on the blue team because the maze map showed 353 hiding. This meant that Player 350 was supposed to be the seeker, and he would have lost. But the director decided to swap 350 and 353. And if Yun-tae had completed the game as a member of the blue team, he would have been the only player and finalist in the 37th squid game, and in the entire squid game, he would have completed the first five games without his tracksuit being bloodied.
In "222", Kim Yun-tae said that their shares dropped by 700 million won because of the baby, but it was actually 640 million lost.
Kim Yun-tae is one of the two finalists who didn't have any lines in Season 2 despite being seen, the other one being Player 039.
Subsequently, his first line appears only at the start of Jump Rope, when Jeong-dae asks "What the hell is this?" and Yun-tae replies "I think we're supposed to cross to the other side."
When director Hwang gifted Lee Kyu-hoi flowers, he stated that he hoped Yun-tae found peace in heaven.[3]
The only time Yun-tae and Seung-won are seen voting is in the last vote since the rest were off-screen. When they voted in the first vote, it was between a montage, the second was in the part where the players chanted 'one more game' and it panned up, the third vote was between 377 (last player to vote before it cuts away), and 232 (the first player to vote when it returns), only 448 is seen voting in the revote, and the next vote goes up to Gi-min before cutting away. This is mainly because players in the 300s range are rarely seen voting.
He was one of 5 O players to cast the vote that gave the team the majority in voting, the other 4 being In-ho in the 1st, Player 426 in the 2nd, Nam-gyu in the revote for the 3rd, and Jeong-dae in the 5th. In other words his vote for O meant that the O team had a guaranteed majority and no matter how the following players voted, the next game [Jump Rope] would take place [and likewise for the cases of In-ho, 426, Nam-gyu and Jeong-dae]. Out of those five players Players 426 and Player 100 died in the games they confirmed would happen [Mingle and Sky Squid Game], Nam-gyu and Yun-tae survived the games they confirmed [which were Hide and Seek and Jump Rope respectively] and both died in the game after their special confirmed one and In-ho survived the game he confirmed [Six-Legged Pentathlon] as well as the following game [Mingle] and was then ''eliminated'' in the Special Game.
His occupation as a math teacher is similar to Player 062 and coincidentally both were the third player to die in their respective death game and further still Yun-tae placed 7th, exactly twice as good as Player 062 who placed 14th in the Games.
He was the third last known seeker to kill a Hider. The last two being Park Min-su and Seong Gi-hun.
Yun-tae's line after the argument between Gi-min and Jeong-dae over who would go up on deck first in Jump Rope was replaced by a line from Player 039 in the French version.
He was the only member of Im Jeong-dae's team who was not seen or present during Special Game.
He, Player 443, Player 192 and Player 344 are the only O voters to not laugh at Dae-ho.
Yun-tae was one of the last players to join Jeong-dae's team, as he wasn't seen with them during the special game or when they mocked Gi-hun, nor did he seem to have much of a relationship with other team members besides Seung-won.
Yun-tae didn't speak much in Season 2, until Season 3 when Jeong-dae asked what the game was in Jump Rope.
Yun-tae is the only finalist who died from falling off one of the platforms but did not have an announced elimination.
He is the penultimate player in the 3XX range to die, as well as the penultimate player with a palindrome number to die.
He is the last player in the 35X to be eliminated in the 37th Squid Game
Since Yun-tae placed 7th in the competition, his 33rd Squid Game counterpart is Han Mi-nyeo.
In early development for Hide and Seek, it would seem as if Yun-tae was going to be a hider, as he appears along side other hiders at the top of the Hide and Seek maze layout.
During the X and O confrontation and before the fifth vote, he sits in the exact same spot. When he's resting after Jump Rope, he sits in the furthest bed, being the closest thing to his spot.
Throughout the show, he performs several extremely exaugurated facial expressions, such as when telling Player 096 and Gi-hun to move, when the prize is increased, when the vote is in his favor, and at the starting point in Hide and Seek.
He is the last O voter finalist to pass Hide and Seek.
He was counted 43rd in the Team O headcount.
He is the only finalist in the 37th Squid Game whose 33rd Squid Game counterpart was never seen in the 2020 squid games.
He is the first player to vote O six times in the 37th Squid Game.
He is the first player to start a tie in the last vote, leaving it 1 X to 1 O.
Yun-tae's actor Lee Kyu-hoi, Roh Jae-won (who plays Nam-gyu), and Lee Seo-hwan (who plays Park Jung-bae) all starred in the drama Daily Dose of Sunshine.
He is the only finalist to die on his belly as appose to his back.
Yun-tae was originally supposed to be a Hider, as seen on a BTS map.
He and all his friends [this technically includes his friend Player 235/Park Jae-cheol] were killed by one player.
He is the only player in the front row of the headcount to not be involved in the Special Game.
Out of all the players who were not behind another player while crossing the bridge in Jump Rope, he took the longest.
Since he is a teacher and is around children most of his working days it is even more disturbing he was willing to casually kill an infant child.
According to a list of uncertain origin about every player's games in the Six-Legged Pentathlon  he and Park Min-su/Player 125 were the last two living players to play Gong-gi there and both were killed by Player 333/Lee Myung-gi/MC Coin/The Amazing Myung-gi.
He appears to be a good runner as he was able to catch up with the much younger Player 350 in Hide and Seek and kill him which probably means he had very bad luck in not encountering any Hiders previously in the game.
