History[]
Jang Do-yeong was ₩1.4 billion in debt when he was contacted by Squid Games staff in Itaewon Station with an opportunity to win large sums of money. Upon seeing his name and the amount of his debt in the Squid Game dormitory, he became embarrassed. 
He survived Red Light, Green Light thanks to the warnings of Seong Gi-hun. Later in the dorms, he was one of the players to beg for his life as he knelt on the floor, which happened after the results of the first game were given. After Gi-hun reminded everyone of clause 3 of the consent form which could allow everyone to leave if the majority agrees, much to his and everyone else's relief. Despite that, he watched as the Piggy bank filled with Prize money, much to his amazement. 

So, we can take a vote again and decide to leave after the next game?
Jang Do-yeong asking a Manager how the voting works in 001
He then voted to continue the games to try to make more money. For the next game, he teamed up with four others to play Six-Legged Pentathlon. They passed ddakji with ease. The next part of the challenge, Biseokchigi, that involved throwing a stone to hit a target, which was shaped like a gravestone with Squid Game symbols on it. Jang Do-yeong was tasked with completing the challenge. He struggled to knock down the stone, and he even accidentally stepped on the designated white line on the one attempt where he hit the target. After many attempts, the stress caused him to lose control and pee his pants. Eventually, he managed to hit the target, but it was too late, as his team ran out of time. 

Fate[]
Please don't...
Jang Do-yeong's last words to Kang No-eul in "Six Legs"
His team failed to complete the five games due to Do-yeong's repeated failures to hit the target and they were eliminated. Do-yeong was left alive, with non-fatal shots and a pair of corrupt Workers planned to use him in their organ trafficking operation, the workers put Do-yeong in a Coffin and marked the coffin with a "†".
However, Kang No-eul (Soldier 011) saw that Do-yeong was still alive, so she ordered the workers to open the coffin.  Do-yeong pleaded to her for help, but No-eul shot Jang Do-yeong in the chest and head as a coup de grâce, preventing the corrupted soldiers from taking his organs.

Trivia[]
The footage of his last throw on Biseokchigi was re-used during the Pentathlon Montage in One More Game.
Do-yeong would have to survive Hide and Seek for his debts to be paid off via his share.
The station where Do-yeong got recruited at was on Seoul Metro Line 6. Coincedientially, that is the number of times he needed to pass Biseokchigi.
In another show, Park Sung-hoon's (who plays Hyun-ju) character states, 'Do-yeong you bastard.'
His vote count was 106 O against 98 X.
If he had survived the Pentathlon and future games his voting pattern is hard to guess. He would have needed to survive up to Hide and Seek to have the money to pay his debts but it is not certain he would have made it that far based on his nervous personality: additionally most of his team-mates in the Pentathlon voted X and they might have been quite angered to see him vote 0 for the third game and especially after that [in a sort of reverse of Min-su's situation in the second vote, Do-yeong's team mates might have tried forcing him to vote X]. However at 79 million won/1.4 billion won only about 5.7 percent of his debt was covered after the second game and with 356 million won after Mingle, still only about 26 percent of the debt covered. A compromise might be that he would vote 0 for the second and final time after the Pentathlon, then immediately vote X after Mingle if he survived that game.
Mistakes[]
For a split second, Jang Do-yeong is seen being shot in the head as he's being eliminated with his team, yet is still alive and has no visible wound before finally being fatally shot in the head and chest by No-eul.
