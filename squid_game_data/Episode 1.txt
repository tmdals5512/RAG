Official summary[]
Hoping to win easy money, a broke and desperate Gi-hun agrees to take part in an enigmatic game. Not long into the first round, unforeseen horrors unfold.

—Official Netflix synopsis
Plot[]
Seong Gi-hun tells the audience a story from his childhood (in the 1980s), Gi-hun liked to play games everyday with his friends, especially his best friend Cho Sang-woo, the game they played was the Squid Game, which Gi-hun won and shouted "Victory". It was a time of happiness and innocence, with no worries.
In the present (June 8, 2020), Gi-hun is a cash-strapped divorced dad in Seoul, South Korea, where he works as a chauffeur. He's knee-deep in debt due to a combination of losing his once-stable career at a car manufacturing plant, a failed fried chicken restaurant and snack shop, and a gambling addiction. He lives in the working-class neighborhood Ssangmun-dong with his elderly mother, Oh Mal-soon, who gives him some money to get his daughter, Seong Ga-yeong, dinner and a present for her birthday, which is that day. After she leaves to go to her own job, he looks for her debit card, which she has hidden. He then steals her funds and gambles multiple times on horse races with his friend, Park Jung-bae. After winning ₩4.5 million won, he elatedly calls his daughter asking what she wants for her birthday. Unfortunately, he is spotted and chased by a group of Loan sharks and is robbed of his newfound money by Kang Sae-byeok, a pickpocket and North Korean defector. The loan sharks threaten to take his organs if they are not paid by the next month and make him sign a contract with his own blood. Gi-hun then takes his daughter out for food with what little money he has left. He treats Ga-yeong to a basic birthday dinner of street food and a useless gift from a claw machine (a toy gun). Gi-hun feels disappointed that he can't give her as much as her stepfather, despite her reassurance that she prefers the street food over the fancy steak dinner her stepfather treated her to.

    The salesman giving Gi-hun the business card  
At the train station, Gi-hun is approached by a mysterious salesman to play a game of Ddakji. Each round, whoever loses would give the other ₩100,000. However, since Gi-hun doesn't have that money, that salesman instead slaps him whenever he loses and says that he can pay by being physically abused, instead of with cash. Feeling he has nothing to lose, Gi-hun continues to play and is slapped many times, but eventually wins ₩100,000. The salesman gives him the money as promised, along with a strange business card with a phone number on it, and tells him to call it if he wants to play other games for bigger prizes. Gi-hun is suspicious and tells the salesman he won't be tricked into some kind of pyramid scheme, but the salesman reveals he knows Gi-hun's identity, history and that he is indebted to loan sharks for ₩160 million and to the bank for ₩255 million.   
Gi-hun is briefly shocked but is quickly distracted by the prize money and returns to his neighborhood, overjoyed. He stops to buy some mackerel from Cho Sang-woo's mother, a street vendor, who brags that her successful son is abroad on business. Gi-hun comments that Sang-woo got into the prestigious Seoul National University because of him, because Gi-hun used to take him to school when they were kids. Gi-hun returns home ecstatic and shows his winnings to his mother and pays her back what he took from her checking account. She asks how he got the money, and Gi-hun is vague with his answer. His mother informs him that his daughter's stepfather has a job offer in the United States and that Gi-hun needs to show that he is financially able to support his daughter to have any chance to stop her from leaving. She worries that Ga-yeong will forget how to speak Korean in United States and be unable to speak with him anymore.
During the night, Gi-hun looks at photos of Ga-yeong and tries to sleep, but he can't because of all the thoughts in his head, so he decides to do what his mother told him and try to get his daughter back. Gi-hun calls the number on the card that the seller gave him, the person who answers tells him that if he wants to participate in the game, states his name and date of birth. Gi-hun goes to an address given to him and a van arrives, the driver, who is dressed in a pink jumpsuit, asks Gi-hun if he is Mr. Seong Gi-hun, to which he says yes, the driver asks him for a password and Gi-hun answers "Red Light, Green Light", the password is correct and the driver opens the door for him. Gi-hun gets into the van and notices that everyone inside is asleep, he asks the driver how long it will take to get to the game site and if they should pick up anyone else, but then sleep gas is released and Gi-hun falls asleep, then it is revealed to the audience that the driver is wearing a black mask with a white circle, over which he has a gas mask. The masked man drives and takes Gi-hun and the others to the game site.
Gi-hun is woken up by classical music and discovers that he's in a gigantic Dormitory along with many other people, all dressed with a green tracksuit with white lines on the arms and legs and a number on the jacket. The players are monitored by a group of men dressed in pink jumpsuits from a control room and arrives the Front Man (the man in charge of the place), who is dressed with a black hood and a black mask that resembles a face. Gi-hun gets up, goes down the bunk bed stairs and meets Oh Il-nam, an old man who is counting people, Gi-hun asks him if he's counting how many people are there and Il-nam tells him to be quiet because he is distracting him, Gi-hun tells Il-nam to look at a screen in front of the dorm and that there're 456 people there, then Gi-hun notices that Il-nam is Player 001 (which means that he was the first one to get there), Gi-hun looks at the number on his jacket and finds out that he is Player 456 (which means that he was the last one to get there), Il-nam tells Gi-hun that he already knew that there're 456 and that he was just counting the numbers, since his doctor told him that doing so helps prevent dementia. Gi-hun questions why Il-nam is there because of his advanced age, the old man tells him that his end is near and then tells him that he has a brain tumor.
Suddenly, a fight breaks out between Sae-byeok (Player 067) and Jang Deok-su (Player 101), a vicious gangster and her former mentor. Sae-byeok and Deok-su start arguing about Sae-byeok supposedly running away from Deok-su, to which she declares that she became independent and Gi-hun recognizes Sae-byeok. Gi-hun pushes Deok-su away, grabs Sae-byeok and sees the scar on her neck, Gi-hun realizes that she's the stupid pickpocket who stole his money and demands the loser that she return it to him. Deok-su kicks Gi-hun and both men confront each other, Deok-su asks Gi-hun who he's and Gi-hun tells him his name and that he's from Ssangmun-dong, Deok-su tells Gi-hun that he and the fool of Sae-byeok have unfinished business, to which Gi-hun tells him that he also has unfinished business with her. Deok-su asks Gi-hun what his relationship is with Sae-byeok, to which Gi-hun asks him what he has against her and declares that Deok-su is obviously a thug who takes advantage of girls like Sae-byeok, then Gi-hun asks Deok-su if he told Sae-byeok to steal his money and the gangster tries to hit him, but fortunately Gi-hun manages to dodge the blow. Gi-hun tells the other players that Deok-su is trying to hit him and asks for someone to help him.

     A Manager explaining the games to 456 confused players  
The dorm door opens and Gi-hun asks the people who come to help him, he tells them that there is a pickpocket and a thug there and to catch them. Then nine guards, all dressed like the driver with black facemasks and dark pink jumpsuits, enter the room and face the players. Eight of the guards with circles on their masks and one with a square on his mask, who informs them that they will play six games over six days, and that whoever wins all six games will receive a large Prize money.
Oh Yeong-uk (Player 118) asks the Manager why they should believe what he is saying, since they took all their stuff and put them to sleep on the way to that place and brought them to that dorm. Then they show up and tell them they're going to pay them to play games. So he asks him if he expects them to buy what he's saying. The Manager tells him that they reluctantly took all of those measures to maintain confidentiality when they bringing them there and that they'll return everything to them once the games are over. Kim Mi-ok (Player 107) asks the Manager why they wear masks, to which the Manager tells her that they don't reveal the faces and personal information of their staff to the players and that it's a measure they take for fair games and confidentiality, so he asks them to understand. Then Sang-woo (Player 218) tells the Manager that he doesn't trust anything he's saying, he tells him that they tricked them and kidnapped them. He declares that he's making as many excuses as he can think of so that no one knows about the illegal actions that they are doing there and tells him that he must give them a reason to believe them, then the Manager says Sang-woo's name and number.
The Manager plays a video of Sang-woo playing Ddakji with the salesman and tells his story, causing Sang-woo to be recognized by Gi-hun. Sang-woo is 46 years old and the former team leader of Team Two at Joy Investments, Sang-woo siphoned money off from his clients' balances, invested it in derivatives and futures options and failed. Sang-woo's current loss is ₩650,000,000.
Then the Manager proceeds to mention the debts of other players:

Mi-ok (Player 107) ₩540,000,000 in debt
Oh Yeong-uk (Player 118) ₩1,020,000,000 in debt
Jung Min-tae (Player 322) ₩880,000,000 in debt
No Sang-hun (Player 119) ₩1,390,000,000 in debt
Park Ju-un (Player 369) ₩900,000,000 in debt
The Manager tells the players that they're all living on the edge with debts that they simply cannot pay and reminds them that when they played Ddakji and won, they gave them the money they promised them and asks them if they want to live like a piece of trash, running from creditors or or take advantage of the last opportunity they offer them.
Ali Abdul (Player 199) asks the Manager what games they'll play, to which the Manager tells him that they will not give out advance information about the games in orden to play fair, Gi-hun asks how much the prize money is and the Manager reveals a piggy bank, he tells them that the piggy bank will accumulate the money after every game and that they will reveal the amount to the players after the first game. The Manager tells them that if they don't want to participate, then they should let him know at that time.
Nobody asks to quit and instead they line up to sign the games consent form agreeing to the three clauses:
1) a player is not allowed to stop playing.
2) a player who refused to play will be eliminated.
3) if the majority of players wish to stop playing, the games may be terminated.
As the players each pose for identity photos, their photos are shown filling up an illuminated, diamond-checkered pattern on a floor somewhere.     
The guards lead all the players through multiple staircases through to the room for the first game, Red Light, Green Light. When Sang-woo arrives, Gi-hun approaches him and is surprised to see him here. Gi-hun tells that Sang-woo shouldn't be in here since he is a successful person, but Sang-woo wants to tell the truth later. A retractable roof opens revealing natural light over a dirt lot within four walls painted with resemble grass, trees, and a large blue sky and contains a large doll and a finish line at the other end. At the starting line, a voice announces the rules of the game: the players must cross the finish line in five minutes, but can only move when the doll turns her head away and sings "무궁화 꽃이 피었습니다 ('the hibiscus flower is blooming')". The players must stop when the doll finishes singing and turns her head around. A player who is caught moving when the doll turns around, or who doesn't cross the finish line in time, will be eliminated.   
Front Man arrives in his room and answers a call (speaking in English), he informs that the first game can begin and sits on a leather couch to watch the game on a giant TV.

     In the game, players must stand still when the doll says "Red Light", even when the deaths of the players around them unnerve them  
Once the game begins, Players 324 and 250 run quickly, determined to win first. 324 takes an early lead but is still moving when the doll is quiet, and a bang is heard and he falls to the ground. In the control room, his photo is darkened from the illuminated square. The players behind him are confused as a voice announces that 324 is eliminated, and the game begins again. 250 catches up to where 324 is lying on the ground, and mocks him for being eliminated, telling him to get up and leave. When he sees 324 suddenly coughing up blood and realizes he has been shot and not just stunned, 250 turns to run away and get shot as well. When his blood lands on Player 306's face, she screams in horror and is then killed for moving. That causes that the players discover that if they lose, they will die and they panic, several players run towards the doors and are detected moving, causing them to be eliminated, Player 133 is eliminated and his corpse falls on Gi-hun. Eventually, everyone is either still or dead, with the voice once again reminding the players of the rules of the game.  
The game resumes, but every player is too afraid to move forward, with the exception of Il-nam, who is enjoying himself despite the carnage. Eventually, other players start to follow his lead, with those who are careless getting eliminated. Meanwhile, Gi-hun is still lying on the ground frozen in fear. Sang-woo catches up to him and warns him of the timer and that he cannot stay on the ground for much longer. He also advises Gi-hun to hide behind other players, as the doll is a motion sensor and won't detect him if he is behind someone else. After Sang-woo's advice, Gi-hun finally gets up and resumes the game.
Sae-byeok creeps up behind Deok-su and threatens to kill him in revenge by pushing him to make the doll catch him. He begs for mercy as she taunts him, but she pulls him to the ground while the doll is looking the other way and overtakes him.
Gi-hun takes Sang-woo's advice to hide behind Player 305, but his quarry is shot. Oh Yeong-uk (Player 118), injured, then grabs his leg and begs him for help, preventing him from making any progress on the next two "Green Lights". Running out of time, Gi-hun pulls himself free and Yeong-uk is shot, this time fatally.
Front Man watches the remaining players try to win the game with a sip of fine liquor and turns on a Jazz machine which plays the song: "Fly Me to the Moon". 
Eventually, some players reach the finish line, including Sae-byeok, Deok-su, Sang-woo and Il-nam. Gi-hun almost makes it to the finish line when he trips over a corpse, fortunately Ali Abdul (Player 199) grabs him and prevents him from falling and being detected moving. With only 3 seconds left, Gi-hun and Ali run, jump and manage to reach the finish line just in time. The players who did not finish the game within the time limit (including Player 309) are eliminated. The roof of the game room closes as it is revealed that they are on a secluded island.

Trivia[]
This episode features the Red Light, Green Light game.
Hwang Jun-ho is credited, despite not appearing in this episode.
There were two scenes that were outtakes but were added in to show Gi-hun's caring personality.[1]
The scene where Gi-hun gives Sae-byeok's coffee back after bumping into her is an outtake. Jung Ho-yeon was hiding her laughter behind her hair.
The scene where Gi-hun feeds a stray cat was added by Lee Jung-jae.
During the game, Oh Il-nam is scanned with a thinner green outline, hinting to his place as the founder of the games.
The episode title in Korean "무궁화 꽃이 피던 날" directly translates to "The Day Hibiscus Flower Has Bloomed." The English translation subbed it as "Red Light, Green Light" to give the English audience more comprehension and understanding of the game.
