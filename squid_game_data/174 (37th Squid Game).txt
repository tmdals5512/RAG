History[]
Player 174 was presumably in massive debt and decided to join the Squid Games for a chance to win a huge cash prize. He called the number on the recruiting card, and got into a mysterious car. He then woke up in a giant room with 454 other players. He was likely confused and decided to make his way into the center of the room, where he watched as many other players debts were revealed in front of him. Later on, he listened to the manager explain the rules of the games, he signed a contract along with the other players, he took his photo in the photo booth, and he made his way up a flight of stairs to the first game.
During Red Light, Green Light, Player 174 and the other players watched as a strange man started screaming about how dangerous the games were. Player 174 and the other players ignored Gi-Hun's warnings, as they thought he was crazy and wanted the money for himself. Player 174 and the other players then continued the game without any worry. However, after sounds of gunshots ran out, and players started getting gunned down, Player 174 made a run for the doors in fear for his life.

Fate[]
During his attempts to run towards the doors, Player 174 was shot dead by a sniper for breaking the rules of the game. This is confirmed by his icon disappearing in the panic and him never canonically appearing again.

Behind the scenes[]
Player 174 was portrayed by Yeom Sang-jin and 2 unknown extras.

Trivia[]
In the first Squid Game in 1988, a player named No Hyun-Woo won the games with this number.
Similar to Park Do-seok in the 33rd Squid Game, he has less appearances and is not noticed much.
He was the first Player in the 17X range to be eliminated.
Mistakes[]
Player 174 is shown during Round 2 of Mingle.
His icon appears during Round 3 of Mingle despite him being eliminated in Red light, Green light.
