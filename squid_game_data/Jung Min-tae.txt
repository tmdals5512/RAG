History[]
Jung Min-tae was a homeless man with ₩880 million in debt, and chose to enter the Squid Games to win money. After waking up in a large room with 455 other people, he signed a contract and took his photo. He managed to survive the first game, Red Light Green Light, after most of the contestants had been wiped out. Back in the lobby, many of the players that survived including him were frightened because of the game's style of being eliminated. A few players and himself called out the staff for killing them. A Vote was held if the game should continue, he voted yes and explained why he voted so, but the majority voted no, and asked if he could continue the game along with the other players who wanted to stay. No Sang-hun confronted Min-tae for trying to bring other to his death wish, but it was interrupted by a soldier. However, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players.
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs, and the third game, Tug of War, and also beat his partner, who was either Player 028 or Na Jo-eun in the fourth game, Marbles. He survived all the way until the fifth game, Glass Stepping Stones. For the game, he chose the number 10, which meant he would be the tenth to go.

Fate[]
Damn it, you better go! Please just go on!
Min-tae to Deok-su before being pushed by Mi-nyeo in "VIPS"
When it was the turn of Jang Deok-su, the person in front of Min-tae, to guess, he refused to budge, saying that if they wanted to get out alive, they would have to go before him, as he refused to risk his life. Min-tae argued with Deok-su and refused to move up, thinking the timer would intimidate Deok-su into moving. Deok-su, however, had no issue with dooming everyone to their deaths. When Min-tae tried to argue that it was the rules for Deok-Su to go first, Deok-su declared that they were in hell, and there were no rules in hell. Deok-Su also ignored Min-tae's threat to push him, and as Min-tae continued to plead with Deok-Su, the impatient Han Mi-nyeo, the player behind Min-tae, pushed him onto Deok-su's glass, prompting Deok-su to push him off to his death. His body was placed in a coffin and subsequently cremated.

Personality[]
Are you Crazy, man! But the rule is that we go in order!
Jung Min-tae trying to get Jang Deok-su to go forward during Glass Stepping Stones in VIPS
Min-tae was portrayed as a determined man, willing to risk his own life in order to change it. He is convinced that, for him, sticking out to the end is better than giving up and go back to his miserable life. He also was one of the players who had the most potential to win the 33rd Squid Game due to his motivation and skill in the games, but he got unlucky by ending up between Deok-su and Mi-nyeo in glass stepping stones.
Min-tae was clearly short tempered as he is understandably quick to anger when Deok-su and Player 096 was stalling time for their different reasons. Especially when Deok-su attempts to force him to move, as Min-tae increasingly got more agitated and aggressive.

Trivia[]
In "Six Legs", after Jung-bae told Gi-hun that to keep playing may have been better than leaving, Gi-hun mentioned a man who said the same and died in the Squid Games. He could have been referring to Min-tae.
Min-tae was the only contestant in Glass Stepping Stones that died without stepping on the wrong glass tile.
Min-tae might be homeless, as he once stated that he has no home, where he played Ddakji seems to be a alley which strengthens the fact he is homeless.
His name is very similar to a protagonist in All of Us are Dead.
Technically he is the last living player to not cheat at any time or have an unequal advantage in the games. In order, the players who died after him; -Jang Deok-su/Player 101: Was given a lighter by Han Mi-nyeo in Dalgona to melt the candy easier and was also outright told what the third game was in advance by Player 111/Byeong-gi.   -Player 212/Han Mi-nyeo: Was on Oh Il-nam's team in Tug Of War and he possibly knew the game in advance as well: she was also skipped in the Marbles partner choosing meaning she passed the game without participating in it. -Player 360 was on Deok-su's team in Tug of War which technically means he also was part of the advantage since Deok-su knew the next game in advance.  -Player 017, Do Jeong-soo, had been a glassmaker for over 30 years before entering the Squid Game and hence had a large advantage in the Glass Bridge game. -Player 218 Cho Sang-woo was given a significant hint about the Dalgona game by Kang Sae-byok before it started and hence probably guessed the game was indeed Dalgona in advance and was also on Oh Il-nam's team in the third game. -Player 067/Kang Sae-byok had a hint about Dalgona from observing the sugar [and talking to Cho Sang-woo], was also on Oh Il-nam's team in Tug of War and in Marbles her partner outright surrendered to her. -Finally Player 456/Seong Gi-hun was again on Oh Il-nam's team in Tug of War and in the Marbles game his partner/opponent, Oh Il-nam again, also surrendered to him.
Since he said that on the outside of the games he might be killed like a dog it is possible he also had brutal debts from vicious loan sharks who would physically mutilate him to collect the debts.
He is the first of the second half of players to die in Glass Stepping Stones.
Since Min-tae placed 8th out of 456 players in this Squid Game it means when he died exactly 1/57 of the original players were left in the game or in other words he made it to the top 1.8 percent.
He was the second highest-placing member of his Tug of War team: only Player 017/Do Jeong-soo outlived him.
Despite being allies and agreeing with Player 212/Han Mi-nyeo in the first vote over the agreement that the players should continue the games, Mi-nyeo effectively kills him in the Glass Bridge game by shoving him forward impulsively.
