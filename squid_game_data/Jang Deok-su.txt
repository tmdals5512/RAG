Jang Deok-su (장덕수 Jang Deog-su?), also known as Player 101, was a participant in the 33rd Squid Game, as well as one of the few semifinalists in the competition.[2][3] Prior to his entry, he was a gangster running from gambling debts and the secondary antagonist of Season 1.
During the games, Deok-su formed a gang of players, which he was the leader of. He frequently clashed with Seong Gi-hun and members of his team, especially Kang Sae-byeok and Han Mi-nyeo.

Personality
Once the lights are out, sit quietly in a corner. Don't even breathe. If I hear you breathe... I'll come for you.
Deok-su warning Byeong-gi about the Special Game he will cause in Stick to the TeamDeok-su is a brutal and low-functioning sociopath who terrorizes and murders other players, taking delight in specifically targeting weaker contestants, regardless if it’s to improve his odds or just out of pure rage. Deok-su's cruelty is so great that even contestants like Cho Sang-woo are shown to be absolutely disgusted by how vile he is. Even in instances where his teammates were horrified by their actions (after the Tug-Of-War game), Deok-su can be seen remorselessly laughing as he kills more players. Deok-su is even more  evil than Oh Il-nam, the Front Man and the Masked Officer, who are controlling the games, as he lacks any redeeming qualities, is actually sadistic and eager to get his hands dirty. 
Despite relying on his strength most of the time, Deok-su is also intelligent and capable of strategizing. He starts a riot to eliminate the competition as soon as he realizes that there is no punishment for killing other players in between games. This also shows he is an utilitarian, believing that the game will end earlier if he massacres most of the contestants, and also opportunistic too, since he use the flaws in the rules to his advantages. He keeps the members of his team close to him, including the doctor to give him information about the games, but is constantly doubtful about his allies as he is aware that any of them might betray him. During the Marble game, as he is about to lose to Player 278, he is the only player who recalls the rule of equality among the contestants, which was briefly mentioned earlier, and uses it to save his own life by asking the masked soldier to switch the game. 
Deok-su show signs of an ungrateful individual, since he ditches Han Mi-nyeo in the Tug of War game over stronger men without a second thought, even though he only survived the Dalgona game thanks to her help and would have most likely died without it. 
Deok-su is also a massive hypocrite, since despite having no loyalty to anyone, he gets really angry when someone betrays him, like brutally stabbing his henchman, mistreating Sae-byeok, and laughing at Player 278 after beating him (since Player 278 was not loyal to Deok-su). Another time is him demanding Player 151 to make Player 244 move when the latter stopped to pray, only to later stop and demand others to either moving in front of him or everyone would die when the time ran out. 
Deok-su is shown to be a massive cheater, with instances like rallying his men to storm the game's facility to steal the prize money, to allying with Byeong-gi only for the information about the next game, and killing other players only to improve his odds of winning. 
Deok-su shown numerous signs of a individual with no moral standard or empathy whatsoever, which is evidence by him being the only one to smirk after killing the adversary team in the Tug-of-War game and after finding out that Player 069 hanged himself, opposed to his allies being visibly shocked and distraught. He's also the only one to show no remorse after winning against Player 278, if not ecstatic about it, contradicting to everyone being saddened by their allies's death. 
However, although pretending to be tough and dangerous, Deok-su is ultimately a coward who is afraid of death. He is also so egotistical that he'd rather die with the rest of the players than sacrifice himself for them. His cowardice and paranoia are shown multiple times, including when he intended to start another riot, but hesitated out of fear after Seong Gi-hun warned him of the possibility of his minions betraying and killing him.

History
2020
At some point in his life, Deok-su fell into bad standings with his criminal boss in Incheon and accumulates large gambling debts with loan sharks from the Philippines. He agrees to enter the Squid Game to escape those pursuing him and to win enough money to pay his debts. Following the vote to end the games after the first round, he plots to infiltrate the Games and steal the prize money, so he tries to assemble a crew outside, but his reputation is so badly damaged that he is unable to do so and therefore he has no other choice but to return to the games.[4]
After returning to the game, Deok-su forms and leads a gang with several other contestants, including Byeong-gi, Player 278, and Han Mi-nyeo. He repeatedly tries to assault Kang Sae-byeok, a contestant who once worked for him prior to the Squid Game, and at one point his gang starts a nighttime brawl that results in the deaths of several players.[5]
Deok-su initially includes Mi-nyeo in his gang after she helps him survive one of the rounds, and they have a brief sexual relationship, but he later expels her from the group and replaces her with physically stronger men after he learns from Byeong-gi that the next round is a game of Tug of War.[5]
For the fourth game, Marbles, Deok-su teams up with Player 278. Player 278 initially starts winning, but just as he's about to beat him, Deok-su demands to switch the game, which a guard approves. He ends up winning from a crazy stroke of luck, which causes Deok-su to blaringly cheer. Player 278, much to his despair, tries to escape, but runs into a dead end and gets gunned down as he curses at the guards.

Fate

You goddamn bitch!
Deok-su's last words before being killed by Han Mi-nyeo in "VIPS"
Deok-su survives until the fifth game, Glass Stepping Stones, during which he refuses to progress when he is at the front of the line unless other players move ahead of him. Mi-nyeo, who had earlier vowed to kill Deok-su if he betrayed her, makes good on her word by grabbing Deok-su around the waist. Deok-su tries to pry her off of himself and begs her to let him go, but Mi-nyeo mocks him and pulls them both off the bridge in a murder-suicide, causing them to fall to their deaths.[6]

Behind the scenes
Jang Deok-su was portrayed by actor Heo Sung-tae. He serves as a major antagonist throughout the majority of season 1 and is the main antagonist amongst Seong Gi-hun’s fellow Squid Game participants up until his death. His actor had to gain weight for the role of Deok-su.

Trivia
He was the highest placing player in the 33rd Squid Game whose number begins with 1.
Despite voting for X, Deok-su is the only X voter who doesn't celebrate the games ending.
He is the evilest X voter.
The only reason he voted X was to plan a rebellion by making his outside world gang gather weapons and forcing the guards to give up the location to the Squid Game, he was also had a plan to steal all the money in the piggy bank, but ultimately it all failed and never happened.
Jang Deok-su is one of the seven characters confirmed to be a smoker. The others are Seong Gi-hun, Park Jung-bae, Cho Sang-woo, Han Mi-nyeo, Thanos and The Masked Officer.
He has the highest kill count in the 33rd Squid Game, plus indirect kills.
He is the only Squid Game player seen also killing someone outside of Squid Game. This is seen in "Hell" where he kill gang member (his former subordinate) on Bridge at night.
He is the only shown player to laugh at his Marbles partner after passing the game.
It is confirmed by director Hwang that Deok-su wanted to be a rapper like Thanos but was forced to be a gangster by his father.
He is the only one of the main nine S1 player characters to never have his number announced.
He was possibly in affiliation with Player 202 from the 37th Squid Game as he is a gangster, and 202 also seems to be a gangster due to his tattoos.
