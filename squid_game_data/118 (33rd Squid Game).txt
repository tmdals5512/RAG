History[]
Before the games[]
Oh Yeong-uk began to accumulate a lot of debt, which accumulated a debt of ₩1.02 billion in total. Shortly before the games started, a recruiter approached him and played Ddakji with him. The Recruiter invited him to the Squid Games, where he could win more money, The Recruiter gave him a card.
On June night, call the card number, a van arrived with some pink men with masks in front and behind there were more people, and without warning they put him to sleep. He woke up in a dormitory with 454 other people, was sedated and taken to a giant room on a remote island, where he signed his life consent and had his photo taken to participate in the games.

"Red Light, Green Light"[]
      
After the guards explain the games and the prize, he questions their sincerity, considering they were all gassed, stripped and brought inside a "strange warehouse". When the staff projected footage of several contestants playing ddakji, Yeong-uk was one of the players whose name and amount of debt were stated.
Yeong-uk ultimately decided to stay, signing a consent form to enter and having his photo taken before proceeding. When the instructions for the first game were read aloud, Yeong-uk talked to the man next to him, Seong Gi-hun, asking if the game was the same one that they'd played as kids.
After Player 324 was detected moving and shot with a sniper, Yeong-uk asked Gi-hun what the loud noise made by the gunshot was. 

Fate[]
Help, please. I don't wanna die. Please, help. Help me.
Yeong-uk's last words to Seong Gi-hun in "Red Light, Green Light"
Yeong-uk apparently managed to survive the initial chaos that occurred after Player 324 and 250's deaths and advanced a bit further before being shot in the left leg.[2] He survived his elimination and crawled up to Seong Gi-hun, grabbing his leg and begging him to help him. Gi-hun managed to break out of his grip and run away, and Yeong-uk was subsequently detected by Young-hee and shot a second time in the chest, killing him.
His corpse was later shown during a montage that appeared when the Front Man was watching the game.

Trivia[]
Given the events of the second season, it's possible that Yeong-uk was deliberately shot in the leg so that he would survive long enough for his organs to be harvested and then later killed by a second unrelated sniper, similarly to Kang No-eul.
Outside of the main nine players of the first season, Yeong-uk is one of the only players to not have his tracksuit appear on an extra actor if we exclude icons, though this is mostly impart to the fact that he died so early on. Other minor players like Park Ju-un, No Sang-hun, Jung Min-tae, and Kim Mi-ok all at some point have a random extra with their number.
Yeong-uk would need to be among the last 40 players and survive Tug of War for his share of the prize money to be enough if the Season 2 rules applied.
Yeong-uk and No Sang-hun are named characters that are just one number apart. Interestingly, they also died in different games one number apart.
Mistakes[]
Oh Yeong-uk’s icon shows as a different man who has the same icon as Player 226.
In the Latin American Spanish dub, it is incorrectly and erroneously stated that his debt is ₩100 billion, which would be the largest debt in the entire series and 10 times larger than Im Jeong-dae's debt.
In fact, the total cash prize offered by the games wouldn't even cover half of this amount. To cover it up, he would have to win the games thrice if he had this large amount of a debt. And doing the math, 45.6 billion x 3 = 136.8 billion, meaning even if he spent all of the 100 billion on his debt, he would sill have an excess ₩36.8 billion.
