Jump Rope (줄넘기 Julneomgi?) was the fifth game of the 37th Squid Game. 
25 players (including the baby) participated in this game. 16 players (64%) were eliminated and 9 players (36%) progressed to the next round. At this point in the competition, 447 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 096, Nam-gyu (Player 124), Player 192, Kim Jun-hee (Player 222), Player 306, Player 349, Player 377 and Player 442.
This game took place on November 6, 2024.

Game Information[]
The game arena is a large rectangular room which features Young-hee and Cheol-su standing on two tall circular platforms lifted far above the ground by two pillars, connected by a train rail bridge with a gap in the middle. The walls have a somewhat abstract art style and are a switch from day to night with each side having different styles. Young-hee's side has a train station at night which contains the entrance that looks like a train stations, and the walls are dark purple and blue with some tiny stars on them, also having a giant moon in the ceiling as a lamp. Cheol-su's side is more like the entrance of a house, with the walls being now light blue with some white resembling clouds, and a giant yellow circle lamp in the ceiling, simulating the sun. At the bottom of the arena, there's a flower-themed abyss which is where the players fall to and die.
Players must cross the narrow bridge within 20 minutes, while  jumping to avoid the rotating metal rope, which increases or decreases in speed as time passes. There is a gap in the middle of the bridge, adding to the difficulty of the game. The green railway lights on the right side flash on-and-off to indicate the start of the game. Players may decide the order they cross the bridge amongst themselves.

Trivia[]
Gi-hun and Kim Jun-hee's baby will avoid getting hit by the rope, before they fell into the garden, and they've been eliminated
Player 084 was the only player in the game to not have his elimination announced or even shown on-screen.
This is technically one of the easiest games we've seen in the entire series, since everyone survived more than 10 jumps, everyone jumped very well, and with a little luck, about ten more players could have passed. However, due to Specific Problems, only 9 players passed. It is also possible some people would have lost balance on the further end of the rope, while jumping the gap in the middle of the bridge or due to other players moving slowly.
Player 306 was the only player to be eliminated in the middle of the bridge without touching the rope or getting pushed.
Kim Jun-hee was the only player to be eliminated without stepping on the bridge.
Im Jeong-dae jumped the rope more times than any other player during the game, due to Player 096 holding up the bridge the entire time he jumped. He jumped exactly 99, 100 or 101 times.
Funnily enough, if he jumped 100 times, he would've jumped the exact amount of times as his player number.
Player 096 was the only player who passed and later got eliminated. While 312 and 276 technically passed too by crossing the entire bridge, they died instantly afterward, before the announcer confirmed their advance.
Jang Geum-ja (Player 149) was meant to participate in Jump Rope, but she killed herself during the night after losing her son in Hide and Seek.
Lee Jung-jae accidentally spoiled he would be crossing the bridge while holding the baby in an interview before season 3 came out.
Due to safety reasons, the metal rope was later added in post-production as revealed in an interview with the cast.
The lower-half of each of the dolls were built practically and the upper half was added in CGI.
The rope is rotated by metal anchors attached to motors on the ceiling, rather than the dolls.
Director Hwang Dong-hyuk states that this game represents Young-hee travelling over to Cheol-su's house, who waits for her outside. He says Young-hee is in her best outfit for this visit.
This game lasts for a total of 25 minutes worth of screentime. This is also the same amount of players who passed Hide and Seek and including Kim Jun-hee's baby, participated in this game.
In terms of players who had contributed to the voting system, Lee Myung-gi was the only X member to successfully pass the game, while the other X members (including Jun-hee) were eliminated.
Gi-hun doesn't count because his votes were counted as abstention, as he didn't care to vote after the trauma he dealt with between Jung-bae's death and Dae-ho's words.
The lyrics to the song foreshadow events throughout the game.
'Little friend, little friend, turn around.' Player 349 yelling at Player 377 to turn back, a decision that led to both of their deaths.
'Little friend, little friend, touch the ground.' Nam-gyu crossing over to the pills on all fours.
'Little friend, little friend, lift your shoe.' Park Min-su crossing the bridge while holding his shoes instead of wearing them.
'Little friend, little friend, goodbye to you!' likely symbolizing Kim Jun-hee's death, as she leaves voluntarily (suicide), or just generally referring to the eliminated players.
Furthermore, the way this is said could be resembling that the most of the finalists or Young-hee and Cheol-su were taunting Jun-hee that she was dead either way, presumably singing this in a mocking tone. This can possibly be foreshadowed when Nam-gyu taunts Myung-gi that he was going to die in Hide and Seek, had he not switched with Jun-hee.
Traditionally, this game is played following the song lyrics as instructions (turning around, touching the ground mid-jumps, jumping on one foot, leaving the rope while it's spinning).
The rope can be heard creaking right before the jumps, which can make the game a bit easier.
This is the first game where Gi-hun didn't pass in the last seconds (excluding some rounds in Mingle).
The shots of Young-hee and Cheol-su in the Season 2 post credits scene were not used in the actual episodes.
This game takes place at an altitude of approximately 50 meters (164 feet).
This is the only game in season 3 not to be featured in any of the Season 3 episode titles. (Hide and Seek is mentioned in "Keys and Knives" and "The Starry Night" while Sky Squid Game is mentioned in "⭘△☐").
No women made it to the other part of the bridge, with the closest being Player 448, making it to the very edge.
The largest amount of players the first part of the bridge held was six, Player 377, Player 349, Player 163, Player 192, Player 442, and Lee Seung-won in that order.
This game share similarities with Glass Stepping Stones: Both are the fifth game of their competition, have a time limit, require players to jump, eliminated players will fall from great height. However, Jump Rope seems to have some sort of skills required to win, unlike Glass Stepping Stones where luck is the only factor that determinate the winners. Jump Rope also allowed players to decide the playing orders, while the order is decide before the Glass Stepping Stones game and is locked afterward.
Additionally This game also has a similarity to Glass Bridge/Glass Stepping Stones in that both games saw an unexpected difficulty in the players passing, cause by another player: Jang Deok-su in the Glass Bridge game who refused to move when it was his turn in that game which meant the other players had to risk their lives sooner and in this game Player 096 who was going to push off any players who tried to approach the finish line: in a parallel Deok-su's problem means the players have to move ahead in the game quicker then they want while Player 096's plan means they have to hold back from moving forward.

No elderly players died in this game.

It and Squid Game itself are the only games to not have an eliminated player whose name was revealed through the files or credits.
There is a poster within the starting house that holds the eliminated players on it. When translated, the title is 'timetable,' the writing above the arrow says 'downline,' and the subtitle for the numbers is 'train number.' This means this poster shows the timetable for trains. The trains are symbolized through the players, hence the player numbers. The downline (which refers to where a train is going) states that they are going from the house, to the flower. In summary, the trains (players) begin at the train station (house), and then they go (players crossing the bridge, which is a railroad), and end up arriving at the flower (the players fell to their deaths, the floor being covered in painted flowers).

However, thanks to Player 096's plan, only 9 players (counting the baby) survived this game.
