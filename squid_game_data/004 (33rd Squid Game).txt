History[]
Jang Seung-jong was born on February 16, 1970 to his then 13 year old mother (Jung Mi-hee) and his then 21 year old father (Jang Ha-kwon). He attended Gyeonggi Junior College and worked as a bus driver for Mukook Transportation for 13 years. He lost his job on December 21, 2017 after getting into a car accident while driving under the influence which caused injuries to 4 people. As of 2020, he was unemployed and living with his parents.[2]
Jang Seung-jong gathered a lot of debt, leaving him desperate for money. He eventually met a man in a suit who offered to play ddjaki with him. He managed to beat him at the game, and the man then gave him a card with a phone number to join the Squid Games, He made the call and gave the game his name and date of birth. He then went to an address given to him. Suddenly, a van showed up; He got in through a password, "red light green light." The driver was shown in a pink jumpsuit, but he had a gas mask. Suddenly, sleeping gas is released, making him fall asleep, and then the van drove off.   
He then woke up to classical music in a jumpsuit on a bunk bed, in a creepy warehouse called the "dormitory." Then, the same mysterious drivers, now known as "Guards," came in, telling him the instructions and that they were in a competition. He then signed a contract and proceed to walk into a giant room with multiple staircases, with classical music following the instructions. He then took his picture, following the guards into the first game: Red Light, Green Light.
Jang Seung-jong could be seen confused and doubtful about the giant doll, and an announcer explained the game rules, and so, The game begun. He and many other players ran, though a player, Player 324, ran too fast and couldn't stop in time, followed by a loud bang and him dropping to the ground. Which made Player 250 scared and try to run away in panic, before getting shot, with his blood landing on Player 306's face, which made her scream and also get shot.
After witnessing the death of 3 players, A lot of players tried to run to the doors but were shot and eliminated from the game. Although Jang Seung-jong, along with many other players, managed to survive the initial chaos, and so the game continued, Jang Seung-jong stood still and would be seen running with his close friend, Na Jo-eun on some red lights during the Fly Me to the Moon scenes, Seung-jong would then be able to  pass the finish line, but other players weren't fast enough and were killed for not making to the finish line in time.
Upon returning to the dormitory, Jang Seung-jong was scared and shook from witnessing so many deaths, until the guards barged into the room, which made Jang Seung-jong back away in fear. Some players started to protest about the game, until a guard grabbed his gun and sent out a warning shot in the air to stop the ruckus, which made Jang Seung-jong duck down to the floor, startled. the same guard then reminded the players about the first two clauses of the contract they signed, though another player, Player 218 (Cho Sang-woo), argued back about the third clause, which was that the players could vote whether to leave or stay in the game, and the guards accepted the request to begin a vote, however, the guards first showed the prize money accumulated in the first game, which made lots of players start questioning their choices, and so, the vote begun.
During the voting, Jang Seung-jong voted to continue the games (O). After Player 002 voted, the counter went into a tie. so the last player, Player 001 (Oh Il-nam), was the one to make the final decision, and decided to ultimately, end the games. Jang Seung-jong was sad, since he voted to continue the games, but he came back for unknown reason.
After he came back into the games, he went into a giant room with multiple staircases to get to the second game, Sugar Honeycombs. He walked into the second game with 186 other players. He was looking around until the announcer instructed the players to choose one of the four shapes on the wall and line up in front of it. The four shapes were, the circle, the triangle, the star, and the umbrella. Jang Seung-jong was thinking about what shape he was gonna choose until he decided to choose the umbrella shape, the most complex shape.

Fate[]
It is unknown when he died. He is seen panicking but there is no corpse to be seen, he is last seen in after the Special Game. His icon disappears in Red Light, Green Light.

Trivia[]
Jang Seung-jong shares his name with Player 063, the victor of the 4th Squid Game.
According to his player file, Jang Seung-jong lived in the Pungsan-dong neighborhood of Hanam in Gyeonggi Province.[2]
His icon shows the female Player 004, who is also seen in Sugar Honeycombs and Special Game.
His Red Light, Green Light actor is also the actor of Player 068.
Jang Seung-jong's shares her icon with Player 275.
Jang Seung-jong's Squid Archives icon is the same as Players 048, 150, and 339's grid icon.
Mistakes[]
When Hwang Jun-ho opens the player list for the 28th Squid Game, Jang Seung-jong's file appears. However, it explicitly states that he participated in the 33rd Squid Game, with his parents' ages lining up as such. This was most likely an error on the prop designer's part.
On his file it states that his fathers last name is Kang. This is false since 004's last name is Jang so his would have to be as well. Or he was adopted.
Although it states what school he went to, it doesn't show the admission and graduation date.
Behind the scenes[]
Jang Seung-jong was portrayed by Choi Jong-guk, Lee Kwang-sik, Oh Tae-nam, and Park Gu-young.
