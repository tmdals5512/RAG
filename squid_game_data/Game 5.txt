Glass Stepping Stones (징검다리 건너기 Jing-geomdali Keonneogi?) better known as Glass Bridge was the fifth game of the 33rd Squid Game.
The game involved contestants attempting to cross two parallel bridges by jumping across tempered glass panels while avoiding weaker panels of normal glass. Those who landed on a normal glass panel broke through the panel and fell to their deaths from a high place, resulting in their elimination from the game.  
16 players participated in this game. 13 players (around 81.2%) were eliminated and 3 players (around 18.8%) progressed to the next round. At this point in the competition, 453 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 017, Player 062, Player 096, Player 101, Player 212, Player 244, Player 322 and Player 453.
This game took place on June 29, 2020.

Game information[]
The glass bridge in the VIPS room
Players were asked to choose a vest with a number from 01 to 16 before the game; shortly after it was revealed to the players that each number represented the order in which the contestants would play.  

     VIP's watching the game  
The game took place in a colossal chamber decorated to resemble the interior of a circus tent. Players began the game atop a platform at one end of the room which was connected to another platform at the opposite end by two parallel bridges, each one holding 18 glass panels. Each of the panels was either made from tempered glass, which could withstand the weight of two players; or normal glass, which would shatter on impact and cause the player to fall to their death, resulting in their elimination. The goal of the game was for players to successfully cross the bridges by jumping across the tempered glass while avoiding the normal glass, within a 16-minute time limit.
Once the given time limit had reached zero, all glass panels exploded to eliminate any players who remained on the bridges. Broken glass fragments from the explosion greatly injured the three successful players, with Player 067 receiving a debilitating injury from the shrapnel.

Methods to win[]
If players have knowledge about glass-making like Do Jeong-soo, it will be easier for them to pass since tempered glasses and normal glasses can be told by looking at the reflection of the glasses or hearing the sound of the glasses. Another way to know which way to go is to throw leading players at a glass panel to test whether it is tempered or not.
Although never demonstrated, nothing in the rules prohibits standing on the railings or the ends of the glass, leading to higher chances of surviving regardless of the guess.

Probability analysis[]
    Correct order to complete the game. (The circles are the panels that they had to step on.)  
It is possible to prove that each player will reveal 2 steps on average (including the one they die on). Therefore, we expect around 9 players to be eliminated and 7 players to pass[1].

Both of these calculations assume all players play the game perfectly and remain in the same order. In reality, there may be various "inefficiencies" such as players making mistakes, pushing/killing each other, or running out of time.
Since only 3 players survived, we can conclude the other players were unlucky and/or played quite "inefficiently". This is especially evident in how Player 017 (13th) had to use his glass knowledge to guess 3 panels and was still 1 panel behind. Using the code in the above source[2], it can be shown the 13th player only has a 0.65% chance of needing to guess at least 4 panels, if everyone plays perfectly.

Trivia[]
The players were very unlucky during this game, it has been calculated in most instances seven or more people would win the game. If we assume that each player guesses at random, they should break nine of the eighteen glass panels.
There were supposed to be 20 players alive after Marbles, although only 17 are seen alive at the start of VIPS. It is likely that three pairs did not finish their game in time, and hence three more players were eliminated.
Due to one person ending his life before the game started, there was only 16 players who participated in the Glass Bridge.
In reality, you would not see stains on tempered glass; instead, a light effect called anisotropy would be visible under certain lighting conditions.
This game is based on 징검다리 (Jing-geomdali), a kid's game in Korea. It is referred to as such in the original Korean version. Video of children playing the game here
While unconfirmed, it is theorized that the players in the front could have survived longer within the bounds of the game by guessing a few tiles and then forcing those behind them to go ahead. Though the rules state that their numbers are the order in which they have to play, they are somewhat vague in this regard and do not specify that one must keep guessing until their elimination after getting on the bridge and guessing at least one tile correctly, possibly creating a loophole.
It is also speculated that the players could've survived by walking along the metal beams supporting the tiles or by throwing their shoes at the glass to see whether they were tempered or not. This is unlikely though, as the method would have gone against the spirit of the game and would likely have been disallowed.
A version of this game is featured in both Squid Game: The Challenge and Squid Game: Unleashed respectively.
Before the release of Season 3, glass stepping stones had the highest mortality rate, however, it was beaten by Sky Squid Game.
This game takes place at an altitude of approximately 20 meters (65 feet).
Every Player with their number past 8 (excluding Player 360) is named.
This is the only game in the 33rd Squid Game with no erroneous players.
Because there were fewer players than steps, it was the game with the highest probability of all players dying.
This is the only game so far where playing barefooted is mandatory (although Jump Rope did allow players to be barefooteđ, as Min-su is seen playing it, it is not mandatory to).
Less then four percent of the original 456 players passed to this game.
Most likely, there were 20 tiles because there would be 20 players left after Marbles. That is, the game organizers knew for sure that there would be +- 20 players.
Even though there are 18 tiles and 16 players entering the game only four players actually guessed a tile correctly-Player 096, Player 062, Player 407 and Player 017 [Do Jeong-soo]: None of the female players guessed a tile correctly though Ms. Player 453 did correctly remember the last tile Player 062 went over correctly.
Of the 17 players who qualified for this game 7 of their votes [about 40 percent] are known: only 2, Seong Gi-hun/Player 456 and Jang Deok-su/Player 101 are confirmed to vote X while Player 453, Jung Min-tae/Player 322, Cho Sang-woo of SNU/Player 218, Han Mi-nyeo/Player 212 and Kang Sae-byok/Player 067. A possible estimate [JUST AN ESTIMATE] for the other ten is: Voted X: Player 407, Player 308, Player 096 and Player 021. Voted O: Player 360, Player 244, Player 151, Player 069, Player 062 and Player 017.
No player guessed exactly two tiles correctly.
Of the last four players three were killed by Cho Sang-woo/Player 218 and two of them committed suicide.
