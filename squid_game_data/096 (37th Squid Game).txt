Player 096 was a former marine who was a participant in the 37th Squid Game, as well as one of the few semifinalists in the competition. He appeared in Season 2 and Season 3.
Initially recruited by Kang Dae-ho as a potential teammate in the second game, supported by Park Jung-bae, Player 096 was a consistent member of Team O. He fought against Seong Gi-hun during the fifth game and was eliminated by him.

Personality[]
No one can cross the bridge now! Game over, motherfuckers!https://squid-game.fandom.com/wiki/File:096Quote2.mp3
Player 096 taunting all the Players during Jump Rope after killing Player 276 in "222"Player 096, like the most Team O players is ruthless, greedy, violent and sadistic. During the Special Game, he attacked the X team and killed Player 143. During the Hide and Seek game, he violently stabs Player 105 to death. After each game, 096 seems to become more greedy and psychotic as he shows his true sadistic nature by pushing Player 312 and Player 276 and blocking the bridge to prevent other players from passing, because he wanted to split the money with Gi-hun only. It's also noted that other than his greed, he finds it fun to mock other players when he was blocking the way.
096 shows no sign of moral standard or empathy, since, despite being aware of Jun-hee's pregnancy, he still voted O out of selfishness and greed, knowing there was a baby in the games. He is often considered one of the most evil players, due to his violence and what he did on the bridge. He is extremely avaricious and he is willing to kill anyone just for extra money. This like many members of Team O included his own allies as their deaths meant more money for himself. Even though 096 was originally a marine it's doubtful he had any genuine honor in protecting his nation. Additionally, he shows no remorse as he is constantly mocking  the players stuck on the bridge all while attempting to kill them.    

History[]
Not much is known about Player 096's past, except for the fact that he was a Marine in 946th Cohort prior to joining the games. By 2024, Player 096 was presumably in heavy debt (he considered voting X after the fourth game when the prize money was 1.724 billion won;  since he had been extremely confident prior to this it is likely though not certain this was enough to cover his financial debts).  At some point, he was approached by The Recruiter and played Ddakji with him. After winning, he was given 100,000 won and a business card, which he used to join the Squid Games.
After waking up in the Dormitory, the Squid Game staff explained the rules of the games to the participants. He signed a contract, took his picture and went up the stairs, that brought him and 454 other people[2] into the first game, "Red Light, Green Light". Seong Gi-hun runs in front of everyone and starts shouting that the games are deadly and will kill everyone, however almost nobody believes him and thinks he's insane. The game's rules are announced, and it finally starts. For 7 rounds, no one moves, but in the next one, Kang Mi-na is fatally shot, making people panic. Player 096 managed to stay still during the chaos, and survived. Following Gi-hun's instructions, he passed the first game successfully.

Fate[]
You fucking idiot! We could've ended the game here!
Player 096's last words to Seong Gi-hun in "222"
While Gi-hun helped the players cross, 096 lunged at him, causing them both to fall onto the bridge, in a attempt to kill him for ruining his plan and saying that they could had ended the game here, once again blocking the way, which led to 3 other players to get killed. Gi-hun jabbed his nail into 096's left eye, making him scream in agony as blood poured out. He was then kicked by Player 456 towards the bridge, and was hit into the abyss by the metal rope. He went flying upwards with the rope then thrown into the air. His body was then taken away to be cremated after the game ended. He placed 11th in the competition.

Trivia[]
He was the first supporting character who knew that Kim Jun-hee was pregnant.
It is considered that he indirectly and voluntarily provoked Jun-hee's suicide. He caused for time to be wasted, which prevented Gi-hun from returning to rescue Jun-hee with a large window of time. If 096 hadn't been so voracious and selfish, Gi-hun would have had enough time to go back for Jun-hee.
Both half of his number (048) and double his number (192) were also male predominant O voters that made it to season 3
In the Hungarian dub, during the Six Legged Pentathlon, he says he is in Cohort 46, whereas in the original version, he states Cohort 946.
Player 096's response to Gi-hun's question was "What do you think? I'm playing the game." Before the release of the third season, this quote was mistakenly attributed to Player 202, who appeared on the screen when it was said due to his voice.
Both him and his season 1 counterpart made it as far as the fifth game and died in it.
Coincidentally, Gi-hun was involved in both 096s deaths. He gave his number 1 vest to 096 from Season 1, causing him to go first on the glass bridge.
Unlike his S1 counterpart, he didn't go first in Jump Rope, but was the first one to go after Gi-hun passed.
Matter of a fact, viewers like to call this coincidence "Player 096's revenge."
He placed five spots higher than season 1 Player 096, with season 1 Player 096 making it to 16th place out of 456 players and Season 2 and 3 Player 096 making it to 11th place out of 456 players.
He, Kim Nam-du, and Player 303 are the only known players in the Squid Game series to pass a game and later be eliminated in the same game.
Player 096 was shown to be extremely good at Jump Rope, taking it very slow but not even remotely losing balance once, It is possible like the Gi-hun meme he had played this game before.
Player 096 had the most potential to win the 37th Squid game, as he was highly intelligent and an ex-marine, besides having strong allies. If not for Gi-hun, he would have killed every player in Jump Rope.
His hair noticeably grows in length between his first appearance in the Pentathlon and Season 3. This was most likely not intentional and was a result of extended periods of time between the filming of the two in real life.
Seong Gi-hun gouging Player 096's eye directly evokes the scene of Lee Cheong-san gouging Yoon Gwi-nam's eye and later Gwi-nam himself gouging Cheong-san's eye in the kdrama All Of Us Are Dead.
Coincidentally: Player 096, Gwi-nam, and Cheong-san's eyes that were gouged out was their right eye.
However, in contrast to Cheong-san and Player 096, Gwi-nam's eye was gauged with a phone instead of them being gauged out by fingers.
It's unknown if he decided to leave Gi-hun's team in Six-Legged Pentathlon or they replaced him with Jun-hee.
If we translate the original Korean dialogue, Jun-hee thanks them for accepting them in the team, which implies they probably replaced him with her.
If it wasn't for him, almost every player would've been able to pass Jump Rope, but thanks to him holding the bridge, 12 players died, reducing the amount of players significantly.
When Gi-hun killed 096 he did indirectly avenge all 12 players, as well as any X voter 096 attacked or killed in the Special Game.
After Player 105's death, when the announcer mentions that 105 died and 096 passed, you can hear a slight metallic clink, suggesting he might've dropped the knife after killing Player 105. It's unknown if this was intentional or not.
Both Sae-Byeok and Player 096 deaths were caused by the same 5th game they passed, the difference is that Sae-Byeok died after the Glass Bridge game was over.
In Jump Rope, he is the only one to get his full body hit by the rope instead getting hit on the leg.
He is the last player in the 09X range to be eliminated in the 37th squid game.
During the fifth vote, it seemed that Player 096 was considering whether to change his vote to X or not, maybe because of the death of Player 296 or because the money accumulated already more or less covering his debt.
In a behind-the-scenes video shows that Gi-hun and Player 096 were originally meant to engage in a brief struggle before knocking each other to the ground. But the scene was shortened in Player 096 simply throwing Gi-hun to the ground.
Since Player 096 placed 11th in the competition, his 33rd Squid Game counterpart is Player 151.
He also shares a similarity to Jang Deok-su [Player 101] in that both of them cause an unexpected disturbance to the other players in the fifth game that puts their lives at risk: Deok-su did this by forcing other players to move in front of him on the very dangerous glass panels while Player 096 did the opposite by forcing them to not move forward at all in the Jump Rope game in order to make them lose balance and die.
He was counted 42nd in the Team O headcount.
From when he is introduced to when he dies, One More Game is the only episode he is not canonically in.
He is the only player in the 0XX range to vote up to 5 times for the same option.
He was the second player to pass Hide and Seek and also the second adult player to pass Jump Rope behind Gi-hun which in a funny coincidence makes him the second player to pass two games and they were also back to back/consecutive games. This is the opposite of Park Min-su who also passed the fourth and fifth games but was the second last player to do so in both of those games.
He is the last living male player with the most facial hair. The others who come close are Player 192 and Kim Gi-min who both have slight stubble at some points in the games as well as Player 442 having a very thin beard but they do not have goatee beards like Player 096.
He and Park Jung-bae are the two characters confirmed to be Korean Marine veterans.
He was considering changing his vote to X after Hide and Seek, mulling for about eight seconds before choosing to press 0 for the fifth time. If he had changed his vote to X there is a chance that some of the unwilling 0 voters who voted after him [Player 448 in particular and probably Player 260 and Player 435 as well] might have voted X because they had less fear: there was a greater chance they could leave the games and even if the fifth game ended up happening Player 096 might protect them in the event of another Special Game. Therefore while Player 353/Kim Yun-tae is the most guilty for Jump Rope happening because his vote confirmed it Player 096 might be considered the second most guilty.
If he had not attacked Gi-hun and let the remaining players after Player 084's death pass, and the final game happened, there is a chance the violent 0s might have targeted him first in Sky Squid Game since he almost caused their deaths in the previous game and they could want revenge. Since he is an extremely skilled fighter though-a Marine Corps veteran, participating in the Special Game, overpowering the young and strong Player 276 and being the second tagger to pass Hide and Seek-he likely would have been very difficult to eliminate.
Since Player 096 placed 11th out of 456 players he made it to about the top 2.4 percent: or in other words at the time he died only 1 out of 41 [roughly] of the original 456 players were still alive.
Along with the Recruiter he is probably the most impactful unnamed character in the series since about twice as many players would have passed Jump Rope without him and there may have been enough Xs and prize money to finally end the games and all those people would have lived: while the Recruiter of course generates the events of the Games by recruiting the Players.
Behind the scenes[]
Player 096 was portrayed by Lee Suk. He was credited as "해병남" (Haebyeongnam?) in Korean, which roughly translates to "marine man". In the English dub, he is voiced by Jason Jin.
