Personality[]
Whoa, hey. You immature little dipshits, who taught you that kind of shit was okay?
Player 145 to Thanos and Nam-gyu before the bathroom fight in "O X"
Player 145 is a loyal, brave and trustworthy man. He voted O in the first vote because he needed the money, but his second and third vote shows he has determination to leave the games once he has enough money. Before the Bathroom Fight when Thanos and Nam-gyu were pressuring Park Min-su to change his vote to O Player 145 intervened and calls both of them “immature dipshits” for threatening other X players during the Bathroom fight he throws Player 299 into a mirror and kills him in an unknown manner as he avengers Player 401. He then hid during the Special Game eventually when the riots ended he joined Gi-hun’s Rebellion but even though they lost the battle he kept fighting until the end proving his bravery and loyalty.

History[]
Player 145 was in heavy debt so he decided to join the Squid Games. He was able to make it through the first three games: Red Light, Green Light Six-Legged Pentathlon (teaming up with Kim Gi-min, Player 206, Lee Seung-won and Player 238), with his team passing the game, and then playing Mingle . He voted O in the first vote, and X in the second and third vote, and later appeared in the bathroom , he scolded Thanos and Nam-gyu for pressuring Min-su to change his vote, right after Myung-gi warned him and the rest of Team X about what they were doing, and standing up for his teammates.

Quiet, the adults are talking!
Player 145 telling Player 442 to have some manners during the Bathroom Fight in "O X"
After the fighting begins, he is shown attacking members of "Team O", even killing one of them in the fighting. He was also one of the players who joined the rebellion. During the beginning of the bathroom fight, he managed to grab a hold of Thanos' head, but was pushed by Player 442. At some point of the fight, he is seen stomping Player 442, with the help of Player 324. 145 then proceeds to slam Player 299 into a mirror, shattering it, and throwing the latter in to a wall before they both continue fighting offscreen and throw each other in to Walls (Presumably). He then manages to kill Player 299 in an unknown manner.

After the Bathroom Fight ended, Kwon Byeong-su accused Team O of starting the fight to increase the prize money. Player 192 countered this accusation by accusing the X's of attacking Team O so that they could win the vote, but Player 145 backs up Byeong-su by reminding them that they lost several members. 145 then proceeded to join the X's headcount after Im Jeong-dae asks how many players were lost on each side. Once Kwon Byeong-su was done counting, he along with 145 and the rest of Gi-hun's team hid behind a bed, and discussed about the Special game. Oh Young-il had the idea of attacking Team O first, which Byeong-su and 145 had approved. However, Gi-hun went against this idea, saying that it was what The Frontman and the V.I.P.s wanted. Gi-hun then tells his entire team to hide under bed bunks so that none of them would die, allowing for a perfect rebellion. 145 and Byeong-su hid under a bed together, witnessing all the chaos that was happening during the Special Game.This was oddly out of character, as 145 willingly let the other X voters get brutally murdered.  Where the fuck is that asshole?!
Player 145 asking what was taking Dae-ho so long to retrieve the ammunition in "Friend or Foe"Once the Special Game had ended, the guards came in to scan the dead bodies without knowing that Gi-hun's rebels were playing dead. Once the guards began to scan bodies, Gi-hun tackled a guard from the front, forcing him into a chokehold. Park Gyeong-seok then grabbed the arms of a soldier, allowing for Cho Hyun-ju to steal a revolver, killing the soldier. Two more guards appeared from the sidelines, aiming at Gi-hun. Once Kwon Byeong-su was busy grabbing the MP5 and shooting the dead corpse out of spite, Player 145 was trying to aim at more guards who were running across the dormitory. Once all the soldiers in the dormitory were dead, Player 145 along with the other rebels were taught by Cho Hyun-ju on how to effectively use a MP5. 145 and the others climbed up the stairwell, to be suddenly ambushed by a group of soldiers. Following the deaths of 072 and 206, along with Gi-hun and Jung-bae running to the control room, Hyun-ju asks the group if they still had enough ammo. 145, Kwon Byeong-su, Park Gyeong-seok, Oh Young-il and Player 015, and Player 324 were all running out of ammo. Kang Dae-ho had no ammo left, so he was tasked to bring back the ammo. Due to this, Player 145 was getting more impatient for the ammo to arrive. Young-il suggests that he, Byeong-su and 015 all enter the control room to meet up with Gi-hun and Jung-bae.
Advance team, do you copy? We're out of ammo over here. I'm gonna surrender.
Player 145 telling his team over the radio that they are out of ammo and he is surrendering in "Friend or Foe"
Cho Hyun-ju retreats immediately to find Dae-ho.  A few minutes later, the guards were closing in. Player 324 gets shot in the arm, forcing Player 145 to get Park Gyeong-seok to retreat. Park Gyeong-seok was about to see Player 324 commit suicide, to which he pleads the latter not to do so. Player 145 tells Park Gyeong-seok to run down the stairs back to the dormitory, but that option was no longer viable as more guards came from the stairwell which had the broken cameras that Cho Hyun-ju shot. 

Fate[]
Let us go—
Player 145's last words before getting shot in "Friend or Foe"
After Player 324 kills himself, the guards came and surrounded Player 145 and Park Gyeong-seok, and the former tells the latter that if they put down their guns and surrender, the guards might let them live. Unfortunately, that was not the case, as Player 145 was shot dead by a guard after begging to not kill him.

Legacy[]
Player 145 briefly appears in Season 3. His body was forcefully hanged along with those of Players 015, 072, 324, Byeong-su and Jung-bae, along with the manager taken hostage by the rebellion team (wearing the Player 001 tracksuit) to show the consequences of attempting to take down the games.

Trivia[]
He is the first death shown in Season 3.
He is the second to last death in Season 2.
He is also the longest living unnamed character within the Rebellion.
He was mistakenly credited as Player 182 in "Friend or Foe".
Despite Kwon Byeong-su being given a name in the credits, Player 145 was not given the same treatment (possibly due to the error of being credited as 182).
Player 145 can be considered the "X version of Player 203 (Kim Gi-min). This is due to the fact that both were commanding, intimidating, extremely dedicated to their voting team, violent in the name of their team, and with the fact that Kim Gi-min was also a member of 145's team in the Pentathlon. They have the same hairstyle too.
Excluding Kang Dae-ho, Player 145 and Kim Gi-min were the only two players who unzipped their jacket during the argument between Team X and Team O caused by Dae-ho and Yeong-sam. The notable difference was that Gi-min had threw and slammed his jacket onto the floor, while 145 still had his jacket on.
Since he and Kim Gi-min share many similarities, it is possible that Player 145 could have made it to the finals if he did not participate in the Rebellion.
When the camera pans to outside the bathroom door during the fight inside, the door gets jolted as if struck — the commotion was caused by Player 145 slamming Player 299 into it.
He is shown to have the best fighting skills, as he quickly overpowered and beat up 442 despite being surprise attacked, beat 299 to death, and killed a guard by slamming his head into the ground.
It is possible that being on the same Pentathlon team as Gi-min and Seung-won and seeing their callous and cruel personalities up close was a big factor to make him and Player 206 change their votes from 0 to X, seeing the immorality of the hardcore 0s.
Ironically, when the X's and O's were arguing, Player 145 can be seen first yelling at Kim Gi-min before looking at the other O players.
He is the only player in the 100's range in his Pentathlon team. He is also the only player in the 100's range to die in the Rebellion.
During the bathroom fight, 145 attempts to grab Thanos' head. This is possibly a reference to the marvel character's famous line "You should have gone for the head".
He is the only rebel that killed a player in the Bathroom Fight.
The extra that portrays him during Six-Legged Pentathlon also portrays Player 103 in Season 1.
He is the only Player in Kim Gi-min's team to have their SLP actor known
Mistakes[]
In "Friend or Foe", Player 145 is not seen in the credits. The actor is instead misattributed as portraying Player 182. In "Keys and Knives", Player 145 is attributed to be portrayed by Ha Soo-ho rather than Player 182. This error could have something to do with the reason he does not have a confirmed name, as his equally important ally Kwon Byeong-su is given a name through the credits.
The other 145 during Mingle has an O patch, despite wearing an X patch when he is swapped in for the correct actor.
Behind the scenes[]
Player 145 was portrayed by Ha Soo-ho, Moon Yun-hwan and 3 unknown extras.
