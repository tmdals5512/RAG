History[]
Background[]
Ju Ho-yong was born in Gwangmyeong, South Korea as the second son of Ju Byeong-jin and Hong Myung-hwa. As an adult sometime recently, he would get married to Kim Ji-yeon and try to start a family. He enlisted as a Pink Guard in December 2016. In April 2017, he performed well in Soldier training. In July 2020, he participated in extended service through an administrative support role. After his appointment, he was transferred to Namyang, Cheonggye-si.

33rd Squid Game[]
The night before Sugar Honeycombs he was stationed at the bathroom door, when Han Mi-nyeo began yelling that she needed to go. Eventually he conceded, and Kang Sae-byeok went with her.
They would take very long and Ho-yong would yell at them to hurry up, he would barge in and get yelled at by Mi-nyeo. After that, she threatened to sue him.

37th Squid Game[]
Like all of the soldiers, Ho-yong met in a trailer in the Tancheon parking lot in the early morning of November 1, and changed into his soldier uniform before being transported to the island.
It is unknown if Ho-yong participated in quelling the revolt, and if he did, was killed during it or had survived. His guard file would later be shown when Kang No-eul flips through the soldier files for the 37th Squid Game.

Personality[]
Ju Ho-yong was evaluated as sincere and diligent in his work. His record notes involvement in physical training that included the use of force. He demonstrated social consideration and care toward others, showed strong responsiveness to administrative directives, and exhibited coordination skills in difficult circumstances. He was also recognized for his use of nonviolent physical control techniques in protecting fellow soldiers. It is unknown how his personality is working as a soldier in the Squid Games. Whether he enjoyed shooting players or was forced to do it, breaking his use of nonviolent physical techniques, against his own will, is unknown. He was in court for an unknown reason.
