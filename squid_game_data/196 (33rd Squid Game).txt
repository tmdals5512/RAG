History[]
Player 196 was in heavy debt and chose to enter a high-risk competition to win money. After waking up in a large room with 455 other people, he signed a contract and took his photo. He managed to survive the first game, Red Light, Green Light, after most of the contestants had been wiped out. Afterwards, like everyone else, he was shaken by the deaths of the contestants, when the prize money was finally revealed. A vote was held if the game should continue, and the majority voted no. However, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players.
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs. He was recruited by Cho Sang-woo to join their team. In Tug of War his team consist of Oh Il-nam, Kang Sae-byeok, Ji-yeong, Seong Gi-hun, Cho Sang-woo, Han Mi-nyeo, Ali Abdul, Player 244 and Player 276. He had doubts of Oh-nam's plan but follow through to aid his team to victory. Before the fourth game, he complained about his team, saying they turned into the weakest ones, as there were three woman and an old man. Player 244 and Player 196 suggested to attack another weak team. For the fourth game, he chose a partner, but it was revealed that the game was Marbles and that players would face their partners.

Then what? Can we win by talking? You can barely stay standing old man, so shut up and save all your strength!
Player 196 doubting Oh Il-nam while on their way to play in Tug of War
Fate[]
Player 196 lost to his partner, Player 244 in a marble throwing game, and was subsequently eliminated. While his death is not shown, his corpse is seen, with a bullet hole in his temple.

Trivia[]
His best friend in the games, Player 244, passed dalgona at the same time as him.
He is the only member of his tug of war team to die off screen without his elimination announced. His corpse is still seen, however.
Despite his frequent objections of both Sae-byeok and Ji-yeong [ as well as Mi-nyeo] not being strong enough, they each outlived him, with the first two standing over his corpse.
He and Player 244 were partners in Marbles but both of their Season 2 counterparts both died at the first game.
He seems to have t ageist views against Oh Il-nam  in both the elevator before Tug Of War  and  in the discussion over who should be team guard after between Games 3 and 4, though to be fair he has justification to be sceptical of a very elderly man being helpful in a Tug Of War game or going all night without sleep [and he did agree to follow Oh Il-nam's plan in the actual Tug Of War game]. He also shows some misogynistic views against the female members of his team before Marbles even though it is unlikely two games in a row will greatly emphasize physical strength.
He has some resemblance to Player 045 of the second season and both players die in a team game. with a quite outspoken woman [Player 212/Mi-nyeo and Player 254 respectively.
Though uncertain and NOT CONFIRMED it is possible he may be a Christian as he and Player 244 were closest friends in the game on their team and interestingly Player 196 teamed up with him in the Marbles lobby almost immediately after he heard Player 244 reference men and women having very different roles in a somewhat mysogynistic way in Christianity especially because of Adam and Eve.

Behind the scenes[]
Player 196 was portrayed by Kim Dong-hyun. However, in a continuity error, his icon displays a woman instead a man. He is credited as '꼰대남 (kkondaenam)', a saying for an older man who insists on their way being correct, reflecting his attitude throughout the games.
