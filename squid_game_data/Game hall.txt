The stairs, or the main game hall, is a location in Squid Game. It is where the players are transported to the games by ascending the staircases. It was presumably built in 1988, then destroyed on November 7, 2024.

General[]
The game hall is a vast area filled with staircases, balconies, doorways, and windows. These aspects of the game hall are filled with a vibrant mix of three primary pastel colors: pink, blue, and yellow.  
Usually, before the first game of the series, the players are required to take a picture in this area for their icon before proceeding to the stairs.  
The game hall acts as a hub, connecting almost every room in the entire facility, including all the games, incinerator, and guard resting areas. The room also acts as a place to display the consequences of cheating, with Byeong-gi and the players involved in the Revolt having their corpses strung up as an example.
This area also acts as the main game hall by linking various game halls.

History[]
The game hall first appears in the very first episode, used as a way of showing how truly large the place was. The players all proceed to have their photos taken. It is next seen when the Workers are returning to their living space, the pink guards covering every sector. Next, it is seen when Cho Sang-woo asks Kang Sae-byeok about what she saw. The next time it is seen, the final 39 players all bear witness to Byeong-gi and the hanging guards. It has one more relevent appearence in the first season, with the finalists going back to the dorms after completing Glass Stepping Stones, the lights dimmed. 
It next appears in the second season, with several characters, including Seong Gi-hun, Cho Hyun-ju, and Kim Jun-hee seen up close taking their photos. Soon after, Park Jung-bae is seen running to Gi-hun, the two reuniting. It is next seen when a manager takes Kang No-eul to see the officer, and was later briefly seen before the Pentathlon, with Gi-hun's new team. It is next seen before Mingle, with Hyun-ju apologizing to Kim Young-mi for voting to stay. After the third game, the main group whispers about counting the Os and Xs remaining. The next appearance was in the final episode of the second season, the location the entire Revolt took place, and where Player 072 , Player 206, Player 324, and Player 145 all met their ends. 
In the third season, Park Gyeong-seok's unconscious body is seen being put into a coffin by guards. It is later seen in the first episode with the strung up rebels, including Park Jung-bae, Player 015, and Kwon Byeong-su. It is next seen in the third episode, with Gi-hun talking to Jun-hee about how they are not responsible for other's actions. Finally, it is seen in the last episode, the first time being when the nine finalists are being taken to Sky Squid Game. The very last time it is seen is during the countdown before the island explodes. The lights are dark, the bullet holes from the rebellion are seen, and it is all too empty. This final moment shows how truly important this room was not just to the facility, but to the show.

Trivia[]
The stairs are inspired by M.C. Escher's work called "Relativity".
When a new game is about to begin, the speakers play The Blue Danube in this location usually. You can listen to it on YouTube.
In Season 1, sometimes the staircases and walls appeared to be green, while in Season 2 and Season 3, they appeared to be yellow.
This could have been the quality of the filming at the time as sometimes the color was yellow.
Season 1 and Season 3 both showed players being hung on a mobile after breaking the rules.
The player in Season 1 was Player 111, along with a worker, manager, and two soldiers.
The players in Season 3 were Players 015, 047, 072, 145, 206, 324, and 390, along with a manager.
The stairs also makes an appearance in Squid Game: The Challenge, Seasons 1 and 2.
The Starry Night is the only episode to take place in the games that does not feature the gamehall.
