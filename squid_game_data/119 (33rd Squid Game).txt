History[]
No Sang-hun had a debt of ₩1,390,000,000 that he couldn't pay. The Recruiter offered him to play Ddakji to win money and gave him an invitation card to the Squid Games to win more money and pay off his debts, so Sang-hun decided to participate in the games as Player 119 and was picked up by a van during the night.
Sang-hun woke up in a huge dormitory with 455 other players and the Pink Guards arrived. The guards mentioned the debts of some players, among whom was Sang-hun, who was looking at his player number and he got surprised to see the video of him playing with the Recruiter. Sang-hun signed a contract and took his photo. During the first game, Red Light, Green Light, it was revealed that losing players would be killed, causing panic and the deaths of hundreds of players, but Sang-hun managed to follow the rules and was 1 of the 201 players who managed to win the game and survive.
Afterwards, like everyone else, he was shaken by the deaths of the contestants. He first called out the staff by questioning the types of games they play and type of elimination. After a few other players confronted the staff, he also add that the police will be looking for them since hundreds went missing. Thankfully, Cho Sang-woo remembered the third clause which allowed players to vote on the Squid Game's termination. During the voting process, No Sang-hun voted no, and was calling all the people who voted yes monsters. While the majority voted no and the games ended, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players. Most likely due to his debt.   

Fate[]
Such a young kid. How did you end up like this?
Sang-hun to a young manager before committing suicide in "The Man with the Umbrella"
In the second game, he was supposed to cut out the umbrella from his piece of Sugar Honeycombs, but he accidentally broke the dalgona. When a guard went to execute him, No Sang-hun attacked the guard by poking a needle in the guard's eye. Then, he stole the soldier's gun, grabbed a manager, shot him, and held him at gunpoint, threatening to shoot. He demands the guard to remove his mask, which he did. Immediately after, No Sang-hun kills himself by shooting himself in the head due to his disbelief that someone so young could commit such atrocities. The young manager is then shot by the Front Man, reminding that they will be killed if their identities are revealed.

Personality[]
You assholes! Fuck this! What kind of a sick game is this?! Why do some get an easy shape when others are stuck with difficult ones?! Don't come any closer, you assholes! I'll kill this bastard. I'll really kill him!
No Sang-hun asking the guards why the games were so brutal after failing Dalgona in "The Man With The Umbrella"
Sang-hun was a man who values ​​his life over money and was willing to fight for what is morally right. When the bloody nature of the Squid Games was revealed, Sang-hun got dismayed to discover that the losing players would be killed, so he wanted to leave immediately, Sang-hun also scolded the players who wanted to continue with the sick games. Sang-hun was also a brave subject who was willing to save his own life by himself. In Sugar Honeycombs, Sang-hun confronted the Soldier who was going to kill him, took his Smith & Wesson Model 19 and took Manager 007 to confront the Pink Guards, Sang-hun got surprised to see that the Manager was a very young man and got shocked to see someone so young commit such atrocities, which made him feel so devastated that he committed suicide.

Trivia[]
He is one of few named characters to have a unique family name, being 'No'.
He shares a surname with only one other character in the series: No Hyun-woo, who is the winner of the 1st Squid Game competition.
The gun that Sang-hun used to threaten Manager 007 was a Smith & Wesson Model 19, which is ironic since Sang-hun's player number is 119.
He is the first player to die by suicide.
He is also 1 of the 4 people to die from suicide in Season 1, the other 3 are Player 069, Han Mi-nyeo and Cho Sang-woo.
Sang-hun indirectly saved Hwang Jun-ho by causing the execution of the Manager who was supposed to meet him, preventing Jun-ho's identity as an infiltrator from being discovered.
After showing his contempt for the atrocious and sick nature of the Squid Games and having faced off against the Pink Guards. Fans believe that if Sang-hun had participated in the 37th Squid Game, it is very likely that he would have joined Seong Gi-hun and his team in the Rebellion.
In order for his debt to be paid off, Sang-hun would've needed to be among the last 17 players and survive Marbles.
He was the first player to rebel against the guards, excluding the ones who voted O in the voting round.
Though the source of his debt is unknown, given it is very high and he returned to the games despite being perhaps the player who hated them the most, it is possible it is related to medical care for a loved one similar to Gi-hun/Player 246 and Park Gyeong-seok/Player 246.
His Season 2 counterpart is Kwon Byeong-su (Player 047) since both players valued human life over financial gain, but also understandably having a quick temper with people who commit inhuman actions.
His BTS Actor also portrays Player 379 in Season 2.
He is the only known player to break his dalgona after carving it.
He and Player 296 of the second and third season are the only players to defend themselves after losing a game: they actually both did quite well with Sang-hun overpowering two guards, taking one of them hostage and stealing a gun while Player 296 very nearly killed VIP Richard [then disguised as one of the Pink Guards] and if he had, he could have taken a gun or even disguised himself as one of the guards and maybe escaped that way. Player 278 in the first season also tried to escape after losing the Marbles game but despite making some progress the guards still killed him too.
Mistakes[]
No Sang-hun's icon disappears too early during the distract in Sugar Honeycombs, but he last died in Sugar Honeycombs.
His icon shows as a different man with glasses who is portrayed by an unknown extra.
A female Player 119 can be seen in Red Light, Green Light.
When his corpse is seen when Hwang In-ho kills Manager 007, his player number appears to be mirrored backwards, though this could be to show that In-ho is left-handed.
