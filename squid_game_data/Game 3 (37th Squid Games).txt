Mingle (짝짓기 게임 Jjagjisgi Geim?) was the third game of the 37th Squid Game. It has 50 rooms with 10 different colors, which are: Red, Orange, Yellow, Lime, Green, Teal, Cyan, Blue, Indigo and Purple. Players had to group up and enter rooms following a predetermined number. If players don't manage to make it into the rooms in time or if a room does not contain a required number, they will be eliminated.
Throughout the game, players become desperate to form teams and reach a room before time runs out, leading to players teaming up and betraying each other to survive. In this game, all physical violence is allowed, so players can grab, push and even kill each other.
255 players participated in this game. 155 players (around 60.8%) were eliminated and 100 players (around 39.2%) progressed to the Dormitory before the interlude rounds (The Bathroom Fight, The Special Game, and The Rebellion).
At this point in the competition, 356 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include: Player 023, Player 043, Player 050, Player 073, Kim Young-mi (Player 095), Player 238, Player 249, Gyeong-su (Player 256), Player 285, Player 313, Player 328, Sim Jae-seok (Player 343), Player 419 and Player 428.
This game took place on November 3, 2024.

Game information
The game you will be playing is Mingle. All players, please step onto the center platform. When the game starts, the platform will begin to rotate, and you will hear a number. You must form groups of that size, go into the rooms, and close the door within 30 seconds.
The game explanation in "One More Game"
     Players enter the game.  
All players are required to step onto the platform in the center of the arena. Once the game begins, the platform will spin accompanied by the song "Round and Round". Shortly after, the platform and music will stop and a number will be called out. Players will have 30 seconds to form groups according to the announced number, enter a room with that number of players and close the door.
All doors will automatically lock once 30 seconds have passed. Players that fail to enter a room within the time limit will be eliminated, and shot by the soldiers, as will any players in a room that does not contain the required number.

     Players running to the doors when the number is 2.  
     Ditto, but in gif.  
The game took place in a circular arena with a central platform that resembles a carousel, featuring horse sculptures. The arena is surrounded by a series of colorful, numbers doors, evenly spaced along the walls, which are decorated with lights, bows and intricate patterns, giving a carnival-like atmosphere. To ensure fairness and integrity, the rooms contain cameras allowing the square guards in a control room to verify and confirm that the room contains the required number of people. After the number is called out, the lights go darker and flick faster, warning the players to go to the rooms immediately with the required number of people.

Trivia
The number of players participating in this game is the same amount as the number of players eliminated in Red Light, Green Light during Season 1 and it's about 56 percent of the original 456 players.
It is impossible for all players to win in this game.
For why it's impossible is because if countless people were grouped into a specific number, and the rooms are occupied with the same number, and the rest of the players will have no option, similar to the last round of the game.
This happens in all the rounds, where at least 5 players would be eliminated in Round 1, 20 in Round 2, 18 in Round 3 and 3 in Round 4.
This is the only game in the series where a fixed maximum amount of survivors is set due to the last number called. No more than 100 players could have survived, no matter how many players played it.
The death count in the second and final round seems to have multiple errors, as they exceed the supposed death count for those rounds, respectively. In fact, many players who were already eliminated in previous rounds appeared again later after their death rounds as well as eliminated Red Light, Green Light players to substitute other major/supporting characters who were unavailable for shooting, so that the number of people standing on the carousel each round stays intact even without these characters. An example of this is Player 251 and Player 279 who appeared to be thrown out of their room by Nam-gyu and Thanos even though both of them had already canonically died in the first and second round.
It's possible that this was supposed to be the second game before Gi Hun entered the games again, because after the first game about 200 players were supposed to pass, and after the second only about 140 players would have passed, meaning that only 40 players would have been eliminated in this game. If we swap the second and the third game, we got this: 200~ players after red light green light, half eliminated during mingle, 100 reaming , and around 70 after pentathlon, without counting lights out, so about 50 remaining. With this theory the plot doesn't change to much: during mingle players create teams, during pentathlon the relationships between players strengthen up, and then during hide and seek they have to betray eachother. Also, in this way, the amount of deaths after every game is similar to the one of the 33rd squid games.
Fans speculate that Hwang In-ho didn't actually find a team after splitting up in Round 2 and just used his position as the Front Man in order not to be eliminated.
However, if you count the number of players standing on the spinning platform during round 3, there are also exactly 168 players, a multiple of four. This is possibly the proof that In-ho played that round for real.
It is the only game in which teams must form up as soon as it starts and not before the game begins.
Beaten by Red Light, Green Light from Season 1, it is the second game with the highest number of deaths in the entire series, with 155 players eliminated and it has the highest amount of a non Season-1 game.
The room where Hwang In-ho kills Sim Jae-seok is the same room where Player 313, Player 050 and Player 238 were killed by a soldier for failing to bring the required amount of people in a room.
With 155 eliminations, this was the game that eliminated the most players in the 37th squid game.
According to fan theories, the purpose of the Mingle game, which takes place after the Six-Legged Pentathlon, is to force the groups that were previously formed to betray and eliminate each other, making the game more challenging and intense.
After 30 seconds is up all doors are closed automatically unlike Fort Boyard where Paspartu manually closes, only a few rooms autolocks as result of mistakes (there are more rooms with traps where player can't leave)
There is also another easter egg in this game. In the final round of this game, when players are instructed to form a group of two. By then, Hwang In-ho paired up with Park Jung-bae, Jang Geum-ja paired up with Park Yong-sik and Seong Gi-hun paired up with Kang Dae-ho. It is a foreshadowing reference by the creator by whom they are going to kill at the end of the season (e.g. Jang Geum-ja almost killed Park Yong-sik in 'The Starry Night', Seon-nyeo betrayed her team including Mi-hwa resulting in their death and the other characters killed their partner).
There seems to be an unwritten rule where if the number of players in a room exceeds the announced number, then they can kill other players in the room until the number of players in the room is right before the time runs out, because players who die in the room will not be counted as players (meaning they are eliminated). But even if that was the case, In-ho wouldn't have been shot.
Of the 255 players, only 2 sets of 3 consecutive numbers (306-308 and 348-350) managed to pass the game.
Neither of those 2 trios managed to be complete for Hide and Seek.
You have to be very careful playing this game, especially after the first round, because eliminating players makes the floor of the game area full of puddles of blood and makes it prone to slipping, especially since this game really relies on speed.
In this game, a team can be at a huge disadvantage if one of its members is left outside the room because not only is the member left behind eliminated, but the entire team is also eliminated due to the lack of members (as happened to Young-mi; the team was saved by Myung-gi). Therefore, it is important to look out for each other among team members.
Mingle had the third highest death rates out of all the games, at 61%, behind Glass Stepping Stones and Sky Squid Game.
This is the third game of the 2024 Squid Game: compared to the third game of 2020 Squid Game [Tug of War],  more then three times as many players entered Mingle [255] compared to in Tug of War [80].
Round two saw the most eliminations, at 52, while round four saw the least, at 15.
Past the first round, of the four players who regrettably/reluctantly voted to continue, each has a moment of regret.
Min-su voted to continue, and would see his teammates abandon Gyeong-su in round two, resulting in his death.
Yong-sik voted to continue, and would be stripped from his mother in round three, almost getting her killed.
Hyun-ju voted to continue, and would lose Young-mi in round four.
Jung-bae voted to continue, and would watch In-ho kill Jae-seok in the final round.
There was once a vague rumour that director Hwang Dong-hyuk considered using this, Mingle, as a game in Squid Game season 1 but a source for this can not be found. If it had been a Season One game-again, the source for this can not be found presently- the likelihood is it would have replaced Dalgona because Red Light, Green Light is too iconic and works very well as the beginning game while the third-sixth games are too essential to the plot and in some cases character development to be altered [this particularly goes for Marbles and Squid Game]. Additionally with 187 players entering the Dalgona game it is the closest number of players entering a game to the 255 players entering Mingle in Squid Game 2. In a very, very, very different version of the game where there were less rooms, somewhere from two to five,  it is possible it could have been used instead of the Glass Bridge game though it is likely the physically fastest and strongest players like Player 062 and Jang Deok-su would have performed the best in that game and the finalists could have been very different.
This and Six-Legged Pentathlon are the only games in the series where a higher number of players die than in the previous game.
It is the only game of the 2024 edition where it is not known which player died first [in the other games it is Player 196/Kang Mi-na in Red Light, Green Light, Player 045 in the Six-Legged Pentathlon, Player 310 in Hide and Seek, Player 124/Nam-gyu in Jump Rope and Player 125/Park Min-su in Sky Squid Game].
At the beginning of the second round, Gyeong-seok can be seen on the carrousel with Dong-uk to the right of him. Coincidentally, their numbers are next to each other, 246 and 247.
