Finalists of the 28th Squid Game  
     Finalists during 33rd Squid Game.  
     Finalists during 37th Squid Game.  The Finalists are the Squid Games' Players who pass and survive the first five games. Every finalist receives an identical black tuxedo with individual number and vote patches, replacing their previous standard player tracksuits. Game staff such as Managers and The Announcer usually refer to finalists by their numbers. (e.g. Player 456, Player 333, Player 125, Player 218, Player 100.)
In the 37th Squid Game, all finalists must vote for the continuation or cessation of the games before the next game. If the majority votes for continuation, the 6th game will take place, but if the majority votes for cessation, the game will stop, and the accumulated prize money will be divided among all finalists. 
All the remaining finalists would eat a luxurious final dinner the night before the final game.

If he'd keep his emotions cool and control his footing, he might have passed the game. And since Player 448 was in front of him, she would pass too.

Since Lee Myung-gi pressured him to go, it would be quite difficult for him to keep his focus and fear intact but he still could've passed the game if he jumped a bit further.

But she could've passed if Player 096 didn't block the way since she was maintaining perfect balance alongside Player 377, and would pass the game with little to no struggle. Not to mention the fact that there was a lot of space for her to move after Player 192 and Player 442 fell, meaning she could go near the gap, wait for Player 096 to be stopped by Gi-hun, and jump across the gap and win.
Player 377 - He was doing great on the bridge, maintaining balance and gripping his hips for support. But as Player 349 insisted he'd turn around, he lost his footing. While trying to regain it, he couldn't react and jump in time which led to him being swung away by the rope and fall. He could have made it to the other side with little to no struggle if Player 096 wasn't causing an interlude and if Player 349 had passed too as she was right in front of him, and she also maintained perfect balance. Meaning, both Player 349 and Player 377 would pass the game. Not to mention the fact that there was a lot of space for her to move after Player 192 and Player 442 fell, meaning he could follow behind Player 349, wait for Player 096 to be stopped by Gi-hun, and jump across the gap and win alongside Player 349.
Player 435 - As he made it to the second part of the bridge, being in the middle of Kim Yun-tae, Player 039, and Kim Gi-min, Player 096 suddenly lunged at Seong Gi-hun and tackled him. Meaning, the players would have to wait extra time to get to the other side. Player 435 misses his one leg when jumping and the rope hits it, causing him to lose balance immediately and fall to his death. He could've passed if he had stabilized his jumps and waited longer so that he can pass with Kim Yun-tae right in front of him.
Player 448 - She would've made it if she stabilized herself, as she gasped in fear when she saw the gap in the bridge which led to a distraction causing her to get flung by the rope. If she could've waited longer and stabilized her jumps and calmed herself, she would make it as she was behind Kim Gi-min.
Trivia
Every player who passes the 5th game or reaches the 6th, becomes a finalist and recieves a tuxedo with their player number. If the finalist wins the final game, which is usually squid game or a variation of that game, they will receive the full prize money.
Kang Sae-byeok, Player ?53, Player ??9, Player 3??, Player ??? and Player ??? are the only known finalists who did not make it to the sixth game.
Seong Gi-hun was the only player to be a finalist twice, and was extremely close to being the first double-winner for the Squid Games, but he commits suicide and sacrifices himself for Kim Jun-hee's baby.
As far as one knows, the 37th Squid Game has the largest amount of finalists, that being nine.
Out of the three contests shown in the series (28th, 33rd, 37th), the 33rd Squid Game has the least amount of finalists, that being three.
If we consider 6 as the usual number of finalists, About 210 - 240 players ever made it to the final game. This would also mean that about the 50% of the player numbers (001-456) ever appeared on an elegant tuxedo.
This only counts the Korean version of the Squid Games as counting the others international games means that the number of finalists around the world could be in the  thousands.
The 33rd Squid Game is the only contest to all have named finalists.
The number 456 is the only number that is known to be a finalist's number twice.
The 28th Squid Games and 11th Squid Games are the only known editions with an unspecified amount of finalists, 6+ for the 28th edition and 3+ for the 11th edition.
Some finalists share their names with other players that participated in other annual Squid Game competitions. Of them, the only one that isn't erroneous is Kim Yun-tae, that refers to 2 players. Player 320 of the 16th Squid Game and Player 353 of the 37th Squid Game.
Player ?53, Sang-woo (218) and Gi-hun (456) are the last finalists to die in their respective games, 28th, 33rd and 37th.
Every known finalist from the 33rd and 37th squid games has had at least one kill or an indirect kill.
Depending on each finalist is what the shape of the dinner table is, if its 3, its a triangle, 4, its a square, 5 or more, a circle.
Excluding Im Jeong-dae and Kim Jun-hee's baby, all finalists of the 37th Squid Game were Seekers during Hide-and-Seek.
Hwang In-ho had the highest kill counts out of all known finalists.
In the 37th Squid Game, Kim Nam-du, Kim Yeong-sam, Lee Eun-ju, Park Mi-hwa, and Sim Jae-seok would have all needed to be finalists in order for their share of the prize money to cover their debts.
Of the 47 finalists for whom there is enough information to deduce their gender, only 7 of them were women (Cho Su-jin, Yun Ji-eun, Son Jeong-hee, Park Mi-kyung, Seo Min-ju, Kang Sae-byeok and Jun-hee's baby) which gives a 14.9% probability of a female finalist, that is, around 1 out of every 7 finalists.
With the 33rd finalists, their numbers are a pattern of skipping the even 100s. 067, 218, 456.
It is likely that the games are structured to ensure a very low number of finalists out of the total 456 players as the VIPs would want the last and most ''epic'' game to be focused and intimate with only a few very successful players, and the finalists would not seem as impressive compared to the others if fifty of them or a figure like that made it to the last game. Hence it is likely, between the games being difficult themselves and possible influences like the Special Game and tensions between the players that it is unlikely there could ever be more then around 20 finalists at most for the sixth game [and even a figure that high would only be reached very rarely].
Player 096 in the first season was very close to likely becoming a finalist until he asked Gi-hun if he could take the number 1 jersey in Glass Stepping Stones.
