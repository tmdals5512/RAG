Hwang In-ho (황인호 Hwang In-ho?, born February 2, 1976), also known as the Front Man (프론트 맨 Puelonteu Maen?), is the main antagonist of Squid Game. He is the current head staff member and the person who oversees the Squid Games. He is the brother of Hwang Jun-ho and became the Front Man after participating in and winning the 28th Squid Game in 2015 as Player 132. In 2020, his brother infiltrated the 33rd Squid Game in an attempt to find him after he went missing. 
The Front Man wields absolute control over all three classes of guards and observes the survival competition games through several monitoring screens as the games unfold. He sometimes steps in when a situation gets out of control during the games, such as when the identity of a staff member is somehow revealed (which is forbidden in the games), in which case the Front Man storms in and executes the exposed staffer.
The Front Man also is often seen answering phone calls back at his quarters, communicating with the person on the other line about the ongoing competition. In the games, this is usually the Host, whose orders he carries out. He also ensures that the VIPs have a good time while watching the game.
Following Oh Il-nam's passing, Hwang In-ho succeeds him and becomes the current Host of the Squid Games.
While participating in the 37th Squid Game as Player 001, In-ho used the alias Oh Young-il (오영일 O Yeong-il?).

Personality
Player 456. Did you have fun playing the hero? Now, witness the consequences of your little game.https://squid-game.fandom.com/wiki/File:Frontmanquote.mp3
Hwang In-ho to a Seong Gi-hun before executing Park Jung-bae in "Friend or Foe"
     In-ho masked as the "Front man"  
As the Front Man, In-ho is cold and calculating, being the main overseer of the games. He is shown to be remorseless with his killing, showing no sign of hesitation when killing others who get in his way. He is highly analytical and meticulous with his anonymity as well as his planning, ensuring that the games are hidden from the outside world. Despite his overall lack of morality, however, In-ho places a lot of importance on maintaining equality and fairness in the games. He views it as one of the games' most important aspects and is incredibly strict with it to the point of going as far as killing those who take advantage of the games' nature to get ahead.
Under the alias Oh Young-il, he disguised himself as a player of the games, and presented himself as level-headed, logical, and reasonable. He appeared highly empathetic and caring of others, such as when he aided Kim Jun-hee upon hearing that she is pregnant, even offering her his milk during mealtime, and saved Jang Geum-ja alongside Seong Gi-hun during Mingle. He also stood up for Lee Myung-gi against Thanos and Nam-gyu. He appeared to have a humorous side to him as he had a habit of making bad jokes, although this may simply be an attempt to try to fit in more with others. Additionally, despite knowing that killing is allowed, he still had the restraint to spare Thanos when he begged for mercy. This suggests that while he is still a remorseless person at his core, he does not completely lack a sense of humanity as he still harbors some care towards other people. However, his overall lack of empathy towards others leads him to lack understanding of Gi-hun's own empathy towards others. As a result of this, he frequently observes Gi-hun's behavior towards others throughout the games, which he finds himself both puzzled and intrigued by. Despite this, he can be judgmental about Gi-hun's actions, shown when he smirked at the thought that Gi-hun was willing to sacrifice others in order to launch the rebellion as it contradicted his initial ideals of saving as many of the players as he could.     
However, it is suggested that In-ho was more empathetic in the past and was capable of loving others as he stated that his main reason for joining the games was to save both his wife and unborn child who were both at risk of dying. In addition to this, In-ho is shown to still harbor some care for his brother Jun-ho, as he gave him a non-fatal gunshot wound on his right shoulder instead of killing him, after which he was rescued and brought back to shore, presumably by a fisherman that In-ho sent to save him. He also appears to hold genuine respect for his master Oh Il-nam, ending the Special Round at his request and visiting his deathbed to pay his respects after the latter passes away.  
In-ho's extreme intelligence has been showcased on many occasions, most notably in Season Two. Apart from his behavior during the games, he was able to keep track of Gi-hun who had been searching for him for the past two years without him knowing, and was thus able to capture him which led him to rejoin the games. With his wits, he aided Gi-hun and his team through the second and third games under his temporary alias. However, he was shown to be emotionally manipulative, taking advantage of Gi-hun's ignorance by opening up to him about his reason for joining the games, as well as breaking down during the second game at the thought of losing to gain his trust and get closer to him, just to betray him in the end. His extreme caution comes as a result of his natural intelligence, and he was thus able to betray the team without Gi-hun realizing. He is also shown to have purposefully sabotaged the games, such as when he casted a vote to continue the games after the first game, and when he purposefully failed at the spinning top game in the second game multiple times to slow the team down and put them, especially Gi-hun under pressure. While he outwardly presented himself as a completely different person, In-ho still maintained some of his core traits, namely his ability to kill others without remorse, shown when he killed another player in the third game to save Park Jung-bae.

Legacy
Front Man may seem like the embodiment of absolute evil in the story, having lost all hope in humanity and the world, but after going through all those ups and downs with Gi-hun, maybe he did have a change of heart, that there perhaps may be some hope left after all. That’s how I interpreted the character and portrayed him. In the very final part of the series, he goes to find Gi-hun’s daughter to leave her with Gi-hun’s tracksuit as a keepsake along with his prize money. He also chooses his brother Jun-ho—the only family he deeply trusts—to take care of Jun-hee’s baby. His decisions, I believe, showcase that while he tries to maintain his cold, he definitely has experienced some change of heart.
Lee Byung-hun on In-ho’s change of heart 
While not stopping the games and remaining loyal to the VIPs until the very end, In-ho does commit a few less-evil actions in the series finale. After watching Seong Gi-hun sacrifice himself and cling to his humanity until the end, In-ho, as confirmed by the actor, experiences a small change of heart. This leads him to save Kim Jun-hee’s baby and not remain indifferent toward her, as she and Jun-hee also reminded him of his dead wife and baby, which are interesting details that the actor hopes the audience noticed.
In-ho is last seen in Los Angeles, where he accidentally spots an American recruiter from his car and exchanges strange looks with her, which might suggest they already know each other. However, according to Lee Byung-hun, that doesn’t seem to be the case:

When I asked director Hwang before filming that scene to double-check, he confirmed that In-ho was also unaware that the game was being held elsewhere. So if you look closely enough, In-ho seems both surprised and bitter.
Lee Byung-hun on In-ho’s final scene 
Despite this confirmation, it’s still true that in Season 1, the VIPs clearly told In-ho that the games in Korea were the best, which implied there were games in other countries too.

Relationships
His wife and child
In-ho’s late wife and unborn child were central figures in shaping his life, unintentionally playing a role in turning him into the criminal he became: the Front Man.
In-ho and his wife were married prior to 2015 and apparently had a good marriage until In-ho’s wife was diagnosed with acute liver cirrhosis. Things only got worse when it became clear that she was also pregnant, something that could seriously endanger her life. According to In-ho, his wife was a stubborn woman, and he could never change her mind when they disagreed. Eventually, In-ho decided to do everything he could to save both her and their baby.
When one of In-ho’s longtime vendors heard about their situation and offered help, it was interpreted as bribery, which caused In-ho to be fired from his job. Although In-ho later claimed in a discussion with Seong Gi-hun that what he did wasn’t bribery, both his stepmother and half-brother saw it that way. However, it is unclear what In-ho’s wife herself thought of it, or whether she ever even learned about it.
Desperate, In-ho was invited to play the Squid Games. With saving his wife and baby in mind, he decided to do whatever it took to win, abandoning all his morals as a decades-long police officer. It is currently unclear when exactly it happened, but In-ho was unable to save his wife, as she passed away from her illness. Given the many parallels between In-ho and Seong Gi-hun, it is possible that In-ho, too, came back too late, and that his wife had already died before he could do anything with the prize money.
Her death, along with witnessing the atrocities of the games, made it impossible for In-ho to move on and return to his old life. He couldn’t leave, and instead chose to stay with the games, this time as Oh Il-nam’s employee, helping him run more games.
As the Front Man, In-ho is hardly the same person he was before, even when it comes to his wife. He doesn’t seem to visit her grave anymore. Even so, he is still clearly traumatized by losing her and their baby. He was easily triggered by Thanos mentioning his kids, which led him to lose his temper, beat Thanos, and risk blowing his cover. He also talked about his family with Gi-hun with tears in his eyes, showing how much he still suffers when remembering them.

Hwang Jun-ho
In-ho and Jun-ho have a complicated relationship. As half-brothers, Jun-ho looked up to In-ho from an early age and decided to become a police officer just like him. In-ho loved his younger brother so much that he even gave him one of his kidneys to save his life. Their relationship became less warm after In-ho’s wife got sick and In-ho allegedly accepted bribes, something Jun-ho still believes to this day, and which led to In-ho being fired from his job. Unbeknownst to Jun-ho, this eventually resulted in In-ho participating in the Squid Games and later working for the organization.
Despite not being as close anymore, In-ho managed to maintain a relatively normal relationship with Jun-ho and his stepmother for five more years after winning the 2015 Squid Games. At first, Jun-ho did not worry much when In-ho stopped answering his calls, assuring their mother that he always dodged them. It was only after searching In-ho’s house and finding a Squid Game card that Jun-ho finally began searching for his missing brother.
Jun-ho infiltrated the island and risked his life to find In-ho, who he even believed had been killed there at one point. Even so, Jun-ho and In-ho eventually confronted each other as enemies, with Jun-ho realizing that his missing older brother was actually the leader of the guards and a criminal. Although In-ho shot Jun-ho, he also had someone save him afterward, which suggests that he still cared about his little brother.
Jun-ho and In-ho remain enemies for the rest of the series. However, Jun-ho keeps In-ho’s true identity a secret from everyone else, indirectly protecting him. They meet again in the final season shortly before the island explodes. The meeting gives Jun-ho no answers, as In-ho once again stays silent and refuses to respond to his brother’s only question: “why?”
Six months later, In-ho mysteriously leaves Kim Jun-hee’s baby at Jun-ho’s house, suggesting that he may have finally accepted that he shouldn’t keep Jun-ho completely out of his life, and that he can trust him to take care of an orphaned child.

Oh Il-nam
In-ho first met Oh Il-nam when he was a participant in the 28th Squid Games. While not much is currently known about his reasons, Il-nam decided to help In-ho by offering him a knife to protect himself against the remaining finalist players. In-ho ultimately won the games and later worked for Il-nam as the Front Man, indicating a mutual trust between the two.
In 2021, Il-nam passed away due to his brain tumor, with In-ho closing his eyes and being the only person shown visiting and taking care of him.
In-ho strongly believed in Il-nam’s nihilistic worldview, sharing his negative views on humanity and opposing Seong Gi-hun’s ideals in a similar way. The parallels go even deeper, as in the 2024 Squid Games, In-ho used the name “Oh Young-il,” which resembles Il-nam’s name in more than one way, and he also took the number 001, the same number Il-nam had in 2020. After being praised by the VIPs for doing a top-notch job as the new Host, In-ho humbly said that he was simply upholding Il-nam’s wishes.

Seong Gi-hun
As Player 456, In-ho observed Gi-hun throughout the 2020 Squid Games, but their first actual meeting happened after Gi-hun won, when In-ho met him in a limousine with Gi-hun’s eyes closed. Though brief, this conversation became one of the main reasons Gi-hun began to despise the games, as it showed how the VIPs saw the players as mere “horses” and enjoyed betting on them.
After teaming up with Hwang Jun-ho and deciding to stop the games once and for all, Gi-hun’s main goal was to meet the Front Man again, either to convince him to stop or to capture him, but he ultimately failed at both. However, Gi-hun managed to convince In-ho to let him participate again, hoping that Jun-ho and the others would track him and find the island. Unbeknownst to Gi-hun, In-ho realized his true plan and shut it down before it could work.
As important as their first limousine meeting was to Gi-hun, their second meeting was just as important to In-ho. He accepted Gi-hun’s challenge and allowed him to try and prove that humans are not as hopeless as he and his “masters” believe. In-ho even added a new rule, allowing players to vote after each game and leave with money if the majority wanted to, intending to prove to Gi-hun that even with that advantage, people would still become greedy and choose wrong.
Their rivalry could have ended there, but after watching Gi-hun help and organize other players during Red Light, Green Light, In-ho was encouraged to enter the games personally, challenging Gi-hun directly and keeping an even closer eye on him.
As “Oh Young-il,” In-ho grew relatively close to Gi-hun, becoming someone Gi-hun could rely on. He shared his true life story, although he used the present tense instead of the past to manipulate Gi-hun, and did his best to appear trustworthy and comforting. Gi-hun, unaware of who Young-il really was, cared deeply about him, tried to save him more than once, and reacted devastatedly to his “death.”
After faking his death and returning as the Front Man, In-ho continued to observe Gi-hun, even preventing him from taking his own life during the Hide and Seek game. They finally meet again before the final game, when In-ho reveals his face to Gi-hun. Despite giving Gi-hun the chance to attack and kill him, Gi-hun chooses not to, knowing that In-ho’s death would not change anything.
Gi-hun also receives a knife to kill the other players in their sleep, but he refuses. This choice is the opposite of what In-ho did long ago in his own games, and it makes In-ho tearfully realize that Gi-hun will never lose his humanity the way he did.
Watching Gi-hun’s sacrifice, In-ho finally respects him enough to let him take his own life for the sake of his ideals. In-ho says nothing, but it is implied that he understands he has lost his bet with Gi-hun, just like Il-nam did. Six months later, In-ho visits Gi-hun’s daughter in Los Angeles, gives her Gi-hun’s belongings, and informs her of his death out of respect, with a shameful look on his face.
In-ho and Gi-hun’s rivalry is the main dynamic of the Squid Game series as a whole, mainly in Seasons 2 and 3. Their feud is more of a clash of ideals, as both of them want to prove the other wrong when it comes to their views on humanity. This is especially true on In-ho’s side; he could’ve killed Gi-hun many times, but he chose not to, instead giving him the chance to actually prove his points. As a fellow winner, In-ho is interested in Gi-hun’s choices, tries to corrupt him, and feels inferior to him, but at the same time, a small part of him secretly keeps rooting for him throughout the story, hoping he won’t lose his humanity.

Trivia
According to his player file, Hwang In-ho's resident registration number is 760202-1171651 and he lived in 37-124 Chunghyeon-dong, Seodaemun-gu, Seoul at the time of his participation in the 28th Squid Game.
Curiously, his player file makes no mention of his wife.
In-ho's 2015 player image from Season 1 was not actually taken for Squid Game; it is an edited image of Lee Byung-hun's character, Kim Hyun-jun, from the 2009 Korean series Iris.
Hwang In-ho and the subplot with Jun-ho were not in the original script. They were only added after the decision for Squid Game to be a series instead of a movie.
Lee Byung-hun, the actor who portrays Hwang In-ho, is mentioned in the series by Ji-yeong in "Gganbu" when referring to the movie Inside Men.
Lee Byung-hun playing Front Man was strictly kept a secret to make his reveal a huge surprise for the Korean audience. He wasn’t credited in early Squid Game promotional materials or cast lists to avoid spoiling it. The director and production team instructed the actor to keep his role a secret—apparently, even his own mother did not know. O Yeong-su, Oh Il-nam’s actor, was reportedly also surprised when he first met him on set.
While mentioning the plot lines he would like to explore in season two, Hwang Dong-hyuk stated that one of them would be the story of the Front Man and his brother.[5]
In-ho always puts the transmitter on the right side of the telephone and puts the receiver on the left.[6]
In-ho is shown to be left handed, as he holds and shoots his gun with his left hand. This is further reinforced in Six-Legged Pentathlon game of Season 2, where In-ho fails three times to keep the spinning top spinning with his right hand but is immediately successful when he does so with his left hand.
He carries a Colt M1911 pistol with nickel finish and untracable .45 ACP rounds.
He, Seong Gi-hun, and Cho Sang-woo are the only characters that appeared in all nine episodes of Season One.
Combining all three seasons, he and Seong Gi-hun are the only characters who have appeared—either in present time, flashback, or dream—in all twenty two episodes of the show.
He along with Gi-hun are the only winners known to have later returned to the games as a player.
He donated one of his kidneys to Jun-ho.[2]
In-ho is lactose intolerant.[7]
He serves as the foil to Gi-hun as he is what Gi-hun could have potentially become if he gives in to his anger and grievance.
He is the only character in the series with two episodes named after him ("Front Man" and "001") and one of five characters or groups to have at least one episode named after them, alongside Seong Gi-hun, Oh Il-nam, Kim Jun-hee, and the VIPs as a whole.
Behind the scenes
Hwang In-ho was portrayed by Lee Byung-hun. While wearing his mask, he was portrayed by Kwon Yu-Jun. In the English dub, he was voiced by Tom Choi.
