Personality
He is seen as a carefree and sensible person as he listened to the manager, made silly faces, stood still while everyone panicked and persumbly passed through mutiple games.

History
Mun Jang-ho was born to his father Mun Dae-hee and his unnamed mother on July 14, 1966. He did not formally attend any high school or university, and would start working in 1998, he worked as a marine engineer aboard a Korean merchant ship. Unfortunately, due to abuse of rights and drinking, he became unemployed on March 1, 2018, after 20 years of work.[2] He was unable to find a new job and later decided to participate in the Squid Games, becoming the first player to join.
Mun Jang-ho can be seen listening to the guards with a doubtful expression before the first game. Mun Jang-ho survived the first game, Red Light, Green Light, and shielded Player 196 from running away in the chaos. He would be able to then survive the first game, and cross the finish line. After this, he could be seen agreeing with Player 066 when he confronted the Guards and said that the authorities could track them using their phones. When a vote was held on whether or not to end the games, Mun Jang-ho voted to terminate the games. And would be sent back home after the voting.

Fate
It's unknown what happened to Mun Jang-ho. Despite being shot in Marbles, along with his icon fading out and his elimination being announced there, he was last seen in the Voting. Despite voting O, he was seen cheering so he could be a Supervised player and be alive. there's also a chance he returned to the games. His actor was also seen in talking to some Players after returning to the games.

Trivia
Mun Jang-ho shares his name with Player 129, a man who competed in and won the 2nd Squid Game.
During the panic in Red Light, Green Light, Jang-ho is seen protecting and shielding a nearby player.
According to his player file, Mun Jang-ho's resident registration number is 660714-1156321. He resided in Cheonghak-dong, Yeongdo-gu, Busan.
The archives found by Hwang Jun-ho start with him and not with Player 001, a foreshadowing toward the latter's true identity as the Host of the games.

Behind the scenes
Mun Jang-ho was portrayed by Lee Sang-ha, Jeong Seong-tae, Kwak Jang-ryun in his icon, and two unknown extras. Namuwiki initially listed his actor as Kim Tae-yong, but it is unconfirmed and was later removed due to lack of evidence.[2]
