History[]
Kim In-hyeok was born on April 21, 1979. He began attending Poongsan Commercial High School in March 1999 and graduated, holding two different jobs over the course of his adult life, the first being unknown; while the second was as a delivery driver.[4] He eventually ended up in heavy debt and chose to enter a Squid Games in order to pay it off in 2020. He was taken to the island where the game took place and entered the first game, Red Light, Green Light. As the games started, he and like everyone else, was oblivious about the snipers. 

Fate[]
Because of the deaths of Player 324 and 250 in Red Light, Green Light, Player 455 panicked and tried to escape through the doors along with Player 155 and Player 366, who were both shot running next to him. He then knocked over Player 415 and continued running towards the doors until he was shot in the head by a sniper and killed.

Trivia[]
Kim In-hyeok was the first named player in the 33rd Squid Game shown being eliminated.
His Season 2 counterpart also died in the first game and in a panic.
He shares his last name with Jo Su-jin's father, Jo In-hyeok.
He shares his icon with Player 108 and Player 366.
Additionally, he shares an icon with the man he was running with.
His glasses fell off while he was running, this led to many people believing he was seen before the games, though this is unlikely due to a stuntman probably not being at filming for something like that, plus the lack of glasses.
He is the only 2020 player in the four hundreds to not have his 2024 counterpart be named.
Mistakes[]
In "A Fair World", Kim In-hyeok's photo is significantly different from his initial appearance and is identical to that of Player 397's icon and canonical form, as well as Player 097's and Player 217's icons.
Behind the scenes[]
Kim In-hyeok is portrayed by Tak Hwan-ui canonically, and Tae Jun-ho in his file.
