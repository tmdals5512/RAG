Park Yeong-gil (박영길 Bag Yeong-gil?) also known as Sea Captain Park, was a boat captain who participated in Hwang Jun-ho's search for the island where the annual Squid Game competitions were held and later assisted the team of mercenaries sent to find Seong Gi-hun after his reentry into the games.
Park was secretly also an informant for the overseers of the Squid Games, and pretended to be a fisherman and shrimp boat captain as cover. He was ordered by Hwang In-ho to rescue Hwang Jun-ho and to keep an eye on him.

History[]

Oh, shit! Get out of there! He knows I know! They told him I broke into his house! Hwang, get out! Watch your back! Don't trust him!
Choi Woo-seok immediately warning Hwang Jun-ho about Captain Park 
Virtually nothing is knowing about Park prior to him "rescuing" Jun-ho (if Park Yeong-gil was even his true name or just an alias). He likely worked for the Squid Games for a while prior to this, as he had climbed the ranks all the way up to Manager as of the 37th Squid Game in 2024. His address was 913-22 Haeun-ro, Mujin-si, Jeollanam-do, although he probably only lived here periodically, since his house was cluttered, dirty and covered in cobwebs. He had no family living with him although he did have a dog that was chained up to a kennel in his garden. He was relatively close with some of the higher-ups in the games as in his house Choi Woo-seok found several photos of him with Hwang In-ho and The Recruiter out fishing. The money he made from the games, alongside his uniform, was all stored in a plastic barrel buried into the ground under his dog's kennel. In contrast to his work at the games he had a relatively spotless criminal record, with only one fine for illegal gambling issued around 2014.
Hwang Jun-ho initially believed Captain Park saved his life after he was shot by the Front Man—who was later revealed to be his brother—and fell from a cliff into the sea. However, Park was either instructed or hired by Hwang In-ho to rescue Jun-ho and to keep an eye on him.
Park would often suggest to Jun-ho to turn around when they could not find the island, He would show doubt to Jun-ho but he continue to follow though his request to help find the island. 
When Jun-ho recruited a group mercenaries, Captain park was assigned to sail with one boat consisting of half of the team. During one of the initial searches he realised that the mercenaries had a drone operator with them, which posed a more serious threat to the games as there was a real chance that they could find the island. The captain pretended to not know what a drone was as a way to act coy. In the next search the captain sailed to a decoy island where the team had spotted a door leading underground. The team ventured onto the island and attempted to open the door, but it was rigged with explosives and proved to be a trap, forcing them to return to the boat. Captain Park took the opportunity to try to convince them to go back as one of the members had died and another was wounded. Their leader Kim made the counter argument that since they lost a teammate they shouldn't give up on their search. 
That night Park snuck out onto deck during a storm, when all the mercenaries were asleep inside. He found the drone and began trying to dismantle it with a screwdriver. At this time though, the Drone pilot woke up with a hangover and quickly ran out onto deck to vomit over the side into the sea. Noticing the light from Park's torch he goes into the storage room and catches him in the act. Park pretends he was just examining it out of interest but this doesn't fully convince the pilot. He turns around to face Park and asks him point blank who he really is, and in response Park stabs him with multiple times with the screwdriver, throwing his body overboard once he's done. The commotion wakes up Choi Woo-seok who comes up onto deck just after Park has finished the murder. Park manages to convince Woo-seok that what he heard was simply waves crashing over the deck. Woo-seok heads back to bed but he is already very suspicious of Park and would go on to investigate Park by himself.
Park was successfully able to convince the rest of the mercenaries that the pilot died due to an accident and they set out to sea again soon after, although with extra safety precautions. When Jun-ho recognizes the island that he was shot on, Park tries to argue that they could wreck the boat if they came too close, but this time they don't listen to him. After Jun-ho gets back from the island they meet to discuss where the island could possibly be. Park tries to send them in the opposite direction by suggesting Chinese patrol boats would have prevented the games from being hosted in a certain region, and the mercenaries believe him.

Fate[]
Captain Hwang, it's been fun.
Captain Park's last words to Hwang Jun-ho 
Woo-seok's investigation led him to Park's house where he found sufficient evidence that he was working for the games. Unfortunately though one of his neighbors had called the police, leading to Woo-seok being arrested. Once he was at the police station an officer decided to call Park to inform him of the break in. Park now knew that he had been exposed and he couldn't let the mercenaries continue in case Woo-seok informed them he was a double agent. He informed Captain Ko on Boat 2, via radio, that their mission had been compromised and they needed to kill everyone on the boats. Park armed himself with a gun before going down to where the mercenaries were having dinner. Here he opened fire and killed four of the unarmed mercenaries as they tried to get away. One of the mercenaries managed to get a gun, but he didn't shoot in time and was killed by Park. Park entered the main cabin where he found Kim, who was wounded but not dying, trying to reach a rifle in the corner. He shot the rifle away from Kim before asking him where Jun-ho was. As it turns out Jun-ho was behind Park holding a spear gun, and when Park turned around he fired it, striking Park in the chest. As he lay dying on the floor he informed Jun-ho how he had been tasked with saving him by the Frontman. Jun-ho tried to make him give up where the island was, but Park refused because he believed that Jun-ho could not do anything and eventually died of his injuries.

Personality[]
Throughout Season 2, Park plays dumb, pretending not to know what a drone is, such as calling it a "drome", to avoid suspicion of being an informant for the Squid Game overseers. From the start, he knew of Jun-ho's plan to track Gi-hun with the device, which he then reported to the Squid Game higher-ups, leading to its removal before Gi-hun awoke[1] inside the game. Park never intended to let Jun-ho uncover the Squid Game's location. His betrayal was from him being ordered to do so, with little to no other possible motive or reluctance to him being implied to work under the games for a while, also implying he is loyal to his work under the games. Park also informed the overseers about the island discovered via drone, resulting in the bomb explosion.
