Im Jeong-dae (임정대 Im Jeong-dae?), also known as Player 100, was a participant of the 37th Squid Game who was in extreme debt. He was one of the finalists of the competition.
He acted as the de facto leader of Team O, a faction of players that continually voted to keep playing, and came into constant conflict with Seong Gi-hun, who had entered the games in an attempt to end them.

Personality
Hey, 456. Tell me something. Was this part of your plan the whole time? Oh, that's it, isn't it? You get everyone to join your little rebellion so you could get rid of them all at once.
Im Jeong-dae mocking Seong Gi-hun's failed rebellion in Keys and Knives
Im Jeong-dae is an aggressive old man who constantly demands players to continue the games. He showcases immense greed, willing to convince others to continue playing for his own benefit. Furthermore, he is quick to judge others like he did with Seong Gi-hun (Player 456). In addition, he is shown to value money over human lives and even his own, as after the second game, he was disappointed that so many people had survived, reducing the share of the money available in the prize. He has shown to be unremorseful as shown when he betrayed Seon-nyeo in Hide and Seek upon finding the exit, taking one of the keys for himself to lock her in the maze. He is shown to be capable of rallying groups, getting half of the contestants to vote "O" on at least one occasion. However, when his life is truly threatened, he does beg for mercy or help, though he'll quickly revert back to his aggressive self once he is out of danger.

History
Backstory
Presumably a businessman of significant repute,[1] Im Jeong-dae took out a loan totaling ₩10 billion and was unable to convert it into profit, putting him in deep debt. With few options, he decided to participate in the Squid Games.   
He was one of the many players to have their names and amounts of debt called out in front of players; as expected, this led to a plethora of reactions among the contestants, none positive. This caused him to erupt in front of the players to gloat about the fact he was able to pull off a ₩10 billion loan voicing his views on it as being something to brag about.  

Squid Game
He played Red Light, Green Light as the first challenge. He took no notice of Seong Gi-hun warning of the consequences of getting eliminated until people started dying around him. All the players eventually followed Seong Gi-hun orders and formed multiple straight lines to avoid being caught by the motion-tracking system of Young-hee. The player in front of him, Player 237, got shot but he was able to survive the first challenge. When the surviving players went back to the lobby, Im Jeong-dae hid underneath the bed, he stayed in place until the prize money was shown for winning the first game. He can always be seen in the company of Kim Yeong-sam. During the voting, Seong Gi-hun tried to convince the players to vote "X" to stop playing and Im Jeong-dae started to question him because he kept shouting during the game, he even accuses the Pink Guards of putting Seong Gi-hun in the game to scare them, but Park Jung-bae tells the bitter old man to shut up and that thanks to Seong Gi-hun they managed to survive the game.
When Seong Gi-hun reveals he's been in the games before and Thanos suggests using his experience, Im Jeong-dae tries to convince everyone to have Seong Gi-hun help them win. He celebrated the continuation of the games with Player 240. 
For the second game he partnered with Kim Yeong-sam, Player 023, Player 285 and Player 428. Im Jeong-dae was tasked on completing Jegi. His team completed the games and headed back to the dormitory. When Seong Gi-hun's team came back, he was disappointed that more people passed the second challenge making the prize money smaller. When the voting took place for the second time, he convinced players to join his side to continue the games. He got a crowd to chant "One more game".
During Mingle, he's shown during the 6 people limit, where Im Jeong-dae and Kim Yeong-sam push out Kang Dae-ho and his group out as they already had 6 players at their location. He survived the third game. When 2 people were required per door, he went to a door with his ally Kim Yeong-sam. 
Once the third game was finished, a vote was held and the result was a tie. Im Jeong-dae and the players who voted to continue games sat with each other for the remainder of the day. When a fight took place at the bathroom, he wanted to know how many people died on his side and the other. Once they completed counting, they devised a plan to take out players who voted to leave. The lights of the dormitory went out and his side slowly crept up to their opponents. More players from "X"'s side were taken out. After the special game, he was seen him about to attack Player 359, but was stopped by the guards. The special game also was from Gi-hun's plan to start a riot and steal the Soldiers' guns.
After the events of the rebellion, during which Seong Gi-hun was handcuffed, Im Jeong-dae and his team laughed at him; suggesting that Seong Gi-hun's cries to be killed were all lies. As the fourth game began, Im Jeong-dae was assigned to the blue team, tasked with hiding and finding the exit using three keys. He was nearly pissed when he hears Kim Yeong-sam arguing with the guards about the game being unfair to the Red Team, in which the team Im Jeong-dae was in nearly had an advantage. He was nearly discovered by Nam-gyu, but had a clever plan, he smeared blood on his face to appear dead, successfully evading capture. Seon-nyeo intervened and helped him escape, but Im Jeong-dae decided to leave and lock Seon-nyeo behind, to be killed by Park Min-su.
He voted “O” for the fourth round of voting. In the fifth game, Jeong-dae was visibly nervous after being introduced to Jump Rope, but he eventually managed to make progress. He was seen clapping for Gi-hun when he managed to cross the bridge while carrying a baby. Player 096 blocked Im Jeong-dae from completing the challenge and threatened to push him off the rope. Im Jeong-dae tried to negotiate with Player 096, but it never worked. After 096’s death, Im Jeong-dae advanced as one of the final players for the sixth game.
When eight players returned to the dormitory, the guard announced there were actually nine present. The group was confused, insisting there were only eight, until Jun-hee’s child was counted as Player 222, bringing the total amount of Players to nine. This caused Lee Seung-won to be enraged, as he stated that the eight of them had to go through deadly games to advance, while a baby could just join in. The loss of 640 million won due to the baby’s inclusion led everyone to attempt to kill the child, though their efforts ultimately failed.
The group then shared a dinner at the Squid Game dormitory, followed by the final vote ever. During bedtime, Im Jeong-dae was about to be stabbed by Gi-hun with the knife given to him by In-ho while he was sleeping, but survived due to Gi-hun's hallucination of Kang Sae-byeok telling him to not kill Jeong-dae, referencing Season 1. 
Im Jeong-dae chose “O” one last time, determining his fate in the games. With the vote split 2-5 in favor of a larger team, he planned to eliminate the “X” players in the final game. The “O” team reached an agreement and advanced to the next stage, initially targeting Gi-hun before shifting their focus to Min-su for elimination. Afterwards, Lee Seung-won attempted to attack Gi-hun, but was overpowered. Gi-hun threatens Seung-won that if he doesn't surrender now, he will be pushed off. Just before Lee Seung-won could do anything, Lee Myung-gi immediately uses a metal rod to push the former off the platform, causing him to die. In the second round, the group tried to form another agreement to stop Gi-hun and the baby, but their plans failed, causing Kim Gi-min, and Kim Yun-tae to be killed off, leaving only five remaining players: 039, 100, 333, 456 and the baby. Lee Myung-gi and Seong Gi-hun immediately turn to Im Jeong-dae, who attempted to play the victim, by acting like he is on their side, pointing to a dead Kim Gi-min saying, "Well done guys! You did the right thing! That trash should've died a long time ago!". Unconvinced, Myung-gi and Gi-hun both walk up to Jeong-dae in defense.

Fate
What... what's up? Is it still too low? That's fine. Ten billion won, that's all I need! I'll give you the rest of it, okay? 1.4 billion! Then you can sh... sh... sh... share it between yourselves! It's all yours! Okay?
Jeong-dae offering to share the rest of his shares with Lee Myung-gi and Seong Gi-hun in "⭘△☐"
Im Jeong-dae finds himself cornered by both Lee Myung-gi and Seong Gi-hun. In a desperate attempt to save himself, Im Jeong-dae tries to convince the pair not to kill him, offering a series of excuses. He first urged them to finish off Player 039, who was already severely injured, hoping to buy himself more time. However, Myung-gi refused. Undeterred, Jeong-dae then promised that if Myung-gi and Gi-hun eliminate Player 039 and vote "X" to leave immediately, the three of them plus the baby would each earn 11.4 billion won. Jeong-dae even offered to give an additional 1.4 billion won for Myung-gi and Gi-hun to split between them. Myung-gi declined the offer, pointing out that if Im Jeong-dae dies, the prize money would rise to 15.2 billion won, with no need to share it with him. Myung-gi ultimately pushed him off a ledge using a metal rod and fell to his death from a great height.

Trivia
Im Jeong-dae is the only Hider seen using the exit in Hide and Seek, though Cho Hyun-ju and Seon-nyeo also had the opportunity to do so before willingly turning back or being locked out, respectively.
Im Jeong-dae is the only finalist to not have a confirmed act of direct physical murder, as everyone else was a Seeker in Hide and Seek, meaning that the other finalists (not counting the baby) all committed at least one murder.
He was counted 46th in the Team O headcount.
To pay off his debt, Im Jeong-dae would need to survive and be among the final four players left standing. He ultimately failed in this by finishing 5th overall in the game. His Season 1 counterpart would be Player 360 based on ranking.
His quote "One more game!" and the scene where he rallies his fellow O Team to vote for the game to continue had become a meme in the Squid Game community, with people even dubbed him as "Mr. One More Game".
He is the last player in the 10X range to be eliminated in the 37th squid game and in the South Korean Games as a whole.
Also the last X00, the last group range from 100 to 199 and the last elderly player to die.
Just like Player 453, he smashed his skull on the impact of his fall.
Behind the scenes
Im Jeong-dae was portrayed by Song Young-chang. He was credited as "백억남", which translates to "ten billion won man", and "negative millionaire" in some languages.
