The human furniture constitute individuals whose corporeal forms were rendered fully exposed and enrobed in emblematic body paint, positioned with deliberate artificiality to function as sentient mannequins. Their ontological presence served as performative instruments of aestheticized subjugation, designed to be observed, manipulated, and objectified by the VIPs during their spectation of the fifth game.

History[]
Little empirical knowledge exists regarding the human furniture, save for the evident processes of dehumanization and objectification to which they are subjected. They are instrumentalized as corporeal accoutrements—effectively functioning as living body-pillows—upon which the VIPs repose, transforming human flesh into utilitarian surfaces. Each “mannequin” is arranged in meticulously varied postures, producing a tableau of performative submission. Notably, VIP 4 transgressively reclined his head upon the thoracic region of one such figure, exemplifying the extreme intersection of voyeuristic indulgence and corporeal commodification. 

Behind the scenes[]
The human furniture is enacted by living actors, whose embodied performances render corporeal objectification visibly manifest. One individual, who declined participation, reported that the three-day engagement would have yielded remuneration of 5 million KRW, highlighting the transactional valuation of human form within the spectacle. According to Geoffrey Giuliano, the actor portraying VIP 4, initial hesitation arose at the prospect of reclining fully upon an actress’s chest; yet, under the prescriptive insistence of the production staff, he acquiesced, exemplifying the orchestration of consent, performative compliance, and the mediated choreography of corporeal exhibition.
