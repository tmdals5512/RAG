When I played baseball, my pitches might've been slow, but I had flawless ball control.
Park Jung-bae before completing Biseokchigi on his first go in "One More Game"Biseokchigi (비석치기 Bi-seog Chi-gi?), also known as Flying Stone, is a traditional Korean game featured as the second mini-game in the Six-Legged Pentathlon.
Game Information[]
Objective: The player in charge of this mini-game must use a stone given by a Worker to hit a small tombstone placed 3-4 meters away by throwing it. Once the tombstone is knocked, the team passes this mini-game 
A key rule is that the player must not cross the designated throwing line while attempting to hit the tombstone. If the player hits the tombstone but steps on or crosses the line, the throw does not count and they must try again.
Notable players who participated in this mini-game include Player 007 (Park Yong-sik), Player 045, Player 198 (Jang Do-yeong), Player 380 (Se-mi), and Player 390 (Park Jung-bae).

Trivia[]
In the episode “The Man with the Umbrella”, Gi-hun briefly mentioned Biseokchigi, wondering what the next game could be.
Traditionally, this game is played with real stones or bricks. In the past, children would grab any flat stones resembling a tombstone to play on the streets. For safety reasons, it is now commonly played using thick cardboard or wood during school events or competitions.
The best way to achieve a successful throw is by using an underhand motion and rolling the stone along the ground, which is what Se-mi did.
In traditional street play, the size and weight of the stone could vary, making some tombstones harder to hit than others.
