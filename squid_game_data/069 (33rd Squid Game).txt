History[]
Player 069 was married to his wife in an unknown year. Problems started to rise more for Player 069, as he had grown to be in heavy debt alongside his wife. Due to massive financial problems, him and his wife chose to enter the Squid Games to win money. After waking up in a large room with his wife and 454 other people, he signed a contract and took his photo. He managed to survive the first game, Red Light, Green Light, after most of the contestants had been wiped out. Afterwards, like everyone else, he was shaken by the deaths of the contestants, when the prize money was finally revealed. A vote was held if the game should continue, and the majority voted no and he also most likely. However, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players. 
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs, and the third game, Tug of War. Before Tug of War, Cho Sang-woo wanted to recruit Player 069. However, once he reveal to Cho Sang-woo that his wife has to join the team as well, Cho Sang-woo changed his mind. For the fourth game, he chose his wife to be his partner, but it was revealed that the game was Marbles and that players would face their partners. The couple was shocked to discover that only 1 of them would survive and in the end 069 managed to win the game, causing 070 to be eliminated.

Fate[]
In the Dormitory, 069 tried to convince the majority to leave, but Sang-woo said that if they did, the deaths of the eliminated players would be in vain. At bedtime, 069 decides to kill himself to make the pain of his wife's death disappear, so he ties his sheet in a hangman's knot, ties it around his neck and hangs himself, taking his own life and joining his wife in death. The next morning, the players discover that 069 had died by suicide and the Workers put his body in a coffin. Cho Sang-woo and Seong Gi-hun were shocked, but Jang Deok-su was happy as it just meant more prize money.

Trivia[]
His death is very similar to that of Jang Geum-ja in Season 3, and in a less similar sense, The Recruiter, as all three killed a family member of theirs during the games, with Geum-ja being forced to kill her son, Park Yong-sik in order to protect Kim Jun-hee and her baby, Player 069 was forced to play against his wife, and the Recruiter killing his own father via his superior's orders; all three then committed suicide sometime afterwards.
Of the three, the Recruiter did not kill himself by hanging, in the dormitory, or out of shame and/or regret.
He always sticks to his wife during the games.
His actors name shares the name of one of the finalists from Season 2-3. And also Player 320 who won the 2003 Squid Games.
He is the last player whose elimination is announced in the 33rd Squid Game.
The panther-masked VIP bet $1 million on him to win the competitions.
In the second game when Han Mi-nyeo passes the lighter to Deok-su, the voice announces that Player 069 and Player 070 passed the game in succession, implying that both he and his wife made it through the second game together.
Mistakes[]
In Red Light, Green Light, Player 069 is portrayed as a young man in the Dormitory.
During the initial chaos, a female Player 069 can be seen.
Player 069's erroneous icon is the same as Player 448's icon.
In A Fair World, an another Player 069 can be seen in the dormitory.
Behind the scenes[]
Player 069 is portrayed by Kim Yun-tae, as well as multiple unknown extras in erroneous appearances.
In an interview, Player 069's actor, Kim Yun-tae, said that he likes to believe that his wife, Player 070, beat him in Marbles but sacrificed herself, as she believed her husband had a better chance at winning the games. He also stated they probably entered the Squid Games due to living a difficult life and having marital problems of some sort and that the two would've had some off-screen arguments during the competition.
Yun-tae also stated that he didn't know the innuendo behind the number "69" until other people told him later on.
