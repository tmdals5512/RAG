Game Information[]
When the game starts, the Hiders have 2 minutes to explore the maze. Their goal is either to hide until the game ends, or to find an exit before they get killed by the Red Team within 30 minutes. Each member of the Hiders is given a key with different shape (either square, triangle, or circle). They may use the keys to unlock the doors in the maze, but once a door is unlocked, it cannot be relocked. They may also kill the red team to defend themselves. If one member of the Hiders gets killed by the Red Team, that hider will be eliminated from the game, and the Seeker that killed them will pass.

Trivia[]
Although one of the hiders' main goals was to find the exit and get out, only Jeong-dae is seen accomplishing it.
Hyun-ju is the only hider who is known to have killed a seeker.
Player 185 was originally supposed to be a hider[1], but was instead killed by Nam-gyu in the Special Game.
Kim Yun-tae was also supposed to be a hider, but his role was switched in the final show.
Out of 30 Hiders, only 7 had passed the game without being seen by a seeker or found the exit, making the Hiders role having a survival rate of 23.33%.
There are 7 hiders that were killed by offscreen seekers due to their eliminations being announced off-screen: Players 009, 024, 134, 197, 241, 344 and 418.
Nam-gyu and Lee Myung-gi killed the most hiders and indirectly caused 5 seekers to die from them.
Dae-ho is the only Hider who died without getting stabbed.
Im Jeong-dae was the only Hider to be a finalist.
If a hider escapes through the exit, it will not be announced that he passed the game, this was seen with Jeong-dae.
Player 334 and Player 350 are the only hiders whose corpses are never seen.
Kim Yeong-Sam's comment to Im Jeong-Dae at the beginning in the lobby, "That's what you're good at. Hiding like a rat," could be said to be quite accurate, given that although logic would suggest that players like 024, 134, 197, Park-Jae Cheol (Player 235), and 418, who participated in either the Special Game or the Bathroom Fight, demonstrated the ability to defend themselves and kill, but failed to advance, the hiders who did advance had a much lower profile and none had committed a murder directly by that point in the game, reinforcing the perception that the best strategy was to hide and not actively seek the exit.
Two Hiders died without explaination, and it was originally intended for Player 191 to pass the game, as he is present on a board in the fifth game which lists most of the other players who perish in that game, meaning it was originally intended for one of the two Hiders to be his kill.
