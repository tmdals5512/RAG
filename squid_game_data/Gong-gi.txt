You said you played Gong-gi with bullets during the Korean War.
Park Yong-sik encouraging Jang Geum-ja in "One More Game"Gonggi Nori (공기놀이 Gong-gi Nor-i?), also known simply as Gonggi (공기 Gong-gi?), Korean Jacks or Knucklebones, is a traditional Korean game featured as the third mini-game in the Six-Legged Pentathlon.
Game Information[]
Objective: The player in charge of this mini-game must perform a gonggi gameplay and complete all 5 levels by tossing and catching the stones according to the rules of each level. Once all 5 levels are cleared and the player catches all 5 stones at the end, the team passes this mini-game.
In this game, the Player in charge will be given 5 gonggi stones (generally made of plastic with pebbles inside for weight) and a flat table. To play the game, player must toss a single gonggi stone into the air, pick up another, and catch the tossed stone. The challenge increases as the player progresses, requiring them to pick up additional stones with each round. If the player missed any stones, touches a stone they didn't want to pick up, they have to restart from the beginning, regardless of the level they've passed.
The game tests both precision and coordination, making it a critical challenge in the Six-Legged Pentathlon.
Notable players who participated in this mini-game include Player 125 (Park Min-su), Player 149 (Jang Geum-ja), Player 246 (Park Gyeong-seok), Player 254, and Player 388 (Kang Dae-ho).


How to play Gonggi in each level:


Level

Steps


1

Scatter all stones onto the playing surface.Throw one stone into the air.Pick up one stone from the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.Repeat until all five stones are collected.


2

Scatter all stones onto the playing surface.Throw one stone into the air.Pick up two stones from the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.Repeat until all five stones are collected.


3

Scatter all stones onto the playing surface.Throw one stone into the air.Pick up three stones from the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.Throw one stone into the air again.Pick up the last stone from the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.


4

Throw one stone into the air.Drop other four stones onto the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.Throw the stone into the air again.Pick up all four stones from the playing surface and catch the airborne stone.


5

Throw all five stones into the air.Catch all five stones on the back of your hand.Throw all five stones into the air again.Catch all five stones in the end.

Trivia[]
In the episode "The Man with the Umbrella", Gi-hun briefly mentioned gonggi, wondering what the next game could be.
In the episode “Stick to the team”, Gi-hun briefly mentioned gonggi, wondering what the next game could have been and choosing the players for his team.
In the episode "Gganbu", Han Mi-nyeo claims that she is good at gonggi while trying to partner up with Do Jung-soo and take Player 081's place.
Gonggi was previously considered a "girls’ game" because it doesn’t require any physical strength, only hand-eye coordination. It is also the mini-game with the most female players (around 33 out of 73).
However, male players such as Kang Dae-ho, Park Gyeong-seok, Park Min-su, Player 241, and Player 285 have also played gonggi.
Gonggi is the only mini-game where objects are not presented on a wooden tray, but rather presented on a black table, which players use as the playing surface during the game.
Gonggi is the most complicated mini-game in the Six-Legged Pentathlon, both inside and outside the games, as since it contains tons of layers and rules and requires good hand-eye coordination.
