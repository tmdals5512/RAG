Hide and Seek (숨바꼭질 Sumbakkogjil?) was the fourth game of the 37th Squid Game. This game was played in 2 teams (Blue team as the hiders and red team as the seekers).
The goal of this game was for the hiders not to get caught by the seekers, while the seekers had to kill the hiders, all within the 30-minute time limit. The seekers were given knives in order to kill at least one hider to pass the game before the time limit, otherwise they would be eliminated. Meanwhile, the hiders were given variously shaped keys (circle, square, and triangle) to unlock different doors across the arena. The hiders could pass the game by either staying hidden until the time limit ends, or escaping through the exit, which could only be unlocked by inserting all three keys into the door.
60 players participated in this game. 35 players (around 58.3%) were eliminated and 25 players (around 41.7%) progressed to the next round. At this point in the competition, 431 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 006, Player 007, Player 009, Player 044, Player 120, Player 172, Player 197, Player 202, Player 226, Player 232, Player 235, Player 296, Player 388, Player 394, Player 400, Player 411 and Player 447.
This game took place on November 5, 2024.

Game Information
The lobby of this game is a large black room with a yellow floor divided by a straight line, and a Gumball machine that dispenses blue and red balls. The main entrance to this area is knife-shaped, while the entrance to the game arena is keyhole-shaped. There's a large timer on top of the keyhole entrance, and said entrance is lifted up a bit by a small stairs. 
The game was played in an arena resembling a maze, that contains around 7 floors. The lower walls are grey concrete with bricks of the same color, while the upper walls are dark blue and have many tiny yellow stars resembling a starry night sky. The ceiling has the same design but with bigger yellow stars that serve as lamps. The walls also have various childres drawings like clouds, houses, suns, flowers, bushes, trees, spaceships, swings, telescopes, and many others, reflecting the narrative of kids going home at sunset. Around the arena there's also a few lampposts, cables, what seem to be trashcans, and metal fences, simulating the street.

     Red vests and Blue vests.  
The game will be played in teams. The team a player is on will be decided by a Gumball machine that dispenses blue and red balls, making the placement of a Player in a team totally random. If one gets red, they go to the left side of the room, while blue goes to the right side, splitting the players into two teams.
Having been divided into two teams, the players receive an item as a gift from the guards, the Blue Team receives keys and the Red Team receives knives. When the Blue Team enter the game, they have 2 minutes to explore the maze before the Red Team come to find them. The maze is filled with locked doors and when a door is unlocked it cannot be relocked.

Seekers
The Seekers must kill at least one member of the Hiders to pass the game within 30 minutes, however they are allowed to kill more than one if they so choose. However, if two seekers decide to kill a hider at the same time, it only counts as a half-kill and requires the two seekers to kill another hider to pass the game. If a seeker fails to kill a hider within the time limit, then they will be eliminated. Also, Seekers cannot kill each other, otherwise they would be eliminated. However, hiders can kill seekers.

Hiders

The Hiders must either use the key to unlock the doors and find an exit, or hide until the 30-minute time limit is reached. The hiders can also kill the seekers to defend themselves. If a hider gets killed by a seeker, that hider will be eliminated. Inside the arena, the locked doors have three different keyhole shapes required (either circle, triangle or square) and for the exit door, all three types of keys are needed to unlock it. Once a door is open, it cannot be locked again.
Prior to the game beginning, players were allowed to switch teams if they wished. In order to do this, they had to swap with a member of the opposing team and this could only be done under mutual consent from both players.

Rooms and Areas
Lobby
The Lobby is the area where the players enter first before starting the game, and it's also the area where they get divided into teams. It's a large black room with a yellow floor divided by a straight line, and a Gumball machine that dispenses blue and red balls. The main entrance to this area is knife-shaped, while the entrance to the game arena is keyhole-shaped. There's a large timer on top of the keyhole entrance, and said entrance is lifted up a bit by small stairs.

The Maze
The Maze is where 80% of the entire game takes place, being a large labyrinth of 7 to 8 floors. The lower walls are grey concrete with bricks of the same color, while the upper walls are dark blue and have many tiny yellow stars resembling a starry night sky. The ceiling has the same design but with bigger yellow stars that serve as lamps. The walls also have various children drawings like clouds, houses, suns, flowers, bushes, trees, spaceships, swings, telescopes, and many others, reflecting the narrative of kids going home at sunset. Around the Maze there's also a few lampposts, cables, what seem to be trashcans, and metal fences, simulating the street. The doors around the map are dark green and need to be unlocked with the different keys given to the hiders. In some staircases, the stars on the ceiling disappear, and, instead, the staircases are illuminated by more industrial lamps with a white light.

Starting Area
The Starting Area is the first location that the players find themselves in. It has the same design as the rest of the arena, with the grey concrete and brick walls, and the dark blue ceiling and upper walls with yellow stars. It has double entry doors, a wooden lamp pole in the middle, and seven paths to go, including a slope down and a staircase up.

Trapdoor Room
The trapdoor room is an area likely around the center of the maze. It is first seen by Kang Dae-ho and Player 197, who both find it at the same time, but manage to catch themselves before falling. While only four levels were seen in the show, this room reveals that there are actually around seven to eight levels.

A - Cloud Room
The cloud room is the first of the special colorful rooms seen in Hide and Seek; this room is discovered by Cho Hyun-ju's team, and is the room where Hyun-ju figured out the different kinds of keys. This room has blue pink and yellow walls and roofing with the colors fading into each other, as well as a semi-green floor. There are many paper clouds throughout, as well as a variety of paper flowers, paper clouds, and paper planes. There is paper fencing and little houses, a paper sun, as well as a little paper dog. One of the walls has a large paper rainbow with the 7 colors.

B - Dinosaur Room
The dinosaur room is first discovered by Park Jae-cheol, and is where he fought and lost the fight for his life, as well as the place where Park Min-su discovered the drugs. It resembles rainbow scratch paper, with many dinosaur drawings on the walls, showing many fish and marine reptiles on the floor, pentagonal stars and Flying reptiles like Pteranodons on the roof. The Front wall contains a Tyrannosaurus with some sauropods in the back. The left wall contains a Hadrosaurus, what appears to be a Dromaeosaurus, a Compsognathus, and 2 unknown small dinosaurs. The right wall has an active volcano, a Sauropod, a Parasaurolophus, a Tyrannosaurus fighting with a Triceratops, and what seems to be an exotic plant. There's many other details like meteors, erupting volcanoes, palm trees, mountains, grass, and two big white stars as lamps on the ceiling.

C - Park Room
This room is discovered by Im Jeong-dae and Player 232, as well as Player 349, who killed the latter of the duo within the room. Like the previous, it resembles rainbow scratch paper, but the drawings are different. It appears like a field, with many trees and bushes. It also has a few houses with chimneys, fences, mountains, butterfly wings in some houses, grass, and a lake, with insects on the floor. The ceiling contains many clouds and what seems to be butterflies.

D - Apple Tree Room
This room is briefly seen at the beginning of the game, as Player 232 emerges from it as Yeong-sam chased a hider. It is later seen with Player 197's corpse on the ground, with Seong Gi-hun inspecting it. It is the more simple of the rooms, with a green floor, hills on the bottom of the floor, a few flowers, a giant sun, clouds, and birds on the walls, and a large apple tree. The art style in this room is similar to the Rainbow Exit.

E - Marine Room
Cho Hyun-ju's team would find and use this room as a place to perform Kim Jun-hee's birth. They spent about thirteen minutes in this room, with Hyun-ju leaving and returning, where she would meet her end. This room resembles underwater, and has many shades of blue blending into each other in the walls, simulating waves or ocean depth. There is small paper fish, paper starfish, and paper seaweed around the room, as well as other paper animals like an octopus, a whale, a shark, a squid with a baby squid, a turtle, a crab, and a jellyfish. Some bubbles in many different sizes are also seen around the walls. The ceiling also contains two big stars that serve as lamps for lightning, even though stars aren't related to the ocean.

F - Neighborhood Room
The neighborhood room was used as Player 172's hiding place, as well as where he had his fight with Yong-sik. It is majorly orange. With a green floor and orange path going down the center with hand flowers. It has more hand flowers on the lower walls, with a few painted frogs. There are also large houses with butterflies and a sun.

G - Forest Exit
The first of the exits was first discovered by Seon-nyeo. She attempted to leave, but realized that she only had the circle, instead of all three. She would later return with Jeong-dae, but would be betrayed by him and killed by Min-su. This room has light blue walls, resembling the sky, and contains many trees around the walls, including painted green ones and hand print ones. The floor is solid green, resembling the grass, with additional hand prints on the ground. There are also small ladybugs, butterflies and caterpillars scattered through out. The '나가는곳' is in red text in a yellow cloud.

H - Rainbow Exit
The second exit seen was found and unlocked by Cho Hyun-ju. She would use all three keys to go open it, but would go back for her allies, an action that costed her her life. Park Yong-sik would later come across this room, but would be killed for failing the game. Additional bodies include Players 202 and 241. A section of the floor and wall is green, with a purple flower in the floor, and yellow, purple, orange, and pink flowers on the walls. There are also butterflies with very light blue wings. There are clouds all over the rooms and the actual door has a large red orange yellow green and blue lines simulating a rainbow, with clouds at the end of it. The ceiling contains many bird drawings an a weird four-shaped figure serving as a lamp. The '나가는곳' is in black text in a light yellow house with a yellow roof. The art style in this room is similar to the Apple Tree Room. 

Trivia
For a hider, it is ideal to ensure that the door is closed behind you while hiding in a room.
Both the appearance of the arena and the title of the second episode of Season 3 in which the game takes place are most likely references to The Starry Night, a painting by the Dutch artist Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1889.
Unlike games like Marbles or Six-Legged Pentathlon, in this game, the players could change teams even after the game was explained. This is most likely intentional, as no Seeker would want to be a Hider and be killed.
The room that the blue team uses to escape from the maze, or better known as the "Congratulations Room", was never actually shown on screen directly, so the audience never knew the actual contents of the room. When opened, the song Congratulations by Cliff Richard is played, and a yellow glowing light illuminates the players.
Park Sung-hoon later revealed that the "Congratulations Room" was just a wall.[1]
This is the first game shown in Squid Game where the VIPs are allowed to directly participate in eliminating players in the game.
Since the exit door in this game requires three different types of keys, the Hiders must collect the remaining two keys either by retrieving them from eliminated teammates, attempting to steal them (although nearly impossible), or cooperating with other hiders.
Doors couldn't be locked again after unlocking them. However, the exit seems to be an exception as Player 100 managed to lock it again by removing 1 of the 3 keys.
This game has the highest number of major character deaths in Season 3, including Kim Yeong-sam, Cho Hyun-ju, Seon-nyeo, Kang Dae-ho and Park Yong-sik, making it the most emotional game of the season.
This game is tied with Sky Squid Game for the most named characters to die, with a total of seven (Cho Hyun-ju, Seon-nyeo, Kang Dae-ho, Park Yong-sik, Kim Yeong-sam, Park Mi-hwa and Park Jae-cheol).
The Seekers' knives are actually made of rubber. In behind-the-scenes footage, when Hyun-ju stabbed Yeong-sam, the blade bent out of the camera.
The room in which Kim Jun-hee gives birth to her baby is decorated with marine-themed paper decors & wall paintings, including the squid decor. This can be interpreted as foreshadowing Kim Jun-hee's death, as some types of female squid will die after they give birth to their young.
This game was the first official game of the 37th Squid Game where players were eliminated not by being shot or falling from a height but by being killed by other players (except when time had run out).
Though not stated as an official rule, if two Seekers are to participate in killing the same Hider simultaneously, the point will be split among the participants.
This is shown when Lee Myung-gi and Nam-gyu decides to cooperate and kill Park Jae-cheol together, as the announcer never declared their passing status until they cooperated to kill Player 411 later on.
It is unknown if this rule applies for scenarios where there are more than two Seekers participating.
The Seekers can indirectly eliminate other Seekers by killing more Hiders if necessary to make the Red team struggle, resulting in their elimination. This is when Nam-gyu stated that “If we’re killing more blue teams, we’re eliminating the Red ones too.”
The English subtitles suggest that Seekers might also be referred to as "Taggers".
Director Hwang Dong-hyuk states this game represents playing in the streets until the sun goes down. Then during night, having to make your way home safely.
Seong Gi-hun's statement after the Xs lost the second vote, "What if for the next game we have to kill each other to win?", could be seen as a foreshadowing to this game.
This game shares some parallels with Marbles, both being the fourth game of their respective Squid Game events.
Both games divide the players into two groups in order to make them fight for survival.
Both games have the maximum capacity for half of the players to survive, yet more than half were eliminated due to special circumstances. In Hide and Seek's case, it is due to some Seekers (like Lee Myung-gi and Nam-gyu) killing more Hiders than what they need to pass, consequently dooming a number of other Seekers to be eliminated due to the number of Hiders no longer matching the number of Seekers.
Both games are where the most emotional losses occur in their respective Squid Game events.
Both games' arena feature a maze-like set, decorated to mimic Korean urban hometowns. The sky decorations are also meant to evoke similar times of the day, with Marbles featuring an early twilight backdrop, while Hide and Seek adopts a nighttime theme.
Both were the last game in their respective editions in which players were still eliminated by guards (in the following games were only eliminated by falling or by other players).
Both games indirectly serve to mentally break the players.
This game is the game that appears the most in Season 3, spanning across 3 episodes (4 if counting the scene where it shows up in the finale).
In the scene where Yeong-sam starts looking for the hiding ones, the unreleased soundtrack starts playing, which was not included in the main soundtrack album of Season 3.
The lyrics to the song foreshadow events throughout the game.
'Don't let your hair give you away.' probably reflecting when Player 197 was almost killed by Seong Gi-hun for mistaking his hair for Dae-ho's.
'Don't let your clothes give you away.' reflecting that the player's vests show what they are, and if a seeker sees that a player is wearing a blue vest, it gives away they can be killed.
'Don't let your toe give you away.' reflecting when Kang Dae-ho and Cho Hyun-ju injured their feet, leaving blood trails which led them to their deaths (as their killers found them after following their trails).
There was a leaked scene where Nam-gyu and Myung-gi were hunting Park Jae-cheol in a black room with colorful lines (which ended up being dinosaur drawings). Because of the video quality, many people ended up believing that the game was going to be Laser Tag despite no laser guns being shown, as well as that not being a game from the same time period.
If a Seeker dies during the game, their death will not be announced, this was seen with Kim Yeong-sam and Player 202.
The game contains two (or more) exits as seen during the game, knowing that the room leading to the exit that Seon-nyeo and Im Jeong-dae find is not the same one that Hyun-ju finds next and through which Jun-hee and Jang Geum-ja try to escape.
Although Player 080 is seen in the lobby room before the game, he is never seen in the Maze.
The reason why this game might've taken place could be to finally crush Gi-hun's hope for humanity.
This is the only game where there are no guards present during the game. They only appear after the timer runs out to eliminate the taggers who have lost the game.
In the shot of the hiders leaving the gumball machine room, the players that are seen entering first are actually the most significant deaths of the episode. Seon-nyeo and her followers, Jae-cheol, Player 411, Player 232, Dae-ho, and Hyun-ju.
There are many screams of unknown players throughout the game such as Player 448 screaming at ten minutes left and then screaming again at 9 minutes 25 seconds left, while being chased by Player 442 and then crashing somewhere, as seen in BTS photos and since her scream is the same one in jump rope.
In the English dub, a manager revealed that 110 players were eliminated in the fourth game which was an error as there were only 60 players remaining before the said game. This is most likely a translation error, since the Manager originally says that there were 35 players eliminated.
Player 185 was originally supposed to be a Hider in Hide and Seek, but was killed instead killed off by Nam-gyu in the Special Game.[2]

Im Jeong-dae is the only hider that became a finalist as well as the highest placed hider of Squid Game.
All recurring minor players from Season 2 (024, 028, 048, 080, 105, 119, 134, 154, 310, 334, 344 and 350) died in this game.
No hider who received a circle key survived. Of the seven Hiders who passed, more of them had square keys than triangle keys.
This coincidence is another indirect reference to the hierarchy of the Squid Games, symbolizing that the Workers, who wear circle masks, are the most vulnerable and need to be constantly supervised to maintain order, while the Managers are more capable of thinking for themselves and therefore are trusted with more freedoms.
All three remaining Players with factorial numbers up to this point, Park Mi-hwa, Player 024 and Cho Hyun-ju are designated as Hiders and get killed in this game.
Nam-gyu and Min-su are the only pair of players with consecutive numbers to pass both Hide and Seek.
Seven out of the nine finalists are Seekers during this game, with the exception of Im Jeong-dae who's a Hider, and the baby is not made a player yet.
All recurring players died off screen, with all seekers never being seen and all hiders only having their corpses shown (aside from 334 and 350, who were only audibly shown).
It is the only game in the 37th Squid Game whose rules are explained by the Manager instead of the announcer.
This game was clearly designed to wipe out all, or at least most, of the Blue Team. Several factors support this: the Manager withheld crucial information from the hiders, such as the different key shapes and the fact that all of them were required to exit; there were trap doors; very little time was given for hiding; and the Red Team was composed of the most ruthless, violent, and strongest players, strongly indicating that the team selection was rigged rather than random.
Not only that, but there's also the fact that there's no actual hiding spots in the game arena, and the blue vests they have instantly makes a Hider recognizable.
All players who swapped vests before the game ended up betraying each other:
Player 192 swapped with Player 440, and he ended up killing her.
Geum-ja swapped with Yong-sik, and she ended up causing him to lose the game by stabbing him in the back.
Myung-gi swapped with Jun-hee, and he ended up killing her protector, Hyun-ju, and leaving her after doing so.
When the four main characters are being loaded into their coffins at the beginning of S3 Ep3, they are presented in the opposite order of their deaths.
What make this game especially heartbreaking is that all major players are left worse:
Gi-hun is left in guilt after blindly blaming Dae-ho for the failed rebellion and kills him.
By killing Hyun-ju, Myung-gi destroy whatever chance he could had to mend his relations with Jun-hee.
Geum-ja is forced to watch her son died in front of her, especially since she stab him with her hairpin knife.
Jun-hee is devastated from her childbirth, Myung-gi's betrayal and Geum-ja and Yong-sik's last moment together.
Min-su using the drug led to him being haunted by the guilt of letting Se-mi die.
Not even villainous characters got it better:
Nam-gyu losing his drugs to Min-su, which lead to his withdrawal.
Seon-nyeo's cult being annihilated.
Additionally, this game also serves as the catalyst for Geum-ja and Jun-hee's death: the former hang herself after being haunted for having to kill her son and the latter's broken ankle giving her a massive disadvantage in Jump Rope, a game require jumping.

The four main deaths of the game represent the four directions.
Seon-nyeo sits up right, her head facing north.
From where Geum-ja and Jun-hee sat, Hyun-ju faced east.
Dae-ho's head rolled back into the trap door room, making him face south.
At the beginning of Episode 3, Yong-sik's body faced west.

The blue line that goes down the center of the gumball machine room symbolizes the single file line when the hiders (blue team) enter the arena.
