The V.I.P.s are the overarching antagonists of Squid Game. Comprised of extraordinarily wealthy business magnates from a diverse array of nationalities, they are principal investors behind the Squid Games who travel to South Korea specifically to observe the games firsthand. They not only finance the operations but also derive personal amusement and entertainment by wagering on the outcomes of the Participants.
The V.I.P.s are notorious for attending Squid Game events beyond Korea, extending their influence to international franchises. However, they assert in Season 1 that the original Korean iteration of the  Game remains the most compelling and superior version.

History[]
Season 1[]
The V.I.P.s were originally clients of Oh Il-nam and his financial firm. At some point, having grown weary of their lives of immense wealth and privilege, they convened and established the Squid Games as a means of personal amusement and diversion. Leveraging their considerable capital, the V.I.P.s financed the operation, ensuring that Oh Il-nam and his associates had all necessary resources to organize and execute the  Games with meticulous precision.
Midway through the 33rd game, the V.I.P.s arrived on the island to observe the Squid Game firsthand. They were received by Front Man, as Oh Il-nam was unable to attend personally, having secretly entered the competition as a participant. The V.I.P.s witnessed the fifth round, in which the sixteen remaining players were required to traverse a bridge composed of alternating tempered glass and regular glass panels—stepping on the wrong panel would result in a fatal fall.

During this time, Hwang Jun-ho infiltrates the observation area disguised as a waiter he had previously incapacitated. Seated next to the Panther Mask V.I.P., he becomes the target of the VIP’s flirtations. The V.I.P. demands that Jun-ho remove his disguise, but Jun-ho, seeking to avoid detection, suggests moving somewhere private instead. Once isolated, the V.I.P. attempts to assault Jun-ho; however, Jun-ho draws his weapon and compels the VIP to divulge all information he possesses, which Jun-ho records. Jun-ho then incapacitates the V.I.P. and escapes, leaving the VIP to be later discovered by a masked guard.     The VIPs watching Player 360 dying  They would later observe the final round of the Squid Game, in which Seong Gi-hun and Cho Sang-woo competed to determine the ultimate victor. When Gi-hun defeated Sang-woo but hesitated to claim victory, attempting to end the game prematurely, the V.I.P.s reacted with visible disbelief. Following Sang-woo’s suicide, which enabled Gi-hun to secure his win, the V.I.P.s departed the island and returned to their respective homes. Whether they were disappointed or satisfied with yet another successful and entertaining Squid Game remains unknown. 
Season 3[]
     The 37th Squid Game VIPs watching the vote after the 4th game while dining  
For the 37th Squid Game, a new cadre of VIPs, including a female member, was invited to observe the fifth and sixth games, consistent with prior protocol. Unlike the VIPs of the 33rd Squid Game, this delegation arrived prior to the fourth game, positioning themselves to actively participate in the execution of the game’s losers alongside the soldier contingent. They also monitored the voting process in real time via live display and partook in formal dining with Front Man, further underscoring their privileged integration into the operations of the Squid Game.
Prior to the fifth Squid Game round, the V.I.P.s collectively consented to recognize Kim Jun-hee's infant as a legitimate participant. Following Jun-hee’s death, they sanctioned Front Man’s proposal to enroll the child as Player 222, thereby formally integrating the infant into the competition.

Trivia[]
         Squid Game VIP Actor Reacts To 'Bad Acting' Complaints  
Curiously, the two competitors on whom VIP 4 placed wagers—Player 069 and Player 096—interacted with the final two players, Player 218 and Player 456, respectively, in ways that indirectly precipitated their deaths.
BloodCoin is a cryptocurrency developed and utilized by the V.I.P.s as the official betting currency for Squid Game events. Engineered to anonymize high-stakes wagers on player outcomes, Blood Coin operates on a private blockchain accessible exclusively to this elite cadre of ultra-wealthy individuals.[citation needed]
The performances of the V.I.P.s have been characterized as bizarre depictions of “depraved, affluent Western men.”  Several actors portraying the V.I.P.s reported that director Hwang Dong-hyuk specifically instructed them to adopt this exaggerated style. Due to spatial arrangements on set, they were positioned far apart and required to project their lines loudly to ensure audibility. Geoffrey Giuliano, the actor for VIP 4, offered to revise the dialogue to make it sound more natural; however, the offer was declined due to time constraints.  Additionally, Hwang Dong-hyuk instructed Mr. Giuliano not to ad-lib, despite his habitual improvisational approach to acting.
To date, only five V.I.P.s have been seen unmasked: VIP 4 from Season 1, and all V.I.P.s from Season 3 excluding VIP John.
According to Dong-hyuk, the Season 3 V.I.P.s, who participate as soldiers in the Hide and Seek game, exemplify extreme moral degradation. Their willingness to unmask symbolizes how the ultra-wealthy in contemporary society feel increasingly unconstrained by the need for anonymity.
In reality, the V.I.P.s are far more numerous than depicted in the series, as additional investors observe Squid Game franchises in other countries.  Given the variation in each annual Squid Game cohort and the extensive number of V.I.P.s, it remains uncertain which individuals across the three seasons were longstanding clients of Oh Il-nam, especially since some appear to be too young to have been active at the time of the game's inception.
In the Squid Archives room, there are two separate archives for the V.I.P.s visible in ⭘△☐. One being for the V.I.P.s for each edition of the games and one for "V.I.P Candidates".
The V.I.P.s in Season 3 did find out about the Revolt, despite In-ho had kept it under wraps, and seeing mostly the aftermath of it and the games being continued after it failed.
According to Bryan Bucco, the actor portraying VIP John, the dialogue of the Season 3 V.I.P.s was dubbed in English despite originally being performed in English.
