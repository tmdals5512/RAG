Game Information[]
Objective: The player in charge of this mini-game must kick the Jegi 5 times in a row with their foot to pass this mini-game. 
If the jegi touches the ground before the 5th kick or if the player catches the jegi with their hand, the count falls back to 0. 
According to what Hwang In-ho (001) did, another player is allowed to help to kick the the jegi as long as the jegi touches the fifth player's foot.
Notable players who participated in this mini-game include Player 100 (Im Jeong-dae), Player 120 (Cho Hyun-ju), Player 230 (Thanos), Player 333 (Lee Myung-gi), and Player 456 (Seong Gi-hun).

Trivia[]
The jegi can be kicked in any way as long as it touches your foot, there's no "correct" way to play this game.
Most of the team leaders played this game: Im Jeong-dae, Cho Hyun-ju, Thanos, and Seong Gi-hun.
Traditionally, the game is simply kicking the jegi the most times you can in a row, but in the series it's limited to 5 kicks.
Catching the jegi with your hand is allowed, but the kicks that you previously made won't count.
According to the BTS footage, this might be also applied in the games.[1]
The jegi can be kicked with both legs.
Similar to jegi, in China it is played with a feathered shuttlecock instead of a strip, called jianzi, and in Vietnam it is also known as đá cầu.
Out of all of the players in season three that have confirmed mini-games, Jegi is the most common, with five.
