Six-Legged Pentathlon (5인 6각 근대 5종 경기 Ohin uggak geundae ojong gyeonggi?) was the second game of the 37th Squid Game. It consisted of five children's games: Ddakji Chigi, Biseokchigi, Gonggi Nori, Paengi Doligi and Jegichagi, all to be completed within a 5-minute time limit. 
Players are divided into teams of 5 and must take turns completing all the mini-games. All 5 members of the team must advance around the track with their legs tied together. Each team member must complete one game. If a game is not completed, it must be tried again until it's finished. Any team that fails to complete all five games and cross the finish line within five minutes will be immediately executed.
365 players participated in this game. 110 players (22 teams; around 30.1%) were eliminated and 255 players (51 teams; around 69.8%) progressed to the next round. At this point in the competition, 201 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 198 and Player 254.
This game took place on November 2, 2024.

Game information
The game you will participate in today is a Six-Legged Pentathlon. Team members will start with their legs tied together. At each ten-meter interval on the track, one member of the team will play a mini-game. If that player wins, the team can move on to the next challenge. The mini-games are as follows: Game one, Ddakji. Game two, Flying Stone. Game three, Gonggi. Game four, Spinning Top. And finally, game five, Jegi. You will have five minutes. To win, you must complete all mini-games and cross the finish line before the time runs out.
The game explanation in "Six Legs"
     Players entering the Six Legged Pentathlon  
The game was played in an arena resembling a school playground, with the facade of a school on one wall, flags of various different countries hung from strings overhead and round rainbow racetracks on a sandy floor. The walls are very similar to the Dalgona game in many aspects: The ceiling is much higher due to additional walls sloping to the sides, and the walls look like they were drawn by children, with a faded light blue, clouds that appear to be drawn with crayons, and circle lamps in the sky with yellow lines around them, simulating the sun; although in the Dalgona game, the "sun rays" are curved lines drawn around instead of straight lines as in this game. The lamps in the sky also contain drawn rainbow circles around each, that correspond to the tracks in the floor. There are many fences in the lower walls, and the left and right walls contain small entrances for the Workers to enter and carry the bodies of eliminated players. On top of those entrances, there's a rainbow drawn with crayons and one cloud on each end. The "school windows" also contain many curtains, and some contain chalkboards with many drawings like children with their feet tied, music notes and sheets, times, markers, school schedules, rainbows, clouds, suns, a literal playground.
Players are given ten minutes to form a team of five members. Two teams will start with their legs tied together. At each ten-meter interval along the track, one team member will play a mini-game. If that player wins the mini-game, they can proceed to the next challenge.
Teams are given time to prepare before the game starts and decide which member will play which mini-game. Each team has five minutes to complete all mini-games and cross the finish line to win.

     Example of a team.  
Mini-games
Ddakji
     Ddakji  
The player in charge of this game must use one of the ddakji to flip the other ddakji over. Once the ddakji is flipped, the team passes this mini-game.

Biseokchigi
     Biseokchigi  
Also known as Flying Stone. The player in charge of this game must use a stone given by the guard to hit a small tombstone marked with three shapes by throwing it. Once the tombstone is knocked, the team passes this mini-game.

Gonggi
     Gonggi  
The player in charge of this game must perform a gonggi gameplay and complete all 5 levels by tossing and catching the stones according to the rules of each level. Once all 5 levels are cleared and the player catches all 5 stones at the end, the team passes this mini-game.

Paengi
     Paengi Doligi  
Also known as Spinning Top. The player in charge of this game must spin a top by wrapping a fiber string around the top and launching it by pulling the string. Once the top is spun on the ground, the team passes this mini-game.

Jegi
     Jegi  
Similar to Hacky Sack, The player in charge of this game must kick the jegi 5 times in a row with their foot. Once the jegi is kicked 5 times, the team passes this mini-game.

Trivia
Six-Legged Pentathlon is the only game that combines multiple games into one.
This also makes it the most complex game in the series, as there are many tasks that must be completed in order to pass this game.
Six-Legged Pentathlon is the only team game not to have a special lobby for the team forming before the game.
This game is played by two teams, one on each side of the circle, taking turns, so if the total playing time for both teams and the preparation time for each round is 10 minutes, it will take more than six hours to complete the game.
Unlike the other games that use a timer, the only way to die in this game is to run out of time.
During the game, teams of players completed race simultaneously. On the track on the left side of the arena facing the school, players walked clockwise, and ddakji was played by the outside player on the far left. On the track on the right side of the arena, players walked counterclockwise, and ddakji was played by the inside player on the far right.
Since there were only 73 teams, a single team presumably attempted to pass the game alone at some point.
Many women who played the Pentathlon who originally had their hair down tied it up to make the game easier. Notable players that had their hair originally down are Player 009, Hyun-ju, Jun-hee and Player 308.
The known cases where both teams were eliminated are Team 1 and Team 2, Player 234's team and Player 078's team, Team 70 and Team 71.
The Six-Legged Pentathlon and Sky Squid Game are the only games with original names.
According to fan theories, Seong Gi-hun’s team was deliberately placed to play last. This was intended so that if their team failed to complete the game, they could be quietly eliminated by the Pink Guards (excluding Hwang In-ho/Oh Young-Il, a.k.a. the Front Man) without being seen or noticed by any other players.
Lee Myung-gi and Park Gyeong-seok are the only main characters who passed this game without having any other main characters in their teams.
Many of the pentathlon teammates would later end up betraying each other, becoming enemies and even killing each other (such as Nam-gyu with Se-mi, Gi-hun with Dae-ho or Yeong-sam with Jeong-dae).
Alongside Dalgona, Tug of War, Marbles, and possibly Hide and Seek, this game is one of the few that, when passed, the passed players get sent back to the dormitory instead of waiting in the game area until it's over.
The quickest times to complete each mini-game on the first try were:
4 seconds for Ddakji (done by Player 016 and Kim Jun-hee).
9 seconds for Biseokchigi (done by Player 045 and Park Jung-bae).
16 seconds for Gonggi (done by Kang Dae-ho).
17 seconds for Paengi (done by Seon-nyeo on her last try).
6 seconds for Jegi (done by Cho Hyun-ju).
Based on this calculation, if a team completes each mini-game on their first try, and the time it takes the players to move from one point to another is approximately 8 seconds x 6 points, they can pass the Six-Legged Pentathlon within 1 minute and 40 seconds.

This is the only game of the 2024 edition where no player kills another player. Thanos was responsible for the deaths of three players in Red Light, Green Light, Hwang In-ho killed Sim Jae-seok in Mingle, 26 players were killed by another player in Hide and Seek, Players 276, 312 and 349 were killed by another player in Jump Rope (as well as the 11 players indirectly killed by 096,  Nam-gyu was indirectly killed by Park Min-su, and Kim Jun-hee's suicide), and all 8 players who died in Sky Squid Game were killed by another player or themselves. Very indirectly some players who fail at their mini-game could be considered responsible for the deaths of their team mates.
