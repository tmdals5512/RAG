Catch is the second game of the second season of Squid Game: The Challenge.
107 players participated in this game. 33 players were eliminated and 74 players progressed to the next round. At this point in the competition, 382 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 100, Player 118, Player 152, Player 272, Player 390 and Player 443.

Game information[]
Before the game begun, players were led into the White Room. In the White room, was 4 squares colored Purple, Blue, Pink and Yellow with 26 dots in their color within. Players were told to organize into 4 equal groups and given 1 minute.
The 3 excess players were allowed to sit out, with a catch. They must choose one player in the game to link with. If their linked player was eliminated, so were they. Here are what the excess chose.

111 chose to link with 437
118 chose to link with 152
082 chose to link with 383
The (non-excess) players were led into the game hall, which contained 4 diagonal lines of their color, with 26 dots on each. The groups must decide on an order to organize their players, from front of the line to the back. They received 5 minutes for deciding.
At the beginning of the game, a thrower is chosen and must stand on the center dot. From here, they must throw the tennis ball to someone at the beginning of a line. If the ball is caught, the thrower passes the game, moving to the bleachers, and the catcher becomes the next thrower. If the ball is dropped, the catcher and the thrower are eliminated. Each time the ball is dropped, a new one comes into play. There are 16 available balls, and if all of them are dropped then everybody remaining on the court pass the game.
As the game progresses, it gets harder as the lines get shorter and retract further away from the thrower.
Player 352, who received a disadvantage was given a tennis ball the size of a ping-pong ball, increasing her chances of elimination.
Player 024, who also received a disadvantage, had to perform his throw blindfolded.
