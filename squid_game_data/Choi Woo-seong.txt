History[]
Choi Woo-seong was born in Gangwon Province. He did not undergo any formal schooling on record and obtained a job as a salesman for a liquor store called Cannon House, which he worked for 12 years. He ended up in heavy debt after making a bad financial decision based on a friend's advice. He married a woman named Park Mi-hee and had a son named Choi Dong-guk, who went on to attend college.
In 2020, Choi Woo-seong met a man in a suit who offered to play ddjaki with him. He managed to beat him at the game, and the man then gave him a card with a phone number to join the Squid Games, along with 100,000 won. He made the call and gave the game his name and date of birth. He then went to an address given to him. Suddenly, a van showed up; He got in through a password, "red light green light." The driver was shown in a pink jumpsuit, but he had a gas mask. Suddenly, sleeping gas is released, making him fall asleep, and then the van drove off. 
He then woke up to classical music in a jumpsuit on a bunk bed, in a creepy warehouse called the "dormitory." Then, the same mysterious drivers, now known as "Guards," came in, telling him the instructions and that they were in a competition. The guards said he could win a "handsome cash prize." Some players were asking questions. and The guards answered, A guard with a square symbol on their mask tells them that the players are all living on the edge with debts that they simply cannot pay, so it made Choi Woo-seong earn the guards' trust. He then signed a contract and proceed to walk into a giant room with multiple staircases, with classical music following the instructions. He then took his picture, following the guards into the first game: Red Light, Green Light.
Choi Woo-seong could be seen confused and doubtful about the giant doll, and an announcer explained the game rules, and so, The game begun. He and many other players ran, though a player, Player 324, ran too fast and couldn't stop in time, so his elimination was announced, followed by a loud bang and him dropping to the ground. His best friend, Player 250, who was just right behind 324, went over and checked on 324, thinking 324 was playing a trick on him, but 324 coughed up blood, which made 250 scared and try to run away in panic, before getting shot in the back, with his blood landing on Player 306's face, which made her scream and get shot in the head.
Witnessing the death of 3 players, A lot of players tried to run to the door but were shot and eliminated from the game. Although Choi Woo-seong, along with many other players, managed to survive the initial chaos, and so the game continued, but Choi Woo-seong stood still and passed the finish line, but other players weren't fast enough and were killed for not making to the finish line in time.
Upon returning to the dormitory, Choi Woo-seong was scared and shook from witnessing so many deaths, until the guards barged into the room, which made Choi Woo-seong back away in fear. Some players started to protest about the game, until a guard grabbed his gun and sent out a warning shot in the air to stop the ruckus, which made Choi Woo-seong duck down to the floor, startled. the same guard then reminded the players about the first two clauses of the contract they signed, though another player, Player 218 (Cho Sang-woo), argued back about the third clause, which was that the players could vote whether to leave or stay in the game, and the guards accepted the request to begin a vote, however, the guards first showed the prize money accumulated in the first game, which made lots of players start questioning their choices, and so, the vote begun.
During the voting, the vote was leading more to the side to continue the games, so a player, Player 119 (No Sang-hun) questioned the "O" voters, which made another player, Player 322 (Jung Min-tae), strikes back with the argument that the games were his last chance in life, and 119 got mad and tried attacking 322, though the guards interrupted them and the voting continued. “After this, Choi Woo-seong voted to continue the games (O). After Player 002 (Mun Jang-ho) voted, the counter went into a tie. so the last player, Player 001 (Oh Il-nam), was the one to make the final decision, and decided to ultimately, end the games. Choi Woo-seong was disappointed, since he voted to continue the games, and he eventually came back into the games opportunity was given. Possibly disappointed that he didn’t continue in the games. 
After Choi Woo-seong came back into the games, he went into a giant room with multiple staircases to get to the second game, Sugar Honeycombs. He walked into the second game with 186 other players. He was looking around until the announcer instructed the players to choose one of the four shapes on the wall and line up in front of it. The four shapes were, the circle, the triangle, the star, and the umbrella. Choi Woo-seong was thinking about what shape he was gonna choose until he decided to choose the triangle shape. After he retrieved his shape and sat down, he started carving his triangle shape. He eventually passed the second game.
In the dormitory, Choi Woo-seong decided to team up with Player 014 and Player 186. While Choi Woo-seong went to bed, a fight broke out in the night, and he was caught right in the middle of it.

Fate[]
Woo-seong was killed during the Special Game, He was seen running away from Na Jo-eun until Na Jo-eun caught him to him, Na Jo-eun would then throw him down the stairs and beat him up, causing him to soon die from head trauma. Since he was last seen fighting him before he died, it’s likely to assume that he killed him.

Trivia[]
Choi Woo-seong shares his name with Player 112, the person who won the 6th Squid Game. It's possible, though extremely unlikely, that these two are the same person. When looking at the scenes of actor Kwon Jae-hoon and the subtle match in the son's birth year, the documents indicate that the previous winner is number 112, and winner number 112 and this Choi Woo-seong are namesakes.
According to his player file, Choi Woo-seong's resident registration number is 760523-1347785, and he lives in Dongchun-dong, Yeonsu-gu, Incheon.[4]
He and his counterpart in the 37th Squid Game were both killed by another player.
Coincidentally, Choi Woo-seong's wife, Park Mi-hee, has a very similar name to Park Mi-hwa, who was Player 006 in the 37th Squid Game.
Also, both had their elimination announced on the speaker onscreen.
Excluding 001, 006 is the only player to have their names known in both the 33rd and the 37th.
Considering the place where his corpse was, it's possible he tried to run to the doors to escape but was killed before he could reach them.
There is a possibility that he was killed by Na Jo-eun. It is more convincing to assume that he backed away to escape the fight or was pushed from the pillar to the stairs by Na Jo-eun than that he tried to escape through the door, and such behavior suggests that Na Jo-eun may have forcibly drawn Choi Woo-seong into the fight.
Judging by the screams and the scene right before death, he possibly ranked 93rd overall in the 33rd Squid Game.
He can be seen talking to Deok-su's three subordinates during Sugar Honeycombs, he may have tried to join Deoksu's gang. However, it seems he failed.
His name is incredibly similar to Choi Woong-seok, another player whose name was revealed through the files.
Mistakes[]
Choi Woo-seong's icon shows a woman, even though he is portrayed by a man physically and in his player file.
Choi Woo-seong's icon is the same as Player 279's faulty icon.
Choi Woo-seong's actual face is Jo Su-jin's and Player 276's icon.
In his player file, Choi Woo-seong's wife and son were born on August 1, 1953 and November 17, 1973 but are listed as being 40 and 20 years old, respectively.
Furthermore, the file states that Woo-seong fell into debt on February 1, 1991, when he would have been a minor and legally unable to enter into any financial contracts. The reason for these errors are unknown. If you insert the winning year of the 6th Squid Game winner, No. 112 Choi Woo-sung, into this error, the ages match perfectly, so it is presumed that the records for actor Yeo In-seop and Choi Woo-sung were mistakenly taken from No. 112 Choi Woo-sung.
Behind the scenes[]
Player 006 (Choi Woo-seong) was portrayed by Kwon Jae-hun, Yeo In-seop and two unknown extras.
