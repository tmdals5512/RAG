Personality[]
But then, how do I tell which is the tempered one?
Player 096 asking a Manager for advice on how to pass Glass Stepping Stones in VIPS
Player 096 has always been a cowardly man who has hid behind people. In the 5th game, though, he wishes to change his attitude and take the lead. His attitude quickly though understandably reverts to cowardly once he realizes what the game is.

History[]
Player 096 was in heavy debt and chose to enter the Squid Games to win money. After waking up in a large room with 455 other people, he signed a contract and took his photo. He managed to survive the first game, Red Light, Green Light, after most of the contestants had been wiped out. Afterwards, like everyone else, he was shaken by the deaths of the contestants, when the prize money was finally revealed. A vote was held if the game should continue. His vote is unknown as we don't see him voting, but its presumed he voted X due his personality, still unconfirmed. The majority voted no. However, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players.
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs, and the third game, Tug of War, and beat his partner, Player 229 in the fourth game Marbles, and survived until the fifth game, Glass Stepping Stones. For the game, the remaining players each had to choose a number beforehand. When Player 456 wanted to take number 1, Player 096 asked to let him take it instead, wanting to take control and make his own decision in life for once. However, it was soon revealed that the game was Glass Stepping Stones and as he chose the number 1, he would be the first to go.  
As such, the odds were completely against him, making his decision an extremely poor one with hindsight.

Fate[]
He managed to guess the first panel correctly, and went on to the second, where he jumped onto the wrong panel and fell to his death.

Trivia[]
Both him and his season 2 and 3 counterpart made it as far as the fifth game and died in it.
Funny enough, the deaths of both were related to Seong Gi-hun. Although this Player 096's death was an indirect and unintentional death.
If Player 096 had maintained his cowardly attitude, he would have most likely been a finalist.
He used to be often mistaken with Park Jung-bae. It is confirmed by director Hwang Dong-hyuk that he is actually Jung-bae's long-lost twin brother.
Given his haggard and tired expression before the Glass Bridge game it is possible he was very close to his Marbles team-mate Player 229.
