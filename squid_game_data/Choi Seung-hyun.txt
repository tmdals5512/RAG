Choi Seung-hyun (최승현 Choe Seung-hyeon?), famously known as T.O.P, is a rapper, singer, songwriter and actor who played Thanos/Player 230 in Season 2 and Season 3 of the Netflix TV series Squid Game.[1] He first gained international recognition as the lead rapper of the group BIGBANG before establishing a solo career characterized by his distinct low-register delivery. In addition to his music, he has developed a significant acting career, appearing in various films and high-profile series like Squid Game. 

Background
T.O.P. debuted in 2006 as a member of BIGBANG, contributing as a lead rapper and songwriter to the group’s international commercial success. Beyond group activities, he released solo singles such as "Turn It Up" (2010) and "Doom Dada" (2013), and collaborated on the subunit project GD & TOP. In 2022, T.O.P ended his 16-year contract with YG Entertainment, later confirming his official departure from BIGBANG in 2023. He began his acting career in 2007 with the drama I Am Sam and later gained recognition for his role in the spy thriller Iris (2009), opposite future Front Man actor Lee Byung-hun. He has held leading roles in several South Korean films, including 71: Into the Fire (2010), Commitment (2013), and Tazza: The Hidden Card (2014).
In 2017 (seven years prior to his casting in Season 2), T.O.P, had pled guilty to drug use and was blacklisted in the Korean music industry; during that period, he also overdosed and was hospitalized as a result.[2] During a hiatus from the public eye, he reportedly composed over 100 songs, including the 2025 release "Doom Dada 2.0," which marked his return as a solo artist. Following a multi-year break from the screen, he returned to acting in late 2024 as part of the cast for the second season of the Netflix series Squid Game, playing the character "Thanos", a contestant who is also a rapper that shown to have a drug problem. His character drew ire and backlash from some fans but received praise from others, with the character becoming an internet meme and well regarded character within the Squid Game fanbase.[3]
Hwang Dong-hyuk for his part praised Seung-hyun for the courage to return to the public spotlight after nine years and to portray a character with similar negativities as Seung-hyun had in his own life, saying "Despite the long hiatus, I have to say, as a director, he performed very impressively and I’m very satisfied with what he did with the character."[4] He reprised the role in the penultimate episode of the third season, as a hallusination exerpeicned by Park Min-su.
On January 15, 2025, T.O.P gave his first interview since 2014, where he admitted to The Korea Times that the role of Thanos struck an uncomfortable chord with him, as the character mirrored aspects of his past that he would rather keep hidden. He also revealed that the fear of being typecast in one role made him hesitant to take on the part. 
In preparing for his portrayal of Thanos, T.O.P focused on the complexities of a character heavily reliant on drugs. "The drug that Thanos uses in the series is extremely potent, so I researched the effects of such substances while developing the character," he explained. Through this research, he discovered that individuals dependent on powerful drugs often suffer significant dental damage and experience intense anxiety and symptoms similar to ADHD when not under the influence. 
T.O.P said that he “tried to act differently in scenes where Thanos had not yet used the drug versus those where he had.” He also added physical details to his performance, portraying Thanos with damaged teeth and adopting a rhythm that deviated from the norm. 
Additionally, T.O.P noted that "there’s a style of rap in southern U.S. hip-hop called ‘mumble rap,’ often associated with rappers who use powerful stimulants. I tried to channel that in the way Thanos delivers his lines."[5]
