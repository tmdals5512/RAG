Personality
Na Jo-eun is shown to be greedy, as he was seen looking at the prize money increase happily and he voted O and raged once the games were terminated. He is shown to also be violent in pursuit of financial gain, as he brutally throws Choi Woo-seong down a flight of stairs during lights out and beats him to death, as well as also beating Player 409 to death despite the fact both of them never made any attempt to harm him whatsoever, also highlighting his greediness as he did all of this for financial gain.
However, despite this, he is shown to be quite loyal to his allies, as when he sees an unknown player attacking one of his friends, he strikes the player on the head with a double hammerfist strike and stomps them to death in order to save the friend. Also, when he sees Player 397 fighting Player 307, he joins in and helps 307 against 397.
Jo-eun seems to like fights, pushing through the crowd of players to watch Jang Deok-su and Kang Sae-byeok fight, even getting extremely close to them during their arguments and fight. He can also be seen watching Player 271's death with hardly any emotion, with his friend.
Another example of his violence and bloodthirstiness is shown when he was one of the first few players to fight during the special game, he can be seen talking with his group before the Special Game, likely planning to attack, this can further be proved as we see 307 also raising from his bed to attack.
His loyalty is also highlighted during Red Light, Green Light, as he sticks with Park Pil-sam and Jang Seung-jong throughout the whole game.
Overall, he is shown to be greedy, bloodthirsty and extremely violent, but at the same time is also very loyal to those who are his friends.

History
Na Jo-eun was in tremendous debt at some point in his life and played Ddakji with a man in a suit to win some cash. After successfully winning Ddakji, he was given a card to enter the Squid Games for a chance to win the prize money. Later on, a van arrived, where he got in with the passcode "Red Light, Green Light". Once he got in the van, sleeping gas was released into the van, and he fell asleep as the van drove off. 
After waking up to classical music in the dormitory with 455 other players. He got down from his bed and witnessed Jang Deok-su and Kang Sae-byeok's confrontation. Afterwards, he listened to the guards, who promised a large cash prize of 45.6 billion won if they could pass through all 6 games without being "eliminated". After hearing this, he signed the consent form, where he was in the line in the middle of Seong Gi-hun and Oh Il-nam. He then had his photo taken, and went upstairs to the first game, Red Light, Green Light.  
When the doors opened, Na Jo-eun appeared confused upon seeing the giant doll. Once the game started, he watched as Player 324 and Player 250 raced each other. He then saw as 324 ran too fast and got caught moving. Suddenly, a loud bang was heard, and 324 dropped to the floor. On the next green light, 250 walked closer to 324, and watched the blood come out of his mouth. He suddenly realized the severity of the situation he was in, and ran towards the doors - being shot Infront of Player 306. 306 watched the blood get all over her, and screamed, leading to her being shot.  
Suddenly, chaos erupted as many players ran back toward the doors and were shot. Na Jo-eun was scared, yet was not one of the ones who ran towards the doors. This led to him surviving the initial chaos. He then slowly but carefully moved towards the finish line. 
If he did survive Red Light, Green Light, he would've watched as those who didn't pass the game in time got shot, and then after the game ended and he returned to the Dormitory, he was left to sit there shocked. Suddenly, the guards charged in. Many players were shocked, and complained. A vote was then called by Player 218 after he argued about Clause 3 of the consent form. In the vote, he chose to press O, and was enraged when the Xs won the vote, throwing punches out of anger. It is unknown who he was dropped off with, but we do know he chose to return to the games once given an invite back.  
If he returned, after waking up, he groups up with Park Pil-sam, Player 034 and Jang Seung-jong, and talks about returning to the games, in which Park Pil-sam points out that the number of players who returned is the same 187 has. They then moved on to their next game, Sugar Honeycombs. Unaware of what the game was yet, he chatted with his ally Player 403 and decided to pick either star or circle, he would most likely manage to pass the game before the allotted time ended or without breaking it, as he is seen at the end rubbing his face after showing his finished shape.   
After the game, if he survived it, he gets his food and watches as Jang Deok-su beats Player 271 to death. He then goes to bed that night and wakes up to fighting. He participated in the brawl and was one of the first players to attack, beating an unknown player to death with his bare hands and then tackling and wrestling with an unknown player in the middle of the dorms. Na Jo-eun would then be seen attacking 2 or 3 players at once and knocking 2 of them down onto the ground after striking them. He would then be seen beating Choi Woo-seong to death mercilessly. He would then move to the right side of the dorms, attacking and killing an unknown player by using a double hammer fist strike on him then stomping on him. Once the fight ends, he is erroneously seen fighting with Player 409, he managed to overpower and drop 409 before being forced to put his hands up. If he survived, Player 187 would then surrender and talk to 3 new friends of his. Jo-eun would then meet back up with Players 307 and 403, and would go to sleep beside them.   
If he survived, the next morning, he made his way up to the third game, where he was tasked to get into groups of ten. He is seen in Team Eight, however this is erroneous, as this was supposed to be Do Jeong-soo's team. Afterwards, they walk into the real game hall, where they find out what the next game was - Tug of War. 

Fate
“Since a single scene is usually filmed in multiple takes, once an actor is captured on screen, the same actor has to continue playing that role. Na Jo-eun also had a minor role with no lines, but because he left a strong impression, he continued filming alongside the lead actors. In the end, the schedule went well beyond the planned 4 nights and 5 days, and Player 187 disappears from the screen during the ‘marbles’ scene. To make matters worse, on a day when Na Jo-eun was away for another filming schedule, a stuntman who was filming an elimination scene happened to be wearing Player 187’s outfit and got shot. When I went to the next shoot, the staff explained the situation to me, saying that my character had died. I was really disappointed, but the production team gave me the role of a Masked Man. In fact, after that, I ended up doing more acting while wearing a mask. When acquaintances ask me about it, I joke that I didn’t die and was scouted to become a Masked Man. (laughs) Since the Masked Man role lets me act in a wider variety of situations, I liked it, even though my face doesn’t show.”
Actor Na Jo-eun speaking during an interview. 
It is unknown where he died. He physically appears up until Tug of War with his main actor. His icon faded between Tug of War and Marbles, and his actor Na Jo-eun and the staff, said he died in Marbles and wasn't on set for Marbles due to the filming taking longer, and that the tag 187 was used in Marbles. This information was confirmed in an interview. However, he was seen running out of time in Red Light, Green Light in BTS footage, but does not run out of time in the actual show and crosses the finish line. his death in BTS footage is likely erroneous due to him being seen here with a different actor than his canonical one, as well as the fact his character appears multiple times after Red Light, Green Light. Leaving his fate up to pure interpretation.

Trivia
His name was confirmed in a interview where Hee-sang talked about his experience in Squid Game, as he confirms that during the consent form scene he signed Na Jo-eun.
Na Jo-eun seems to have had a scrapped death scene, due to the interview stating that he was killed on-screen with a stuntman.
He’s one of the few minor characters who had known actors instead of having speaking roles. His actor, Na Jo-eun, was arrested for drink driving.
Player 187 is one of the most consistent minor players in Season 1 with their actor. Other examples of this include Player 014, Player 075, Player 236, Player 311, Player 312, Player 313, Player 397, Player 447 and Player 450.
Player 187 is seen extremely close to his victims before the Special Game, with Player 187 being near Choi Woo-seong in Sugar Honeycombs, and Player 409 being right behind Player 187 in voting.
Player 187 is one of the most social minor players through out the games.
Player 187 has a habit of holding his hips.
Player 187's erroneous actor in Red Light, Green Light is the actor of Player 129, funnily enough Player 187 and Player 129 share icons.
In the BTS Footage of Red Light, Green Light, another Player 187 is seen running out of time. However, this is not in the show. Although, he is seen not to cross the finish line when Ali Abdul carries Gi-hun with his hand.
He was very close friends with Park Pil-sam; they were beside each other before Red Light, Green Light, and both of their erroneous actors were beside each other in Red Light, Green Light, and they were with each other in the
Player 187 was seen moving towards the shapes multiple times in Sugar Honeycombs, but he circles back multiple times weirdly enough.
Player 187 was announced in the German dub to be eliminated in the Special Game, this is likely an error.
His number, 187, is shown among the numbers of players before Sugar Honeycombs, his best friend, Park Pil-sam, points this out by pointing at his chest then the numbers.
A player named Cho Su-jin won the 5th Squid Games in 1992 with this number.
Funnily enough, the player, Cho Su-jin, was on the same error team as Jo-eun.
Player 187 is one of the only players confirmed to have gotten 3 kills during the Special Game. This makes him a serial killer.
Ironically, he was seen carving his dalgona next to one of his victims, Choi Woo-seong in Sugar Honeycombs, and stood next to another victim, Player 409 in the voting.

Behind the scenes
Na Jo-eun was portrayed by Yoo Hee-sang, an unknown extra during Red Light, Green Light, and Lee Kwang-ho during Marbles. Na Jo-eun seemed to be exceptionally close with Park Pil-sam's actor, Kwak Jang-ryun, during filming. The proof of this is that Kwak Jang-ryun and Yoo Hee-sang are seen with each other before the first game, during the voting, after returning, and possibly in the second game. Yoo Hee-sang would be seen with Kwak Jang-ryun even after he changed his tag to 307 and would assist him in the Special Game. Na Jo-eun would also be with him for Tug of War when he was 019.
Hee-sang would confirm in an interview that he signed his actor alias on the consent form. he also confirmed in an interview that he died in Marbles and was a guard when Jun-ho was shot.
