Personality[]
Just keep your damn prize money I don't care! Let me just go home that's all I want!
Player 066 begging to a Manager in "Hell"
While not much is known about him, his time spent in Squid Games shows that he values his life more than the money they offered. Furthermore, he was one of many to confront the employees about their style of elimination and decided to not return after voting for the Squid Games' termination.

History[]
Player 066 was in heavy debt and decided to participate in the Squid Games. After waking up in a large room with 455 other players, he signed a contract and took his photo. He and 200 other contestants survived the first game Red Light, Green Light and was disgusted and scared by the style of elimination the Manager was talking about. When he was in main lounge with the remaining players, he stood in front when the manager and employees congratulated them for winning the first round. He was one of few contestants to speak out against the manager. He demanded the manager take him home and keep the money. He also agreed with No Sang-hun that police will be tracking their phones to save them (although the Guards most likely have some way of preventing that). Fortunately, Cho Sang-woo remembered the third clause which allows the players to vote on terminating the Squid games. He and 100 other contestants voted to terminate the Squid Game and he celebrated with those who were excited that the games were over. He was sent back to his hometown and did not return.

Fate[]
He was one of the 14 contestants to not return to Squid Game after the voting. The front man orders one of his manager to supervise those who did not return.

Trivia[]
Actor Won Choon-gyu is only credited for his appearance in "Hell". Won Choon-gyu did not return to do more episodes after episode two. Furthermore, no extras wore the 066 suit in episode 3 or later. This provides more evidence of Player 066 leaving the Squid Games after game's initial termination.
A player named Sin Seong-yong won the 11th Squid Games in 1998 with this number. This means that his number is the only confirmed one that have survived the games twice.
Mistakes[]
Player 066's icon is gone in Red Light, Green Light.
He was portrayed by a different male during the first game. (as seen in Behind the Scenes)

Behind the scenes[]
Player 066 was portrayed by Won Choon-gyu and 2 unknown extras. Won Choon-gyu was credited as "시위자" (siwija) in Korean, which translates to "protester”.
