Personality[]
No number can determine when the Lord calls for somebody.
Player 244 to Player 151 during Glass Stepping Stones in VIPS
Player 244 is noted to be a very religious Christian and frequently prays to God even in desire situations, asking for his assistance and for blessings. He seems to get irritated easily when others deny the existence of God, a good example would be when Ji-yeong says that God didn't help their team and it was Cho Sang-woo and Oh Il-nam that did.
Additionally, he displays clearly sexist and conservative attitudes by agreeing with 196 about not choosing women as teammates in Marbles, basing his decision on the biblical story of the birth of Eve to justify himself and reinforce roles of weakness towards the women on his team.

You're right. There's a reason why God created Eve from Adam's rib. God has given men and women...different roles and uses.
Player 244 to Han Mi-nyeo in the White Room before Marbles in Gganbu
History[]
Player 244 was suffering in heavy debt he couldn't pay off and chose to enter the Squid Games to win money. After waking up in a large room with 455 other people, he signed a contract and took his photo. He managed to survive the first game, Red Light, Green Light. Afterwards, like everyone else, he was shaken by the deaths of the contestants, when the prize money was finally revealed. A vote was held if the game should continue, but the majority voted no. However, those who were willing to return could, and he returned, along with most of the other players.
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs, and although he is not seen, the announcement of him passing the game can be heard over the intercoms. Before the third game, Tug of War, Seong Gi-hun offered Player 244 to join his team. Player 244 accepts and joins Team 4 alongside Cho Sang-woo (Player 218), Ali Abdul (Player 199), Kang Sae-byeok (Player 067), Oh Il-nam (Player 001), Ji-yeong (Player 240), Han Mi-nyeo (Player 212), Player 196 and Player 276. their team faced Team 5, and Team 5 was overpowering them with their brute force and strength. However, thanks to Il-nam's strategy and Sang-woo's last-minute trick, they managed to win.
After Tug of War, 244 prayed to God to thank him that the team managed to survive, but Ji-yeong mocked 244, who was thanking God for being alive and stated that it was thanks to Il-nam and Sang-woo that they were still alive, 244 declared that God chose for them to live on and told her to thank him. Back in the lobby, Player 244 and Player 196 noticed that their team was the weakest and both suggested to attack another weak team by surprise.
After waking up and heading to the next arena, he partnered with Player 196 in the fourth game Marbles, and won against him. Making Player 244 survive until the fifth game, Glass Stepping Stones. For the game, he chose Number 06, which meant he would be the sixth to go across the bridge.

Fate[]
Thank you. God bless me today.
Player 244's last words before being pushed off by Player 407 in "VIPS"
When it was Player 021's turn, he told him to go right while Players 453 and 151 told him to go left. Player 021 decided to go left and as a result, he fell and got eliminated. When it was Player 244's turn, he started praying. Player 151 forced Player 244 to do something or else he would get pushed. 151 in response tried to push him off, but failed to do so and was pushed off by Player 244. Player 244 grinned from finding the right glass panel. He jumped onto that panel and started to pray again to thank his lord. Player 407 wasted no time to push Player 244 to the incorrect panel, resulting in Player 244's death.

Trivia[]
Player 244's scream as he falls to his death is re-used by Nam-gyu during Jump Rope in Season 3.
His player number may be a reference to Daniel 2:44 that states, "And in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed: and the kingdom shall not be left to other people, but it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever." The kingdom referring to the games.
In an interview, his actor said that he was an atheist, therefore not religious even though his character was religious.[2]
Player 244 is one of the only unnamed characters, and characters overall, to have his icon in the security room be consistent with his character, with other members of Team Four and Jang Deok-su's gang, including Byeong-gi, having random extras. This is most likely due to him being cast much earlier in the filming process compared to other actors, which allowed a correct icon to be inserted before an erroneous one would have taken its place.
Player 244 is one of the few supporting characters to not have his name listed. Another player to not have his name listed is Player 196, his closest friend in the team.
Coincidentally, both of their Season 2 counterparts were players of the opposite gender who died in Red Light, Green Light.
Player 244's Season 2/3's counterpart would be Seon-nyeo (Player 044) since they are both religious and make comments regarding their gods. However, Seon-nyeo is a shaman and Player 244 is a former pastor; both also drastically differ in terms of personality.
Interestingly, they have the same number that ends with the number 44.
In another funny coincidence both of those players are killed by the player who makes it to 9th place in their respective Squid Games [Player 407 and Player 125 Min-su]. Player 244 died in the same game as his killer while Min-su outlived Seon-nyeo by two games.
He and Player 250 are the only non-main character of season one to not be played by any extras in any way.
He placed 10th place, his Season 2 counterpart is Player 306.
Coincidentally both are very religious characters.
During Glass Stepping Stones, when Player 151 is trying to get him to move, he says the line, 'there is no order in which God calls us.' This refers to the fact that he (number 06) kills 151 (07), who would be thought to die after him, showing that despite their numbers, their deaths are not in any order.
If you look very closely a few light tear drops can be seen in his eyes when he realises it is his turn to guess the Glass Tiles next.
Despite his later enmity with Player 407 they are actually the characters who make the same guess Player 062 took for the fifth panel jump in Glass Bridge: they both guessed Player 062 went to the right-side tile on that jump-and they turned out to guess correctly when Player 021 jumps left and immediately falls and dies.
He displays somewhat misogynistic views against Ji-yeong, Mi-nyeo and Sae-byok before the Marbles game begins, saying it is very unwise to choose a girl partner before the next game even though it is unlikely that two games in a row will have a strong importance on physical strength.
Ironically despite that and being a very devout Christian pastor his team is actually quite religiously diverse [I'M NOT SAYING THIS TO CAUSE ARGUMENTS OR RELIGIOUS CONTROVERSY I'M JUST MAKING NOTES]: Ali Abdul [Player 199] is almost definetely a Muslim as his wife was seen wearing a hijab, Seong Gi-hun/Player 456 claims to be a Buddhist in the first episode of the show [specifically saying he did not believe in Jesus], Ji-yeong/Player 240 is a quite outspoken atheist and there is a fairly good chance though not definete that Player 276 is also a Muslim being from Pakistan. Of the others on the team Sae-byok placed her brother in a Christian children's home, Cho Sang-woo/Player 218, Han Mi-nyeo/Player 212 and especially Oh Il-nam/Player 001 are quite unlikely to be religious and Player 196 is hard to guess.
