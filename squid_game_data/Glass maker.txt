Do Jeong-soo (도정수 Do Jeong-su?), also known as Player 017, was a former glass manufacturer and participant in the 33rd Squid Game, as well as one of the few semifinalists in the competition.

History[]
Do Jeong-soo was born on June 23, 1965 in Gyeonggi Province, South Korea. He began attending Bucheon Industrial High School in 1981, later graduating in 1984.[1] He briefly worked a position in construction management before getting a job at a glass factory in 1987, where he continued to work for the next 33 years. 
In the early 1990s, he met his future wife Kim Eun-sook. On July 12th, 1993, Do Jeong-soo's first daughter, Do Ji-hye was born. Later, on November 11th, 1995, his second daughter, Do Ji-yeon was born. 
In 2019, he began falling into debt and had his driver's license suspended after being caught drunk driving multiple times. He was fired from his job the following year as a result. He was unable to find new work and his debt continued increasing, so he chose to enter the Squid Games to win money.
After joining, Jeong-soo managed to survive the first game, Red Light, Green Light, where most of the contestants had been eliminated. Afterwards, he was sent home when a narrow majority of the remaining players voted to end the games. Though his vote is not known it is somewhat probable he voted 0 due to apparently passing Red Light, Green Light first and being capable and quick-thinking in the games. He later returned along with most of the other players.
He managed to survive the second game, Sugar Honeycombs, the third game, Tug of War, and fourth game, Marbles, where he defeated his partner and forced opponent, Player 413 [he was probably the second player to pass this game because Player 413 is the second player called out as eliminated]. Before playing Marbles Han Mi-neyo could not find a partner. When she started to freak out, she went up to Player 081 and Do Jeong-soo begging one of them to partner up. The two looked disturb from her and decided to walk away while Jeong-soo pushed her away. He managed to survive until the fifth game, Glass Stepping Stones. For this game, he chose the number 13, which meant he would be the thirteenth player to go onto the tiles.

Fate[]
I can't tell what it is.https://squid-game.fandom.com/wiki/File:Skillissue.mp3
Do Jeong-soo last words before Sang-woo pushed him off in "VIPS"During Glass Stepping Stones, Jeong-soo could differentiate between the tempered glass and the regular glass based on the refraction of the light, gained from his experience of being a glass manufacturer. Despite this, he chose to keep this information to himself and waited until all players in front of him were dead to tell the remaining players behind him about his skill, fearing to be killed by the others if he had revealed it earlier.
When it was his turn, Jeong-soo finally used his strategy to get to the last glass panel before the lights were turned off. He used the marble of Player 456 to try and tell through the sound that it made and wanted another one to hear the other panel just in case. However, time was running out, and Player 218 pushed Player 017 off the bridge to his death for taking too long guessing the last glass panel, placing him 4th in the entire competition.

Personality[]
Why would I? The players up here tried to kill us at every chance.https://squid-game.fandom.com/wiki/File:Jeongsoomean.mp3
Do Jeong-soo explaining to Seong Gi-hun why he didn't explain his glass-making skills to the other players during Glass Stepping Stones in VIPS
Do Jeong-Soo was able to use his past skills from his former occupations, which was especially shown in Glass Stepping Stones as he can differentiate the glass panels in the game. He is shown to be a quick thinker in rough circumstances as he quickly finds alternative solutions when the not being able to see the refraction of the light after Frontman dimmed all the lights off in the game.
Although objectively selfish, he understandably wasn't willing to help the first twelve players in the game due to him being paranoid that he would be only used as a crutch and not be accounted for.

Trivia[]
He is the first Player to pass Red Light, Green Light, albeit with an erroneous actor which is quite impressive given his older age and build. If he had been able to get past the last panel somehow he would have been the first player to pass two different games [he was also the second to pass the Marbles game]. This also makes him the first player to pass the first game and the first player to die in the first five games.
It is true that he could have saved himself if he used the marble first on his glass panel and then on either panel in front of him to see if they made the same/different sound. However, there is a high likelihood that the sound of his own panel would have been different due to that he was standing on it and thus creating tension in the glass' structure.
He was the last side character player to die in season 1.
Do Jeong-soo is paranoid that he could be betrayed in the last game did not tell everyone about his skills and ironically he died because he told Sang-woo about his skills, so he killed him later
According to his player file, Do Jeong-soo's resident registration number is 650623-1205714, and he resides in 329 Songnae-dong, Bucheon. His wife, Kim Eun-sook, works at a restaurant. His older daughter Ji-hye works at an unnamed company, while his younger daughter is unemployed.
He is one of only 2 relevant players to have their name revealed through the files, the other being Park Jae-cheol
His number appears to be an unlucky number in Italy, which no other major character have this feature.
He shares a number with a finalist, Vanessa of Canada, in Squid Game The Challenge 2 and in an incredible coincidence they both placed 4th in the Games [Vanessa was the first finalist taken out in the sixth game]. Both also talk in some detail about their jobs before entering the Games, Jeong-soo about his experience in glass-making and Vanessa about her impressively being one of very few fisher-women in Canada and the only one in her local harbor.
In the event that Deok-su held to his bluff and refused to move even when the timer had run very low it is possible Jeong-soo would have volunteered to go first on the panels, using his skill to tell the remaining panels apart, to save his own life.
Do Jeong-soo has several similarities to Player 062: -Both were apparently the leaders of their teams based on the positioning in front of the group before Marbles. -Both could tell exactly three panels correctly on the Glass Bridge before dying on the fourth. -Both interact with Gi-hun individually and are seen as arrogant and/or selfish, while somewhat true they also had some fair reasons [Player 062 would have been right in most cases in guessing Oh Il-nam would be the player no one wanted to pair with and Do Jeong-soo was possibly right in thinking the other players could push him and/or something else could go wrong if he volunteered to go first on the bridge]. -Both had relatively sophisticated jobs, Player 062 in being a math teacher and Do Jeong-soo in being a glass manufacturer and both had skills from those jobs that helped them in the Glass Bridge game. -Both passed the Marbles game quite early, their opponents [Player 413 and Player 130] are respectively the second and third players called out to be eliminated in that game. In a contrast/parallel, Player 062 is likely one of the youngest players to reach Glass Bridge [possibly in his early 30s, with only Sae-byeok and possibly Player 021 being younger than him] while Do Jeong-soo is probably the second oldest player to reach the Glass Bridge game [his file says he is age 55, while he looks a little more haggard this could be due to alcohol abuse] behind Ms. Player 453. Also their player numbers both add up to the number 8.
He passed 17 tiles in the Glass Stepping Stones game, the same as his player number.
He was likely the second player to pass the Marbles game since the list of players eliminated in that game is called out with Player 089 first and Player 413 second with Player 413 being the one he is seen partnering with before the Marbles game.
Since Do Jeong-soo placed 4th in the Squid Games competition he made it to the top 1 percent of players.
It is somewhat probable he voted 0 in the first vote due to probably passing Red Light, Green Light first and being capable and quick-thinking in the games.
His second daughter's name, Ji-yeon, is very similiar to the name of Ji-yeong/Player 240.
It is uncertain if he would have been punished/executed by the Front Man after the game for having an advantage if he survived. However given the VIPs only referred to it as ''no fun'' and not strictly cheating there is a high probability they would let him live due to him just using skill to survive the game.
He and Player 453 are the oldest players to make it to Glass Stepping Stones.
Funnily enough, they were in the same Tug of War team.
If the players had actually taken a vote after the Marbles game, either with no prize money or with some, if the vote was in the same pattern as the first vote he would have been the last of the players to vote since as Player 017 he has the lowest player number of a survivor. Hence if the vote were a tie leading up to this at 8-8 he could have had the chance to make the sole decision to continue or stop the games.
Mistakes[]
A blurb on his file states that he worked as a glass worker from March 1997 to April 2020, in direct conflict with the rest of his file, which states that he started in 1987 and stopped working in January, not April.
There is a continuity error in VIPS where he is listed as Player 087 instead of Player 017.
In the VIPs icon he doesn't smile, but in the regular icon grid he does smile.
The 3rd Player 017 you see in Episode 5 was portrayed by an unknown extra, The main Player 017 was portrayed by Lee Sang-hee.
Another Player 017 is seen behind Gi-hun at the start of the initial chaos.
The other Player 017 is seen in the lobby.
Although he is seen partnering with Player 413, he is seen with Player 081 when Han Mi-nyeo begs Jeong-soo to be his partner. It's unknown which one is canon.
In a massive continuity error, he and his team didn't appear in Tug of War.
Behind the scenes[]
Do Jeong-soo was portrayed by Lee Sang-hee, Lee Soo-gil and two unknown extras.
In an interview, he stated that when he got the script for the show, he grew out his hair because he was reminded of the glass craftsmen that worked at a nearby glass factory when he was young, who had similar hair.
