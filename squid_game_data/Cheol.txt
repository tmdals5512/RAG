Kang Cheol (강철 Gang Cheol?) is the younger brother of Kang Sae-byeok and the adoptive child of Cho Sang-woo's mother.

History[]
Cheol was born in 2011 in North Hamgyong Province, North Korea, as the youngest child of his family. His older brother and his maternal grandparents died in a plague. 
When his family attempted to defect to South Korea, Cheol's father died in the process, and his mother survived but was unable to cross the border successfully. Cheol and his sister Sae-byeok, however, managed to reach the South, but Cheol was left in an orphanage due to absences of his father and mother. Sae-byeok was unable to care for him due to her participation in the Squid Game (which she entered in order to bring their mother down from the North and buy a house for them all to live in).
In 2021, Seong Gi-hun picks an eleven-year-old Cheol up at the orphanage and introduces himself as Sae-byeok's good friend. Cheol asks him if he knows where his sister is, but Gi-hun doesn't answer. Gi-hun brings Cheol to Sang-woo's mother so she can take care of him, as well as giving them a suitcase full of money. By doing this, he fulfills his promises to both of the other 33rd Squid Game finalists. He remains in her care until May 2025, when Cheol finally reunites with his mother at the airport.

Trivia[]
Kang Cheol has a single character in his first name, a trait shared with Choi Hyuk.
He, Seong Gi-hun, Hwang In-ho, Hwang Jun-ho, Park Man-cheol, Cho Sang-woo's mother, Kang Sae-byeok, Park Jung-bae, and Gyeong-taek are the only characters to appear in all 3 seasons of Squid Game.
Out of all the kids that appeared in the show like Seong Ga-yeong, Park Na-yeon, Seong Ga-yeong's Stepbrother, Ali Abdul's Son and Kim Jun-hee's Daughter, he is the oldest one among them.
