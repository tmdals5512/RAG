Tug of War  (줄다리기 Juldaligi?) was the 3rd game of the 33rd Squid Game held in 2020. The game consisted of two teams of ten playing tug of war across two raised platforms. the goal was for the winning team to pull the losing team off their platform, resulting in them falling to their deaths leading to their eliminations.
80 players participated in this game. 40 players (50%) were eliminated and the other 40 players (50%) progressed to the next round, 416 players in total were eliminated.
Notable eliminations include Player 194, Player 204, Player 241, Player 245, and Player 410.
This game took place on June 27, 2020.

Game Information[]
In Tug of War, contestants were told to form teams of ten players prior to the game. Each team was tasked with competing against one other team, chosen at random, in a game of tug of war across two raised stages which were modeled to resemble cranes.      
Each player was shackled to their rope before the beginning of the game; the rope itself was thread through a guillotine suspended between the two platforms. To confirm that the teams were ready, two workers waved yellow and black chequered flags to signal the starter to fire the gun to begin the game. Once the losing team was pulled off their platform, the blade of the guillotine sliced the rope in two, causing the losing players to fall to their deaths from a very high place and causing their elimination from the games.

Trivia[]
This is the first game where it is impossible to let all players win, because one of the teams will still die. Much like the Thanos snap exactly half will be eliminated.
This game happened on June 27, which is the same date that Season 3 was released.
This and Squid Game are the only games that had exactly 50% eliminated and lacked a timer.
This is the only game in the show where a named character is not eliminated.
This game is referenced by Gi-hun in the second season. When Jung-bae says that he thinks they'll be fine for the third game if they stick together, Gi-hun says they might need to kill each other in the next game.
This game takes place at an altitude of approximately 30 meters (100 feet).
This is the only game in which the guards cannot eliminate the players, since there is no time limit and the only way to lose is for the other team to throw them to their death.
If a team wins, all players have killed that team, adding it to their kill count, although it was for the game.
Player 241 was the first player to die in this game as he was the player at the back of the line of Team 7 and hence the one who hit the floor first.
This is the only game of the six in the first season to be split over two episodes and not begin and conclude in the same episode.
This, Tug Of War, is the third game of the 2020 Squid Game. Compared to Mingle, the third game of 2024 Squid Game less then one third of the players entered this game [80] compared to the amount entering Mingle [255] [the actual maths figure is about 31.3 percent].
This game had the potential to have a montage like Pentathlon or RLGL, but for some reason they didn't, making this game the only one in Season 1 where the whole game isn't shown.
Team 1, Jang Deok-su's team, technically had the quickest victory on screen of any Squid Game team ever, overwhelming and pulling off Team 7 to their deaths in approximately forty seconds with very little effort.
With 40/456 survivors, approximately 8.8 percent, or just under 1/11  of the original players passed this game. With 80/456 players entering it that is about 17.5 percent of the original players.
If it weren't for the Special Game the night beforehand, this game would've had 108 Players.
If this were the case, then 54 Players would've survived, and 53 Players would've participated in Marbles, due to Byeong-gi's Elimination.
It is likely in this case that instead of ten the players would be split into twelve teams of nine players each.
It is the only game of Season One without a first or last player to pass the game since it is a team game. Player 456/Seong Gi-hun was the last player to pass all five of the other games while Player 017 [Do Jeong-soo], Player 111 [Byeong-gi], Player 360, Player 218 [Cho Sang-woo] and Player 456 [Seong Gi-hun of Ssang Mung-dong] were the first players to pass the games 1, 2, 4, 5 and 6 respectively.
