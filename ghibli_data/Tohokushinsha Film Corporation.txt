Tohokushinsha Film Corporation (株式会社東北新社, Kabushiki gaisha Tōhokushinsha) is a Japanese film distributor, production company and dubbing studio based in Akasaka, Minato, Tokyo. It was founded in 1961 by Banjirō Uemura (who was also once the head of the Japanese branch of ITC Entertainment) as a dubbing house for foreign films; members of the Uemura family continue as its largest shareholders.

History[]
Founded by Banjirō Uemura in 1961, the studio initially focused on dubbing and subtitling foreign films and television content. Over time, it expanded its scope to encompass distribution, broadcasting, and commercial film production. Additionally, it operates Omnibus Japan, a post-production house.
Specializing in providing Japanese-language services for film, television, and video games, Tohokushinsha plays a vital role in bridging international content with Japanese audiences. Currently managed by Keiichi Kosaka, the studio continues to excel and innovate in the Japanese entertainment industry landscape.
