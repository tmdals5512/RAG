Okkoto (乙事主 Okkoto-Nushi) is a character from Princess Mononoke. He is an old and blind boar god and a leader of the boar clan.

In film[]
After Nago's defeat by Lady Eboshi, Okkoto assumed control of the greater boar clan, presumably joining his warriors with Nago's. Okkoto would lead the clan to wage war upon Irontown and any humans they would come across. However, Okkoto was perfectly aware that such a fight would be one-sided in the humans' favor, but had overlooked it as the boar clan was faced with deterioration. The clan's piglets grew weaker, new boar gods stopped appearing, and the humans had begun to outnumber them and best them with their firearms and explosives.
Okkoto would later encounter the wolf clan and Ashitaka, the latter which informed Okkoto of Nago's fate as a demon. Okkoto believed the human, recognizing the scent of his lamented kin through the exiled prince's cursed flesh and even apologizing to him. Moro cautioned Okkoto against fighting the humans, but he would not hear it, pointing out the deterioration of his own clan and their ultimate fate to become food for the humans, unless they fight in a massive battle to leave their enemies in awe of them. Okkoto would part ways with the wolf clan and Ashitaka to prepare for battle, but not before threatening to kill him, unwilling to offer the same cordiality ever again.
On the day of the battle, Okkoto would lead the boars into direct assault against the men of Irontown, joined at the last minute by the wolves. However, Eboshi and her men were joined by Jigo's own company, hunters and explosives experts tasked with killing the Forest Spirit and collecting it's head for Emperor Mikado. With little thought to their own allies, Jigo's men had utterly annihilated the boars with landmines and grenades, and forcing a wounded Okkoto into a desperate retreat alongside San and Moro. 
The three sought the Forest Spirit to heal them. Unfortunately, Jigo's hunters anticipated this and wore the skins of the boar clan's warriors to trick Okkoto into leading them to the Forest Spirit. Blind, delirious, and becoming a demon due to the bullet wounds afflicting him, Okkoto ignored Sans warnings about the disguised hunters, one of whom then knocks out San and causes her to become stuck to Okkoto's corrupting body. Moro then uses the last of her strength to free San. Shortly after, the Forest Spirit appears, and takes life away from both Okkoto and Moro, ending their suffering.

Trivia[]
According to Ghibli Official, Okkotonushi and Moro were once lovers until 100 years ago.[1]
Kanji letters for Hisaya Morishige's name (森繁久彌) literally means "forest will prosper more and more for forever".
Morishige also voiced the elder of Ashitaka's village. Both of Okkoto and the elder bear several resemblances in the backgrounds as they both hold grudges against Yamato people, and Emishi and Tatari-Gami are refernces to Tsuchigumo.
