Kiki's Delivery Service is a book by Eiko Kadono and the first installment in her series of Kiki novels. It later was adapted by Studio Ghibli into an anime film of the same name. It follows Kiki, a 13-year-old trainee witch who must spend a year away from home to establish herself in a new town—a mandatory tradition for young witches. Settling in the seaside city of Koriko with her cynical talking black cat, Jiji, Kiki opens a delivery service using her only magical talent: flying on a broomstick. The novel is structured as a series of episodic adventures where Kiki helps local citizens, including the kind baker Osono and the aviation-obsessed boy Tombo, while learning about independence and self-worth.
The novel's global popularity was largely cemented by two major film adaptations. The 1989 Studio Ghibli animated film directed by Hayao Miyazaki remains the most famous version of Kiki's story. While faithful to the core premise, Miyazaki introduced more dramatic conflict and a personal crisis where Kiki temporarily loses her magic and Jiji loses his ability to talk, themes not present in the original episodic book. Later, in 2014, a live-action film directed by Takashi Shimizu was released as a more direct adaptation of the first two novels, starring Fuka Koshiba as Kiki and covering her initial journey and subsequent challenges in Koriko.
The novel has seen several English language versions, beginning with the first translation by Lynne E. Riggs in 2003, published by Annick Press. In 2020, a new translation by Emily Balistrieri was released, featuring illustrations by Yuta Onoda in the US through Delacorte Press and Joe Todd-Stanton in the UK through Puffin. A Puffin Clothbound Classic edition was released in 2025 to celebrate the story's 40th anniversary with a patterned hardback cover. While many English readers are only familiar with the first book, Kadono wrote a total of six main novels and three special editions that follow Kiki's life through adulthood, including her marriage and the birth of her own children. The main novel series includes the original Kiki's Delivery Service, followed by Kiki and Her New Magic, Kiki and the Other Witch, Kiki's Love, Perch of Magic, and the final volume, Each and Every Departure. In addition to the main storyline, Kadono released three special editions titled People Who Met Kiki, Kiki and Jiji, and the most recent 2022 release, Keke and the Half Witch.

Plot[]
'Everyone has some type of magic inside them. . .'
On her thirteenth birthday Kiki must decide whether she's going to leave home with her cat Jiji in tow and move to a town without another witch. She knows she has to use her magic to make a living, but Kiki's no good at potions or spells...
Can Kiki learn to control her magic and make her own way in the world?
