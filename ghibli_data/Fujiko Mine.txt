Fujiko Mine is a main character in The Castle of Cagliostro. She is a self-described “lady spy” who often steals the very thing Lupin III is after. 
Lupin practically worships Fujiko, but she only returns the affection if it helps her accomplish her goals. Fujiko states that she and Lupin had been lovers, but also that she ended the relationship.
She treats everyone with respect and usually ends up helping Lupin in some way. She is very tough, having held off squads of goons with a machine gun and grenades.

Appearance[]
In The Castle of Cagliostro, Fujiko Mine’s appearance departs from her more traditional portrayals in the Lupin III franchise, reflecting the distinct artistic direction of Director Hayao Miyazaki. Her design in this film is often described as more tomboyish and utilitarian compared to her usual "femme fatale" aesthetic. 
Throughout her mission, her wardrobe shifts to match her various roles within the castle. She spends a significant portion of the film in a professional housekeeper’s uniform, consisting of a dark dress and a white apron, which allows her to move unnoticed among the Count’s staff. When engaged in more dangerous reconnaissance or combat, she switches to a camouflage military jumpsuit that highlights her skills in paramilitary tactics and infiltration.
In the final stages of the story, Fujiko adapts her look once more to suit her role as a field reporter. Wearing a practical trench coat and functional attire, she presents a professional image to the world while broadcasting the truth behind the Count's operations. This variety in costume underscores her versatility as a master of disguise and her preference for utility over fashion during a high-stakes heist.

Personality[]
Fujiko Mine’s personality is defined by a pragmatic and professional approach to thievery. While she remains self-interested and motivated by profit, she is portrayed with more maturity and competence than in other iterations. She operates with a calculated coolness, prioritizing her objective of finding the counterfeit plates over emotional entanglements. Her independence is a core trait; she is not a sidekick to Lupin but a peer who happens to be working the same job from a different angle.
Despite her mercenary nature, Fujiko demonstrates a level of professional respect and occasional loyalty toward Lupin and his crew. She provides them with vital information and equipment not out of pure altruism, but because their success often creates the distractions she needs to move freely. She is notably less manipulative toward the protagonists in this film, acting more as a reliable, if opportunistic, ally who understands that their goals occasionally align.
Her sharp wit and resourcefulness are evident in how she manages her various undercover roles. Whether she is blending in as a domestic servant or taking charge as a reporter, she maintains a focused and unflappable demeanor. This version of Fujiko is a master of situational awareness, knowing exactly when to stay in the shadows and when to expose the Count’s crimes to serve her own ends. 

Biography[]
Fujiko Mine operates as an independent agent with motives that frequently diverge from Lupin’s. Entering the scene under the guise of a professional housekeeper, she gains access to the inner sanctum of the castle. This position allows her to move freely through the corridors, gathering intelligence on the Count’s operations and the layout of the fortifications. 
Her primary objective throughout the film is the discovery and theft of the Count’s counterfeit printing plates. While Lupin focuses on rescuing Clarisse, Fujiko spends her time scouting the castle’s automated security systems and hidden vaults. Despite her self-interest, she provides critical assistance to Lupin’s team by supplying them with internal intelligence and weaponry at key moments. 
During the film’s climax, Fujiko shifts her role to that of a field reporter. Using a camera to document the counterfeit operation, she broadcasts the evidence to the world, effectively dismantling the Count's criminal empire from the inside. After the dust settles, she successfully makes her escape with the printing plates, maintaining her reputation as a cunning and opportunistic thief.
