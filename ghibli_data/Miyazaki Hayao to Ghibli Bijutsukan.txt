Miyazaki Hayao to Ghibli Bijutsukan is a film featuring Gorō Miyazaki and Isao Takahata touring the Ghibli Museum. A total of 63 minutes long, it was released in 2005.

Info[]
Director Isao Takahata, a mentor to Hayao Miyazaki and the person who understands him best, gives insights into Miyazaki's creative genius by taking taking the viewer on a tour of the Ghibli Museum in Tokyo, designed by Miyazaki himself. The museum is a fantasy world very much like the ones Miyazaki creates in his movies. The viewer is shown the interior, and shown night and aerial views of the museum. In addition, the viewer is shown vistas of Genoa and the medieval mountain cities of Italy that influenced Miyazaki when he visited them. This film is an excellent way to gain a greater appreciation of Miyazaki's works.
